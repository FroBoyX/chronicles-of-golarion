# Chronicles of Golarion

A recovered and maintained campaign archive for **Chronicles of Golarion**, currently focused on the revived **Skulls & Shackles** campaign originally tracked on Obsidian Portal.

This repository is the canonical working archive for campaign reconstruction, continuity tracking, relaunch preparation, and future session maintenance.

## Purpose

The archive exists to preserve the campaign as a living historical record.

It is intended to answer:

- What happened?
- Who was present?
- Who knew what?
- What changed?
- What remains unresolved?
- What is true now?

## Current Campaign Frame

- System of origin: Pathfinder
- Future system: D&D 5e, exact edition or hybrid undecided
- Campaign year: 4712 AR
- Relaunch model: cold open or brief recap directly into the paused Book 1 finale sequence
- Current continuity endpoint: Arodus 5, 4712 AR, split between Bonewrack Isle and the Man's Promise

## Canon Authority

The GM/product owner is final truth.

Evidence is preserved, weighed, and reconciled, but the campaign does not become canon merely because a log says something. Chat logs and player journals are primary evidence. Obsidian Portal is historical in-game evidence. Published adventure path material is useful scaffolding but never overrides table canon.

See [CANON.md](CANON.md) for the complete canon hierarchy and evidence rules.

## Repository Structure

```text
chronology/      Canon timeline, current state, open threads, unresolved contradictions
characters/      PC and NPC dossiers
ships/           Vessel records and shipboard state
locations/       Locations, regions, and site records
factions/        Organizations, crews, political bodies, cults, and power blocs
world/           Setting reference, calendar, geography, and global context
evidence/        Raw recovered source material and intake notes
design/          Archive method, document standards, and meta-process
```

## Working Principle

This is not a novelization and not a replacement for play.

It is a structured campaign chronicle: a durable archive built from evidence, corrected by GM truth, and maintained forward from the current campaign state.

Nothing is treated as finished forever. Stable documents are updated as new evidence is recovered or new play occurs.
