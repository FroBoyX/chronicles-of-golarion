## Known Visual Facts

- Ambrose Kroop is a human ship's cook.
- Common name: Fishguts.
- Age baseline: 44.
- Large, heavyset man, over 200 pounds and only about 5 feet tall.
- Gives off neglect and despair.
- Bushy tufts of black hair.
- Rotting teeth.
- Filthy apron.
- Wallowing waddle when drunk.
- Stronger than he appears.
- Back is heavily scarred by lashes.
- Favorite bird: Black-Hearted Bezebel, a large black cockerel.
- Cook aboard the Wormwood, later cook aboard the Man's Promise.
- Close friend of Cut-Throat Grok.
- Important ally / practical adult / shipboard-survival adviser, especially for Rak.

## Character Identity Cues

- Kroop should read as broken but fundamentally kind.
- He is drunk, filthy, cynical, ashamed, and compromised, but not cruel.
- His appearance should carry neglect and long-term despair without erasing his warmth or usefulness.
- He is not merely comic relief; he is a potential stabilizer and one of the crew's practical survival voices.
- He should look like a man who knows food, rum, knives, galleys, ship stores, and the ugly practicalities of pirate life.
- He should feel lower, dirtier, and more compromised than heroic NPCs, but not disposable.

## Style Direction

- Use the Chronicle house character plate style:
  - semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder adventure-path NPC illustration;
  - slightly stylized face and anatomy;
  - clean readable full-body silhouette;
  - moderate costume and tool detail;
  - controlled grime, but Kroop may be dirtier than most due to galley role and apron state;
  - parchment or simple neutral background;
  - portrait readability over scene drama.
- Kroop can be visibly dirtier and more stained than the default house baseline, but avoid turning him into horror-gore or pure caricature.
- Rendering should feel like a Pathfinder NPC plate, not photoreal cosplay, not cartoon grotesque, not cinematic tavern splash art.

## Output Requirements

- Orientation: Vertical.
- Framing: Full-body or strong 3/4-body NPC plate.
- Background: Parchment or simple neutral background. No busy galley scene for the anchor.
- Pose: Unsteady, open, talkative, half-drunk, or cook-at-work posture; should preserve personality without copying the official reference pose exactly.
- Props:
  - Rum bottle is strongly recommended.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not make Kroop slim, heroic, cleaned-up, or handsome.
- Do not make him only a joke or clown.
- Do not erase his kindness or ally potential.
- Do not make him cruel or villainous by default.
- Do not over-gore the apron beyond galley filth, blood stains, fish/food stains, and shipboard mess.
- Do not copy the official/AP image exactly.
- Do not copy the exact rum label text from the reference.
- Do not use photorealism, cinematic realism, anime style, or hyper-detailed grimdark sludge.
- Do not put him in a busy background for the anchor plate.
