## Character Identity Cues

- Pirate survivor first, not noble adventurer.
- Alchemist / cook's mate / practical tool-user rather than generic reptilian bruiser.
- Capable and dangerous, with humor and shipboard practicality beneath the menace.
- Visual identity should combine nagaji ancestry, pirate survival, and alchemical competence.

## Style Direction

Use the Chronicle house character plate style established by Syl and Fipps:

- semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder / Skull & Shackles character illustration;
- full-body or near full-body plate readability;
- clean silhouette on parchment or neutral background;
- practical pirate material culture;
- weathered but not sludge-covered surfaces;
- enough stylization that ancestry cues feel illustrated rather than photoreal.

Rak expands the house style into a non-human PC anchor. Future non-human portraits should use him as precedent for preserving species cues without sliding into photoreal monster design, dragonborn design, anime styling, or cartoon lizard-man treatment.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not push future Rak images toward dragonborn, crocodile monster, or anime lizard-man.
- Do not remove alchemist gear unless the variant specifically changes equipment state.
- Do not enlarge the tail back to the earlier oversized version.
- Preserve the brown scale color family and integrated natural scale pattern.
- Preserve the practical pirate-alchemist silhouette rather than making him ceremonial, noble, or over-armored.
- Spear can remain a signature visual element if future canon does not contradict it, but alchemy and pirate survival are at least as important to his identity.
- Do not copy the uploaded animated reptilian reference's exact style, costume, proportions, or cartoon treatment.

## Revision Policy

- No remake without GM request.
- Variants should preserve the nagaji pirate-alchemist identity lock.
- Acceptable variants: cropped portrait, shipboard working state, galley / alchemist state, Bonewrack survival state, or action pose using the same identity anchor.
- Remake only if GM explicitly asks or if later canon proves the approved image violates a core fact.
