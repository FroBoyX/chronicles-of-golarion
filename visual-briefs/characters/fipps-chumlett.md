## Known Visual Facts

- Fipps Chumlett is a NE male human pirate.
- Baseline role: Wormwood swab.
- He is fat, pushy, and bullying.
- Shaved head.
- Uses his size as an intimidation tactic.
- Large due to fat, not muscle, skill, or strength.
- Lumbers rather than moves with real combat grace.
- He tries to hold himself in a way that can look threatening if unchallenged.
- He is incredibly nervous under the mask.
- He is a true follower of stronger personalities, especially Plugg / Scourge / Syl pressure structures.
- Current state: presumed alive at Coconut Beach, worn down and possibly injured or bandaged after frog encounter.

## Character Identity Cues

- Fipps should read as a cowardly bully, not a mighty bruiser.
- His intimidation pose is performative and insecure.
- His body language should include tension, overcompensation, and readiness to bluster or backpedal.
- He should be visually distinct from heroic large-bodied pirates: soft weight, poor conditioning, sweat, bluster, and anxious eyes.
- If a weapon is shown, it should feel like a prop he brandishes to seem dangerous rather than a warrior's natural extension.

## Style Direction

- Use the locked Chronicle character baseline from `slippery-syl-portrait-v1`:
  - semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder adventure-path NPC plate;
  - slightly stylized face and anatomy;
  - clean readable silhouette;
  - moderate costume detail;
  - controlled grime and weathered shipboard materials;
  - earthy pirate palette;
  - not photoreal, cinematic, or hyper-detailed.
- Fipps should remain grounded in Skull & Shackles pirate material culture: worn shirt, sash, belt, boots, scuffed leather, rough cloth, maybe a dagger or belaying pin.
- His costume should be simpler and less competent-looking than Syl's.

## Output Requirements

- Orientation: Vertical.
- Framing: Full-body or 3/4-body NPC plate.
- Background: Parchment or simple neutral background, matching the house NPC-plate baseline.
- Pose: Standing with forced swagger or puffed-up intimidation, but with nervous undertone.
- Props: Optional dagger, cudgel, belaying pin, or work gear; avoid making him look like a trained elite fighter.
- Variants needed:
  - `fipps-coconut-beach-v1` for worn-down current-state beach scene.
  - `fipps-nightshift-boatswain-v1` if his delegated authority aboard the Man's Promise is illustrated.
  - `fipps-token-v1` if a VTT token crop is needed.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not make Fipps muscular, heroic, or genuinely imposing.
- Do not make him clean, stylish, or charismatic.
- Do not turn him into a comic-relief clown; he is still a cruel bully.
- Do not overdo grotesque caricature to the point of losing Pathfinder NPC believability.
- Do not give him Syl's elegance or knife-fighter competence.
- Do not use photoreal skin, cinematic lighting, or hyper-detailed concept-art finish.
- Do not create a busy ship or beach scene for the anchor portrait.

Create Fipps Chumlett as a full-body Pathfinder adventure-path NPC character plate on a plain parchment or simple light neutral background.

Style: semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder / Skull & Shackles NPC illustration, using the same house character-plate style as the approved Slippery Syl anchor: slightly stylized face and anatomy, clean readable silhouette, moderate costume detail, controlled grime, earthy pirate palette, weathered shipboard materials, not photoreal, not cinematic, not hyper-detailed.

Character: NE male human pirate swab, fat and pushy, shaved head, a lower-crew bully aligned with harsher officers. He is large because of fat and poor conditioning, not muscle or skill. Pose: forced swagger, shoulders pushed wide, chin raised too hard, trying to look dangerous. He may clutch or brandish a dagger, belaying pin, or rough work tool like a bully's prop rather than a warrior's weapon.

Costume: practical worn Skull & Shackles lower-crew clothing: stained shirt or sleeveless work layer, rough trousers, sash, belt, scuffed boots, sweat-darkened cloth, simple knife sheath or work gear. Weathered but readable. Less polished and less competent-looking than an elite pirate.

Important locks: fat pushy bully, shaved head, coward under the mask, lower-crew pirate, forced intimidation, clean readable NPC plate silhouette, moderate detail, no heroic physique.

Avoid: muscular bruiser, competent warrior, charming rogue, comic clown, noble clothing, photorealism, cinematic realism, excessive grime sludge, over-detailed costume clutter, busy ship background.
