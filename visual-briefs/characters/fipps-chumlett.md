## Locked Visual Facts

- NE male human pirate.
- Fat, pushy bully.
- Shaved head.
- Large from fat / poor conditioning, not muscle or trained strength.
- Lower-crew Skull & Shackles swab.
- Forced intimidating stance.
- Nervous coward under the mask.
- Worn shirt / sleeveless vest / sash / rough trousers / scuffed boots.
- Simple pirate work-gear feel.
- Not an elite duelist, assassin, or competent warrior.
- Parchment / simple neutral NPC-plate presentation.
- Painterly Pathfinder adventure-path illustration style.

## Character Identity Cues

- Fipps should read as a cowardly bully, not a mighty bruiser.
- His intimidation pose is performative and insecure.
- His body language should include tension, overcompensation, and readiness to bluster or backpedal.
- He should be visually distinct from heroic large-bodied pirates: soft weight, poor conditioning, sweat, bluster, and anxious eyes.
- If a weapon is shown, it should feel like a prop he brandishes to seem dangerous rather than a warrior's natural extension.
- His cruelty should remain present; do not make him harmless comic relief.

## Locked Style Notes

- Strong match to `slippery-syl-portrait-v1` as house Pathfinder NPC plate baseline.
- Parchment / light neutral character plate background.
- Semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder / Skull & Shackles rendering.
- Clean full-body silhouette.
- Moderate costume detail.
- Readable lower-crew pirate clothing.
- Costume is simpler and less elite than Syl's, preserving social-rank difference.
- Face and posture carry cruelty, insecurity, and overcompensation.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not make Fipps muscular, heroic, or genuinely imposing.
- Do not make him clean, stylish, or charismatic.
- Do not turn him into a comic-relief clown; he is still a cruel bully.
- Do not overdo grotesque caricature to the point of losing Pathfinder NPC believability.
- Do not give him Syl's elegance or knife-fighter competence.
- Do not use photoreal skin, cinematic lighting, or hyper-detailed concept-art finish.
- Do not create a busy ship or beach scene for the anchor portrait.
- Do not infer Coconut Beach injury or bandage state from this isolated anchor unless a later current-state variant requests it.
