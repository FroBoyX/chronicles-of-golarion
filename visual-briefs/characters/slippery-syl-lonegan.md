## Locked Visual Facts

- Tall, slender CE female human pirate knife-fighter.
- Sinisterly beautiful, cold, controlled, and predatory.
- Long dark hair.
- Practical Skull & Shackles pirate clothing.
- Two knives visible.
- One knife held close to her body as a compulsive touch-point.
- Dark pirate / reptilian tattoos across both arms and upper chest.
- Parchment or simple neutral NPC plate background.

## Character Identity Cues

- Syl should read as socially unsettling, not merely attractive.
- Her calm is predatory: she looks composed because she has already decided where the knife will go.
- Her relationship with knives is intimate and compulsive rather than decorative.
- She is a shipboard enforcer and knife-fighter, not a noble duelist, generic assassin, or chaotic brawler.
- She has enough tactical self-control to threaten, observe, and command under pressure.

## Locked Style Baseline

- Semi-realistic painterly Pathfinder adventure-path NPC plate.
- Slightly stylized face and anatomy.
- Clean readable silhouette.
- Moderate costume detail.
- Controlled grime and weathered shipboard materials.
- Earthy pirate palette.
- Not photoreal.
- Not cinematic.
- Not hyper-detailed.
- Portrait / full-body readability over scene drama.

## Output Requirements

- Orientation: Vertical.
- Framing: Full-body or strong 3/4-body.
- Background: Parchment or simple neutral NPC-plate background.
- Variants needed:
  - `slippery-syl-bonewrack-fork-v1` if a current-state action/standoff version is needed.
  - `slippery-syl-mirei-threat-v1` if the Erastus 30 knife-threat scene is illustrated.
  - `slippery-syl-token-v1` if a VTT token crop is needed.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not remake casually.
- Do not erase the tattoos unless the GM explicitly supersedes the lock.
- Do not over-glamorize or pin-up the pose.
- Do not make her wild, manic, or comic-chaotic.
- Do not turn her into a generic assassin detached from shipboard pirate culture.
- Do not push the rendering toward photoreal skin, cinematic realism, or hyper-detailed concept art.
- Do not replace the simple portrait-plate background with a busy scene for anchor work.
