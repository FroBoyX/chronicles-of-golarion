## Known Tactical / Spatial Facts

- The Man's Promise is a three-masted sailing ship.
- Approximate ship size: 105 feet long by 30 feet wide.
- Suggested tactical scale: 5-foot grid, roughly 21 squares long by 6 squares wide for the hull footprint before exact AP/map correction.
- The boarding party secured the aft deck and ship's wheel during fog-bound fighting.
- From the aft deck, the PCs worked down toward the middle deck.
- The central fight concentrated between two lifeboats and near the main mast.
- The starboard lifeboat is tied to Rak's premature lifeboat release.
- A Man's Promise crew member ended up in the water from the starboard lifeboat incident; possible shark involvement remains unresolved.
- Giffer Tibbs died below the stairs on the port side.
- The captain died in the captain's cabin; blood later remained splashed across floor, desk, and walls.
- The recovered map confirms 16 map-visible / represented deaths: 15 Man's Promise-side figures plus Giffer Tibbs. This does not cap the full shipwide death toll.

## Required Map Facts

- Top-down tactical readability before painterly beauty.
- Full-deck or upper-deck view of a three-masted sailing ship, approximately 105 ft x 30 ft unless corrected.
- Aft deck / sterncastle with ship's wheel must be tactically clear.
- Port-side stairs must be visible and suitable for marking Giffer's death below them in a GM-only version.
- Middle deck must include main mast and two lifeboats.
- Starboard lifeboat must be identifiable for the premature release incident.
- Captain's cabin access / stern authority area should be visible or clearly indicated if the map covers the relevant deck level.
- Fog-bound boarding atmosphere may be included visually, but not so heavily that grid and tokens would be unreadable.
- GM-only variant may include numbered annotations for deaths, surrendered figures, and open-check markers.
- Player-safe variant should remove named death / spoiler annotations.

## Map Identity Cues

- Captured merchant / prize-ship identity, not Wormwood identity.
- Damage, looting, and violence from the boarding should be visible but readable.
- The ship should feel like a contested deck, not a settled home port vessel.
- The tactical geometry must preserve movement lanes, stairs, lifeboats, mast positions, and command-space relationships.

## Style Direction

- Top-down VTT battle-map clarity.
- Grimy swashbuckling fantasy: weathered planks, rope coils, cargo remnants, fog, blood marks, splintered rail, lifeboat rigging, and salt-stained deck.
- Use contrast and clean silhouettes for tactical features.
- Avoid painterly clutter that hides movement, cover, elevation, or hazards.

## Output Requirements

- Orientation: Top-down map.
- Framing: Full upper deck if possible; otherwise upper deck plus sterncastle, centered on aft-to-midship tactical flow.
- Grid/scale: 5-foot square grid variant required; no-grid variant optional after approval.
- Player-safe / GM-only:
  - GM-only grid map with annotations for known death positions and open checks.
  - Player-safe grid map without death labels or unresolved token annotations.
- Variants needed:
  - Truth-layout sketch / readable tactical scaffold.
  - Beautified VTT map after GM approval.
  - No-grid export after grid version accepted.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not change the aft-deck-to-middle-deck tactical flow.
- Do not omit the wheel, central mast, two lifeboats, port-side stairs, or starboard lifeboat.
- Do not make the map so foggy or painterly that tactics are hidden.
- Do not mark the full shipwide death toll as 16; 16 is only the map-visible / represented count.
- Do not assign named surrendered or injured tokens beyond confirmed evidence.
- Do not copy an official Paizo map directly.
