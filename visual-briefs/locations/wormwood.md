## Known Visual Facts

- The Wormwood is a three-masted pirate ship commanded by Captain Barnabas Harrigan.
- It is a moving social machine of captivity, hierarchy, punishment, labor, reputation, and survival.
- Key spaces include main deck, poop deck, officers' quarters, captain's cabin, quartermaster's store, galley, crew berths, and bilges.
- Public work, punishment, and authority occur on the main deck.
- Harrigan and Plugg display command from elevated command spaces such as the poop deck.
- The ship culture includes public punishment, hard labor, rum as control, theft risk, reputation politics, and dangerous officers.

## Required Visual Facts

- Three-masted pirate ship.
- Dark weathered wood, stained sails, rigging, hard-used decks.
- Oppressive, functional, and cruel rather than romantic or clean.
- Enough open deck space visible to imply public punishment and crew assembly.
- Harrigan-scale threat: the ship should feel larger than the PCs and socially dangerous.
- Tropical Shackles atmosphere: salt, hard sun or storm-grey air, humidity, rope, tar, rust, and sea grime.

## Location Identity Cues

- The Wormwood is Act I captivity: it should feel like a workplace, prison, and war machine at once.
- It is not a ghost ship, royal galleon, or generic fantasy vessel.
- Visual hierarchy matters: raised command space, crowded labor areas, rigging overhead, and visible punishment/discipline zone.
- It should imply that officers see everything and sailors are always under pressure.

## Style Direction

- Grimy swashbuckling fantasy.
- Weathered nautical realism with Pathfinder/Golarion pirate-fantasy exaggeration where appropriate.
- Palette: sun-bleached wood, dark wet browns, tar black, old rope tan, rusted iron, salt-white highlights, storm blue-grey, and tropical glare.
- Mood: oppressive, disciplined, dangerous, humid, and alive with crew tension.

## Output Requirements

- Orientation: Wide establishing shot.
- Framing: Exterior three-quarter view from slightly low waterline or nearby boat/deckline, showing the ship's scale and rigging.
- Background: Tropical open sea, humid sky, hard sun or storm-dim cloud.
- Player-safe scope: Yes; this establishes the ship without future spoilers.
- Variants needed:
  - Later: upper-deck punishment / Bloody Hour scene backdrop.
  - Later: cramped hold interior.
  - Later: galley / Kroop scene backdrop.
  - Later: night watch deck variant.

## Avoid / Do Not Drift

- Do not make the Wormwood pristine, noble, or ceremonial.
- Do not make it a generic European castle-ship or Halloween pirate caricature.
- Do not over-fantasize the hull into an impossible warship unless GM requests.
- Do not use direct Paizo image reproduction.
- Do not visually imply it is currently the PCs' ship.
- Do not flatten the ship into only "where the PCs woke up"; show the broader coercive order.
