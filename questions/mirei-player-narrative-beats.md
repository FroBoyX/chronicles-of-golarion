## Use In Player Discovery Chat

This file is not meant to be sent as one long questionnaire.

Use it as a **question bank** and **active discovery guide**. The player-facing chat should:

1. Ask small titled batches of 3-6 questions.
2. Build each new batch from the active narrative beats already confirmed.
3. Avoid repeating questions that the player has already answered.
4. Summarize each answer batch into player-confirmed beats.
5. Update or propose updates to `questions/mirei-active-narrative-beats.md`.
6. Append or propose player-memory updates to `evidence/player-memory/mirei-player-discovery-notes.md`.
7. Flag GM-check items rather than deciding canon when an answer affects chronology, hidden state, or other PCs.

If the player-facing chat cannot write to the repo, it should output a clean **GM Archive Handoff** block after each batch.

## Current Seeded Player-Confirmed Beats

These should be treated as already established unless the player revises them:

- Mirei grew up on the river performing with her family and naturally drew larger crowds.
- Mirei left because she could no longer ignore the call of the sea.
- Mirei's first emotional response to the open ocean was wonder.
- When Sho noticed Mirei becoming closed off, Mirei mostly felt seen.
- Mirei feared Sho would try to act before Mirei was ready, which shaped why Mirei avoided or delayed opening up.

Use these as anchors. Do not restart discovery as if Mirei's background is blank.

## Sendable Framing

We have Mirei's recovered sheet, old logs, player-safe archive notes, and some player-confirmed discovery answers. This is not a full rebuild request. The goal is to preserve Mirei's truth rather than only her mechanics or event sequence.

Short answers are fine. Memory fragments are useful. It is also fine for the player to say a premise feels wrong.

## Player-Safe Boundaries

The Mirei discovery chat is used directly by the player. Keep it player-safe.

Safe to use:

- Mirei's own backstory and sheet identity.
- Mirei's experiences aboard the Wormwood and Man's Promise.
- The player-safe version of her current state: returning from Riptide Cove with Harvus, Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle after confessing Plugg's betrayal assignment.
- Player-confirmed emotional truth.

Do not reveal unless the GM explicitly provides it:

- Sho's current hot-box / Kahina details beyond what Mirei knows.
- Rak's current island-side situation beyond what Mirei knows.
- Hidden actions by other PCs.
- Exact unresolved NPC loyalties or survivor politics.
- GM-only plans for Plugg, Scourge, or mutiny outcomes.

## Sensitive Material Rule

Plugg's pressure should be explored carefully and in the player's language.

Good framing:

- coercion;
- isolation;
- survival;
- perceived privilege;
- fear;
- shame;
- contamination;
- performance becoming control;
- whether Mirei still felt agency.

Avoid:

- making the material more explicit than the player does;
- turning the dynamic into simple romance, seduction, or villainy;
- forcing trauma interpretation if the player frames it differently;
- using Plugg's perspective as truth.

## Syl's Knife Threat

Before the storm interrupted everything, Syl stopped Mirei on the stairs after Mirei had been sent below as "Mistress Mirei." Syl had been watching how Mirei handled the crew. She pressed a knife into Mirei's back, mocked the Mistress role, threatened Mirei's voice, focused on Mirei's whispers with Sho, and dug the knife in hard enough to draw blood. Mirei pushed back, and Syl read that as a spark of fire behind the farce.

47. In Mirei's memory, did Syl's threat feel like a real near-death moment, a test, intimidation, jealousy, or just another danger swallowed by the storm?
48. Did the knife wound matter afterward physically, emotionally, both, or not much because the table glossed over it?
49. Did Mirei hide the wound, ignore it, treat it, or forget it once the storm crisis started?
50. Did this exchange change how Mirei saw Syl: rival, predator, observer, possible informant, or someone who understood the farce too well?
51. Did Syl seeing through the "Mistress" role make Mirei feel exposed, understood, threatened, or angry?
