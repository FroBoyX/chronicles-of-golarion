# Mirei Player Narrative Beat Questions

## Document Status

- Type: Player-facing short question list
- Maturity: Draft, updated with Syl knife-threat wound follow-up
- Recipient: Mirei's player
- Purpose: High-value relationship, motive, and memory beats only

## Sendable Version

We have Mirei's recovered sheet, the old logs, and the current archive, so this is not a full rebuild request. This is meant to jog the important relationship and motive beats so we preserve Mirei's truth rather than only her mechanics.

Short answers are fine. Memory fragments are useful.

## Mirei's Core

1. What did Mirei mean when she felt drawn to Port Peril: destiny, Desna, the sea, curiosity, escape, or something else?
2. Did Mirei understand her own otherworldly presence, or did she mostly experience it through how others reacted to her?
3. What did her harp mean to her: art, identity, magic, comfort, status, calling, or something else?
4. How sincere was Mirei's devotion to Desna, and how did it shape her choices?
5. Did Mirei think of herself as brave, lucky, protected, naive, chosen, or something else at the start?

## Press-Ganging and Harp Loss

6. What does Mirei remember about the night by the moonlit bay?
7. Did Mirei know or suspect Slippery Syl was following her before the sap hit?
8. When Mirei woke and the harp was gone, what was the emotional impact?
9. Did losing the harp make Mirei feel stripped, silenced, endangered, angry, or something else?
10. Did Mirei remember Syl as the one who took her, or was that only learned later?

## Wormwood Performance

11. What were Mirei's early performances aboard the Wormwood like: survival tactic, joy, prayer, comfort, manipulation, or instinct?
12. Did Mirei perform because she wanted to, because the ship expected it, or because it gave her leverage?

14. If that memory is right, was it literal supernatural attention, poetic narration, bardic magic, Desnan omen, or table exaggeration?
15. Do you remember which general period that performance happened in: early Wormwood, after harp recovery, after a punishment, after Rosie, or after Man's Promise?
16. What songs, moods, or imagery do you associate with Mirei's shipboard performances?

20. What did Mirei think of Grok after the harp recovery?

## Syl's Knife Threat

Before the storm interrupted everything, Syl stopped Mirei on the stairs after Mirei had been sent below as "Mistress Mirei." Syl had been watching how Mirei handled the crew. She pressed a knife into Mirei's back, mocked the Mistress role, threatened Mirei's voice, focused on Mirei's whispers with Sho, and dug the knife in hard enough to draw blood. Mirei pushed back, and Syl read that as a spark of fire behind the farce.

45. In Mirei's memory, did Syl's threat feel like a real near-death moment, a test, intimidation, jealousy, or just another danger swallowed by the storm?
46. Did the knife wound matter afterward physically, emotionally, both, or not much because the table glossed over it?
47. Did Mirei hide the wound, ignore it, treat it, or forget it once the storm crisis started?
48. Did this exchange change how Mirei saw Syl: rival, predator, observer, possible informant, or someone who understood the farce too well?
