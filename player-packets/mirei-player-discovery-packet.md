## Safe Current Summary

Mirei is an aasimar Sea Singer bard from the River Kingdoms, devoted to Desna. She has silver hair, emerald eyes, and a graceful or otherworldly presence.

She came to Port Peril seeking destiny and a deeper understanding of herself. The sea called to her in a way that felt larger than the rivers she had known.

At the current relaunch point, Mirei is alive on Bonewrack Isle. She is returning from the grindylow caverns with Harvus G'ron, Sandara Quinn, Tilly Brackett, and Goyle Onórach. She has confessed that Plugg tasked her with betraying the other PCs, but she did not act on that order.

## Before the Wormwood

Mirei grew up in the River Kingdoms and seems to have had a life shaped by music, movement, beauty, and performance.

Current player-led framing:

- She grew up on the river, performing with her family and gathering larger crowds.
- She left because she could no longer ignore the call of the sea.
- The feeling associated with that call was wonder.
- The River Kingdoms gave her movement; the sea looked like the next, larger freedom.

This framing fits both **Touched by the Sea** and **Lillend's Harp**.

Discovery questions should help recover whether Mirei saw destiny as Desna's call, the sea's call, personal curiosity, or something she did not yet understand.

## Press-Ganging in Port Peril

Opening date for reconstruction: **Erastus 4, 4712 AR**.

Mirei had not been in Port Peril long, but felt drawn to it. She stopped at the Formidably Maid near the docks and later walked toward the bay under a thin crescent moon.

She was caught up in the ever-present song of the sea and did not notice danger.

Slippery Syl Lonegan followed her from the shadows, struck her with a sap, searched her for valuables before the official taking of possessions, and dragged her into the Wormwood's hold.

When Mirei woke aboard the Wormwood, her harp was gone.

For Mirei, this loss was not only property. It was identity.

## First Night: Sandara Returns the Harp

On the first night or very early aboard the Wormwood, Mirei saw her harp in Sandara Quinn's possession.

Mirei asked for it back simply:

> "Can i has that back?"

Sandara produced the harp and asked whether it was hers. Mirei confirmed it:

> "Yes it is"

Sandara returned it:

> "You'll probably want that."

Sandara explained that people aboard ship should keep friends close, avoid Plugg and Scourge's attention, ask if they need anything, and avoid starting what they cannot finish.

Sandara recovered the harp through Cut-Throat Grok's superstition. She told Grok that the harp was cursed by the sea, that only Sandara could cleanse it, and that the owner would need to play it regularly so the ship would not be sunk.

This gives Mirei three early relationship hooks:

- Sandara as helper / warning-giver / person who returned the harp.
- Grok as superstitious quartermaster whose fear of sea-curses could be used.
- The harp as both reclaimed identity and a new shipboard obligation.

## Wormwood Performance

Mirei performed aboard the Wormwood and drew crew attention through music.

Giffer Tibbs was notably fond of Mirei's supper performances and praised her playing after warming to her.

Memory to test: there is a strong table memory that one nightly harp performance was described as powerful enough to draw the attention of extraplanar beings. This is not yet directly supported by recovered indexed evidence. Treat it as a strong memory to confirm or refine, not settled canon.

Discovery should ask what Mirei's performances were for:

- survival,
- joy,
- prayer,
- comfort,
- crew influence,
- a way to keep the sea-cursed harp story alive,
- or something more instinctive.

## Mirei and Sho

Sho Astor remembers that Mirei noticed Sho's disguise early, probably on the first or second day, and that Sho was able to confide in Mirei about it.

This is player-memory evidence, now corrected by the GM to identify Anji's character as Mirei.

Possible questions:

- Did Sho asking or allowing Mirei to keep that secret create trust?
- Did Mirei treat it as practical information, emotional vulnerability, or both?
- Did Mirei see Sho as disciplined, moral, severe, fragile, brave, or something else?

## Day 2 Hostile-Crew Message

On Day 2, Slippery Syl led a hostile group meant to send a message to the newer crew.

The group included:

- Slippery Syl Lonegan
- Jaundiced Jape
- Aretta Bansion
- Fipps Chumlett

An altercation followed. Aretta took blows from Harvus, was distracted by Mirei, and was ultimately knocked unconscious by Sho.

At Day 2 Bloody Hour, Syl, Jape, Aretta, and Fipps were whipped for showing up late to duty. This punishment fueled Aretta's later revenge.

Mirei-specific discovery angle:

- What did it mean for Mirei to distract Aretta?
- Was Mirei acting instinctively, protecting Sho/Harvus, using performance/social misdirection, or simply surviving?
- How did Mirei feel seeing the hostile group punished afterward?

## Plugg's Attention and the Man's Promise

After the capture of the Man's Promise, Mirei was aboard the prize crew.

Plugg singled her out and pressured her into a privileged but dangerous role. He treated her as useful leverage and assigned her to spy on or betray the other PCs.

This role should be explored carefully. Do not assume simple romance, seduction, or villainy. The important questions are coercion, isolation, survival, perceived privilege, and what Mirei believed her options were.

Safe known outcome:

- Mirei did not act on the order to betray or kill the other PCs.
- She later confessed.

Open discovery questions:

- Did Mirei ever seriously consider following through?
- Did she feel trapped, chosen, owned, frightened, protected, or ashamed?
- Did she think Plugg could protect her from Scourge or the rest of the ship?
- Did she try to signal distress before confessing?
- Did Sho noticing Mirei becoming closed off matter to her?

## Bonewrack Isle

Mirei helped the group survive island hazards, including bog-fly or insect pressure.

During the grindylow cave sequence, Mirei accompanied Harvus back into the caves rather than returning with Rak and Sho.

Mirei helped recover Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle from the grindylow caverns.

After the rescue, she confessed that Plugg had tasked her with killing or betraying the others, but she had not done it.

Mirei-specific discovery angle:

- Why did she go back into Riptide Cove with Harvus?
- Was it courage, guilt, loyalty, refusal to abandon Sandara, or refusal to let Harvus go alone?
- Did rescuing Sandara make the earlier harp return feel repaid, transformed, or irrelevant?
- What did confession cost Mirei?

## Relationships To Explore

### Sandara Quinn

Sandara returned Mirei's harp and warned the new hands how to survive the Wormwood.

Mirei later helped rescue Sandara from the grindylow caverns.

Questions:

- Did Mirei trust Sandara immediately?
- Did getting the harp back create a debt?
- Did rescuing Sandara feel like repayment, loyalty, friendship, or something else?

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus is currently with Mirei after the grindylow rescue.

Harvus knows Mirei had been tasked with betrayal, but his immediate rage was tempered by the fact that she had helped preserve lives and rescue Sandara.

Questions:

- What did Harvus mean to Mirei before Bonewrack?
- Why did she return to the caves with him?
- Was confessing to Harvus harder because of what they had just survived together?

### Sho Astor

Questions:

- Did she avoid Sho because Sho might notice too much?
- Did Sho's discipline comfort Mirei, intimidate her, or both?

### Rak Wraithraiser

Questions:

- Did Mirei fear Rak, trust Rak, find him amusing, or see him as unpredictable?
- Did Plugg use Rak specifically as a threat or test in Mirei's mind?

Questions:

## Initial Question Batches For The Player Chat

### Batch 1 — Before the Sea

1. What was Mirei's family performance life like on the rivers?
2. Did she love the audience, the music, the travel, the attention, or all of it?
3. When she felt called toward the sea, did it feel like Desna, destiny, curiosity, escape, or something else?
4. What did the River Kingdoms give her that she could not keep by staying there?
5. What did she think the sea would give her?

### Batch 2 — The Harp

1. What did the harp mean to Mirei before the Wormwood?
2. When she woke and it was gone, what was the first feeling: fear, grief, anger, silence, vulnerability, or something else?
3. When Sandara returned it, did that create trust, debt, relief, suspicion, or all of those?
4. Did Mirei understand the sea-curse story as funny, frightening, useful, or strangely appropriate?
5. Did playing the harp aboard ship feel like reclaiming herself or making herself visible to danger?

### Batch 3 — Performance Aboard the Wormwood

1. Did Mirei perform because she wanted to, because she had to, or because it gave her leverage?
2. Did she choose songs to comfort people, impress people, manipulate mood, pray, or speak when ordinary speech was unsafe?
3. How did she feel when crew started noticing her performances?
4. What does she remember about Giffer liking her music?
5. Does the memory of a performance drawing extraplanar attention sound true? If so, what did that moment feel like?

### Batch 4 — Sho and Secrets

1. What did Mirei notice about Sho's disguise?
2. Did Sho confiding in Mirei make Mirei feel trusted, burdened, protective, or simply practical?
3. Did Mirei keep the secret easily?
4. Did Mirei see Sho as a friend, moral anchor, dangerous truth-teller, or something else?
5. Did Sho noticing Mirei becoming closed off later make Mirei feel seen or exposed?

### Batch 5 — Plugg's Pressure

1. When did Mirei first notice Plugg's attention changing?
2. Did Plugg make the role feel like privilege, threat, ownership, protection, or all of those?
3. What did Mirei believe would happen if she refused him outright?
4. What did she think he was asking her to do to the others?
5. What stopped her from doing it?

### Batch 6 — Bonewrack and Confession

1. Why did Mirei go back into Riptide Cove with Harvus?
2. What does she remember from the rescue of Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle?
3. Did rescuing Sandara change how Mirei understood Sandara returning the harp?
4. What finally made Mirei confess Plugg's task?
5. After confessing, did Mirei expect anger, rejection, forgiveness, punishment, or something else?
