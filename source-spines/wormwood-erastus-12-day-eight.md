## Weather and Duration Lock

The prior day, Erastus 11 / Wormwood Day 7, is heavy rain with worsening conditions. On Erastus 12 the storm closes fully around the Wormwood.

Use the day as more than twenty hours of grueling emergency labor:

- both watches are called and kept working;
- normal day and night boundaries collapse;
- brief relief periods are too short to dry clothes, eat properly, or sleep;
- the crew remains under continuous pressure after darkness;
- the storm does not break before the narrative closes.

The Wormwood is not merely inconvenienced. The ship is at the mercy of the waves, and survival depends on sustained seamanship from officers, specialists, common crew, rivals, and future allies.

## Rosie Loss Placement

Rosie's loss is locked to this day.

Current GM truth:

- Harvus spots Rosie in the sea.
- Rak begins throwing a line toward her.
- At the same time, Sho ties herself to a second line and goes overboard to swim toward Rosie.
- Rosie catches the thrown line at least once but repeatedly loses her grip as the storm and ship movement pull it away.
- Sho fights toward Rosie while the Wormwood's speed drags against her tether.
- Mirei instinctively uses message magic to reach for Rosie across the storm.
- Mirei tells Rosie to hold on, says they are trying to reach her, and urges her to tie the rope around herself when it reaches her.
- Rosie gives no recovered response.
- Sho does not reach Rosie.
- The Wormwood's movement and necessary course change pull Sho away from Rosie.
- The rescue effort shifts from reaching Rosie to recovering Sho before the ship drags or drowns her.
- Rak, Mirei, and several others fight to haul Sho back aboard.
- Rosie disappears from view and is lost at sea.
- Rosie is not confirmed dead. She leaves the story here. A later return would require rescue by another vessel or equivalent off-screen survival.
- After the failed rescue, the crew must immediately return to fighting the storm.

## Controlled Embellishment

The storm sequence may include brief cooperative or heroic moments needed to make the ship feel fully crewed, provided they remain role-consistent and do not create new long-term relationship outcomes.

Good uses include:

- one rigger catching another when the mast jerks;
- a future enemy bracing a PC against a sliding deck;
- Peppery holding the command layer together through expertise;
- sailors who normally distrust each other hauling the same line;
- officers working physically when command alone is insufficient;
- Habbly treating hands or binding injuries between urgent repairs;
- Rak and Kroop keeping hot food or broth moving when possible.

Keep these moments brief. They show that the Wormwood survives through collective labor, not that the crew has reconciled.
