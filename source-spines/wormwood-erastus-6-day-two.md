## Locked / Strong Facts

- Corrected date: **Erastus 6, 4712 AR**.
- Player-facing title: **Laying Down the Law**.
- Weather is normal by scaffold.
- This is the first real workday before shipboard monotony sets in.
- The Wormwood should be treated as a crowded, named, recurring place, not as a generic ship.
- Use named crew rather than generic unnamed sailors when background presence is needed.
- Scourge encouraged Syl, Jape, Fipps, and Aretta to rough up the new hands and delay them.
- The harassment begins below, before the new hands can properly report to work.
- The harassing group is Slippery Syl Lonegan, Jaundiced Jape, Fipps Chumlett, and Aretta Bansion.
- The hostile unit should read as four distinct pressures: Syl as cold tactical violence, Aretta as reactive spite and wounded pride, Jape as silent pressure, and Fipps as a false-front bully.
- Fipps is large because of fat rather than strength, uses size and posture to intimidate, and becomes visibly nervous when isolated or directly challenged.
- Aretta and Jape pressure Sho first.
- Syl pressures Harvus first and keeps him off rhythm in the cramped lower hold.
- Mirei distracts Aretta at a crucial moment.
- Sho knocks Aretta unconscious.
- Harvus overwhelms Syl after Aretta falls.
- Sho and Harvus turn on Jape together and knock him out.
- Fipps is cornered last, panics, draws a dagger, is disarmed, and is knocked out before he can use it.
- Rak walks through the defeated bodies as if none of it mattered.
- Conchobhar and Crimson are present as background new hands but should not pull focus unless needed.
- Rak, Harvus, and Sho report the delay to Scourge on deck.
- Scourge sends them on and says he will deal with the failed harassers later.
- Day 2 work seeds ship geography and crew roles.
- Harvus works as a rigger around the ship's vertical labor.
- Maheem reads as a distinct hostile co-worker: big, Rahadoumi, disciplined, permanently scowling, and complicit without seeming chaotic.
- Mirei works near Tilly and uses conversation to make work pass more easily.
- Tilly reads as tough, dry, rum-loving, and as someone who chose the sea but not necessarily the Wormwood.
- Sho's swab movement seeds the path to Grok's quartermaster store.
- Rak works in the galley under Kroop, matching his later apology about losing track of time cooking up the grub.
- Shivikah is seeded as a capable watcher who measures people as threats, assets, and costs.
- Bloody Hour punishes Aretta, Fipps, Jape, and Syl for being late.
- The punishment turns the failed harassment into grievance, especially for Aretta.
- Mirei performs that night and receives reactions from Harvus, Rak, and Badger.
- Jack and Giffer are lightly seeded near the performance as lower-crew witnesses.
- Rak later returns to Grok, apologizes for missing her earlier, and begins the brew thread.
- The day ends with both sides learning something: the hostile crew knows the new hands can hit back, and the new hands know that does not make them safe.
