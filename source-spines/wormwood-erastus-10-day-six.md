## Strong Facts / Drafting Locks

- The encounter happens after the evening meal.
- Plugg brings Owlbear Hartshorn onto the main deck as entertainment, intimidation, and a lesson.
- The crew treats Owlbear as a joke, beast, or object.
- Owlbear is hulking, slow, simpleminded, blind in one eye, and bears signs of prior humiliation, including tar and feathers.
- The staged rules are fists or wrestling only, with no magic or weapons.
- Sho initially accepts the challenge because she sees Owlbear as one of Plugg's brutes and wants to put him in his place.
- Sho fights tactically and recognizes the weakness created by Owlbear's blind side.
- Sho gains control of the fight without panicking.
- During the fight, Sho realizes Owlbear is another captive and victim, not the beast Plugg presents him as.
- Sho stops when Owlbear is beaten and no longer a true threat.
- Sho's mercy comes from both pity and defiance.
- Owlbear understands that Sho chose not to continue hurting him.
- Owlbear also sees Plugg's reaction to Sho's refusal.
- The encounter begins the positive bond between Sho and Owlbear.
- No separate night activity is securely recorded after the fight.
