## Weather and Day Function

The AP weather scaffold assigns a weakening storm to structural Day 9. Erastus 12 is now locked as the full-storm day and Rosie's loss at sea.

Erastus 13 should therefore function as storm recovery rather than a new set piece:

- the rain and sea remain dangerous but steadily weaken;
- normal watches begin separating again only after emergency labor has continued into the morning;
- rigging, deck, stores, galley, holds, and minor damage all require inspection and repair;
- crew members are exhausted, bruised, cold, and working on little sleep;
- Rosie's absence becomes visible through ordinary shipboard spaces and routines;
- morale remains low even as the immediate danger recedes.

## Post-Rosie Harvus / Sandara Placement

The recovered Harvus / Sandara scene was previously carried as floating Erastus 14-15 evidence because Rosie's loss date was unresolved.

Current GM chronology locks Rosie's loss to Erastus 12. Harvus directly describes it as `Rosie's death last night.` That makes Erastus 13 the strongest current placement for the conversation.

Use the scene after the day's recovery labor, once the storm has weakened enough for short private conversation but before the crew has emotionally recovered.

Strong facts:

- Harvus seeks Sandara's aid and calls her `my queen`.
- Sandara is visibly shaken but responds warmly and invites him to sit.
- Harvus says anger is growing in his heart.
- He connects the anger to losing his family young, Scourge damaging his wing, Rosie's loss the previous night, and the Wormwood.
- Harvus says Sandara eases his soul.
- Sandara asks about his wing and places a hand on his shoulder.
- Sandara tells him to keep the rage contained because they may need it.
- Sandara says things are worsening aboard the Wormwood.
- Sandara says Rosie angered Scourge and Plugg quickly.
- Sandara says she was busy in the rigging during the storm but suspects Rosie's going over may have been intentional.
- This remains Sandara's suspicion, not confirmed fact.
- Sandara tells Harvus to keep his friends close and says she will pray to Besmara.
- Harvus says Sandara restrains his rage.
- Sandara asks him to call her Sandara rather than queen.

## Strong Facts / Drafting Locks

- The storm weakens rather than stopping abruptly.
- Both watches remain engaged through at least part of the morning before normal watch structure resumes.
- The crew clears standing water, checks lashings, repairs rigging, dries or inventories stores, and restores shipboard routine.
- Peppery remains central to ship handling and damage assessment.
- Plugg and Scourge push recovery work immediately, allowing little time for grief.
- Rak returns to the galley and practical food work after the emergency labor.
- Sho is bruised and exhausted after being dragged behind the ship.
- Sho may feel some limited personal blame but understands Rosie's loss primarily as an accident of the storm.
- Mirei carries the absence of any response to her messages.
- Harvus repeatedly looks toward the sea or wake but finds no sign of Rosie.
- Rosie's fiddle remains aboard.
- Rosie is lost at sea, survival unresolved. Do not confirm her death objectively.
- Crew members may speak or behave as though she died because that is what they reasonably believe.
- The recovered Harvus / Sandara conversation occurs during the evening recovery period.

## Do Not Overstate

- Do not confirm Rosie's death in objective narration.
- Do not confirm that Plugg or Scourge caused Rosie's fall.
- Do not turn Sandara's suspicion into fact.
- Do not invent a formal memorial, funeral, or officer acknowledgment.
- Do not invent major structural damage not established on Day 8.
- Do not imply the crew receives a full day of rest.
- Do not erase exhaustion merely because the storm weakens.
- Do not invent other private conversations or exact dialogue.
- Do not make Sho unusually cautious after the failed rescue.
- Do not make Mirei's magic technical or procedural in retrospective description.
