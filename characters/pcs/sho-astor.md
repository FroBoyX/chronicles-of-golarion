## Overview

Sho Astor is a disciplined, defiant PC whose Book One arc centers on restraint, moral responsibility, and refusal to become the sort of pirate the Wormwood expects.

At the current relaunch point, Sho is separated from the island party and still believed locked in the hot-box aboard the Man's Promise.

Her scene with Kahina Bukekayo creates a parallel climax: while Harvus returns with rescued witnesses and Rak navigates hostile island politics, Sho must answer whether remorse has action behind it.

## Identity

- Name: Sho Astor
- Type: PC
- Ancestry: Suli
- Class / role at original system level: Monk
- Current location: Man's Promise
- Current condition: Alive, imprisoned in the hot-box at current reconstruction
- Immediate scene partner: Kahina Bukekayo

## Backstory

Sho was found as a child in Jalmeray and raised by a lesser monastic order.

Her heritage was recognized, and she was trained heavily. The training left little room for softness, desire, or a sense of belonging.

Sho sought small moments of ordinary pleasure: reading, enjoying the elements, window-shopping, clothes, sweets. These desires were treated as distractions from discipline.

A noblewoman named Agatha Dreamriver became important to Sho after intervening when guards mistreated or humiliated her. Agatha questioned the monastery's view of Sho and encouraged her to seek the world.

Sho eventually left to explore and pursue knowledge, with Magnimar and Agatha remaining future emotional anchors.

## Press-Ganging

Sho followed a map lead in Port Peril.

The deal involved runes older than expected and an old peddler who demanded a drink.

She was drugged and woke aboard the Wormwood without the map or her possessions.

## Wormwood History

### Defiance

Sho repeatedly resisted the moral logic of the Wormwood.

She challenged excess violence, objected to unnecessary killing, and drew attention from Plugg and Scourge.

### Owlbear

Sho fought Owlbear Hartshorn when Plugg brought him out for sport.

She defeated him but refused to kill him.

This established one of Sho's core Book One themes: strength under restraint.

### Aretta

Sho's conflict with Aretta in the bilges ended with Aretta's death after slow or inadequate shipboard care.

This is a moral burden and likely part of why Kahina's later challenge matters.

### Boarding the Man's Promise

Sho participated in the boarding action.

She warned Harrigan when a sailor threatened him from behind, earning Harrigan's recognition and later reward.

She also objected to Rak's killing of downed or pleading opponents, reinforcing her opposition to reckless killing.

## Man's Promise and Bonewrack

Sho returned toward the Man's Promise with Rak after the initial Bonewrack exploration while Harvus and Mirei continued or returned toward Riptide Cove.

At the beach or shore party confrontation, Sho was beaten unconscious by Scourge's forces after defying or refusing the terms imposed on her.

She later woke in the hot-box aboard the Man's Promise.

## Current Hot-Box Scene

Sho is still believed to be locked up.

Kahina Bukekayo, a Man's Promise survivor, has spoken with her.

Kahina challenges whether Sho's remorse for the Man's Promise dead is meaningful.

If Sho claims regret, Kahina wants to know what Sho is willing to do about the people who caused or continue the suffering.

This is not only a prison scene. It is a moral interrogation.

## Relationships

### Kahina Bukekayo

Kahina is currently Sho's most important immediate scene partner.

She may be enemy, judge, temporary ally, or all three.

### Rak Wraithraiser

Sho has fought alongside Rak and owes survival to him in some contexts, but she is deeply concerned by his willingness to kill downed or helpless opponents.

Sho has considered that if Rak continues reckless killing, she may need to stop him.

### Harvus G'ron

Sho sees Harvus as a possible comrade and may hope his rage can be controlled or redirected.

She is not present for his rescue of Sandara and may learn later that he created a legend while she was imprisoned.

### Mirei

Sho and Mirei shared survival under the Wormwood and Man's Promise.

Sho does not yet know the full result of Mirei's confession at the current reconstruction point.

### Captain Harrigan

Sho has an odd respect for Harrigan, and Harrigan recognized her warning during the Man's Promise battle.

This does not make him safe or good, but it complicates her view of authority.

### Peppery Longfarthing

Sho has thought Peppery attractive in a commanding way and received warning or indirect counsel from her.

### Agatha Dreamriver

Agatha remains a backstory emotional anchor and possible future objective.

## Current Knowledge

Sho knows:

- she is a prisoner again,
- Scourge and Plugg remain dangerous,
- Kahina holds Man's Promise casualties against her and the Wormwood side,
- Kahina may be willing to act against Plugg and Scourge if Sho proves sincere.

Sho likely does not know:

- Harvus rescued Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle,
- Harvus killed the Whale,
- Mirei confessed Plugg's betrayal order,
- Rak's current island position,
- Fipps is at the beach.

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Sho anchors the ship-side scene.

She prevents the relaunch from being only a jungle reunion.

Her choices can affect whether Man's Promise survivors become enemies, allies, witnesses, or a separate faction.
