## Summary

The recovered Day 12-16 cache adds important pre-Man's Promise Sho material around Owlbear, Mirei, public defiance, mast punishment aftermath, and Harvus learning Sho's secret.

## New / Sharpened Beats

### Day 12 — Owlbear

- Sho spoke with Owlbear while he was chained.
- Owlbear framed himself as lonely and indicated that Plugg keeps him chained because Plugg worries what happens if he is loose.
- Sho interpreted Owlbear as more sad and lonely than bad.
- Sho told Mirei that she thought Owlbear was not as bad as others believed.
- This supports the existing Owlbear section: Sho's pity and protectiveness toward Owlbear did not only begin in the fight; it continued into quiet conversation afterward.

### Day 12 — Sho / Mirei Confidantes

- Sho invited Mirei to dinner and privately asked how she was doing.
- Mirei said the day had gone well.
- Sho warned that things felt tense aboard ship.
- Mirei answered that making more friends seemed wise.
- This supports a pre-Plugg-pressure friendship / confidante channel between Sho and Mirei.

### Day 13 — Harvus Discovers Sho's Secret

- Harvus bluntly confronted Sho with the fact that he had deduced she was a woman.
- Sho reacted with alarm and asked him to keep it quiet because fewer people knowing was better.
- Harvus said he figured it out during one of her whippings and agreed to keep the secret.
- This adds a second PC discovery point after Mirei: Harvus knew before Man's Promise, and Sho asked him to keep quiet.
- This also reinforces that punishments and physical exposure threatened Sho's disguise.

### Day 14 — Boarding Drill and Defiance Pressure

- Sho participated in Riaris Krine's boarding drill with Mirei, Rak, Harvus, Shivikah, and Tam Tate.
- Sho initially resisted making a game of throwing objects at others, but accepted the harsh logic that training could not baby friends if boarding combat was real.
- Sho eventually performed well enough that Riaris identified her as the only one who did not fail the drill.
- This supports Sho's competence and willingness to adapt to harsh practical combat logic even when she finds it cold.

### Day 14 — Bloody Hour and Mast Punishment

- After the drill, Scourge used both boarding failure and rum-ration refusal to stage a broad punishment.
- Sho struggled not to speak when Scourge invited confession and framed silence as insubordination.
- Sho did break silence when punishment for silence was threatened, protesting the logic of being punished for doing nothing.
- Harvus then drew Scourge's attention away with his own insult, interrupting Sho before she escalated further.
- Sho was grabbed by Fipps Chumlett and taken to the mast.
- Plugg punished Sho directly with the cat, framing the strikes as lessons in command and understanding.
- Sho slumped unconscious.
- Plugg whispered over her unconscious or collapsing body, mocking the absence of the "tough girl" that night.
- This likely resolves the broad shape of the punishment that threatened Sho's disguise and left her later trying to check the others.

### Day 14 Night — Peppery / Syl / Checking Mirei

- After waking, Sho saw others still down around the mast and asked Peppery whether they lived.
- Peppery refused to let Sho openly check them and ordered her below, but did not report all of Sho's repeated defiance.
- Sho quickly checked Mirei's health while Peppery turned away.
- Slippery Syl appeared with a dagger and threatened to report Sho to Plugg.
- Patch Patchsalt arrived, and Sho avoided further escalation.
- This gives a concrete scene for Sho's later claim that she barely checked Mirei before being cut short.

### Day 15 — Sho / Mirei Aftermath

- Sho privately asked Mirei how badly she was hurt.
- Sho said she was worried when she woke and found everyone around the mast.
- Mirei relayed Sandara's advice that they needed to keep their heads down and wait for the right time.
- Sho said she would try to quiet her tongue but still hoped eventual justice would be physical.
- Mirei suggested gathering more friends.
- Sho said friendship-building was not her strength, but Mirei was good at it; Sho could keep quiet and serve as muscle when needed.
- This reinforces Sho's emerging role as restrained force rather than social organizer.
