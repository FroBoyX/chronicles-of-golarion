## Overview

Rak Wraithraiser is a nagaji PC with pirate legacy, alchemical competence, practical shipboard usefulness, humor, and a dangerous streak of violence.

He begins as a press-ganged sailor and cook's mate, becomes tied to Kroop and Grok, earns Harrigan's attention by catching reefclaw for dinner, saves allies during the Riptide Cove failure, and at the current relaunch point is guiding a hostile or semi-hostile supply party across Bonewrack Isle.

Rak is not simply comic relief. His choices repeatedly affect survival, loyalty, and moral tension.

## Character Premise

Rak is competent, funny, useful, and dangerous.

He can build social warmth with the right people, especially Kroop and Grok, but he also crosses moral lines that other PCs cannot ignore.

His Book One arc should keep both truths visible: Rak helps people survive, and Rak can decide that violence or removal solves a problem.

## Wormwood History

### Cook's Mate / Kroop / Grok

Rak served as cook's mate under Ambrose Kroop.

His relationship with Kroop gave him practical access to food, work rhythms, and private galley conversations.

Rak also developed a meaningful bond with Cut-Throat Grok. The exact speed and nature of the friendship / flirtation / intimacy remains open, but Grok's later goodbye is important.

### Erastus 16 — Reef Task / Reefclaw Reward

Rak was sent into the Erastus 16 reef / crab task with Sho, Harvus, Mirei, and Badger.

His reefclaw catch and usefulness in the task helped establish him as more than a useless press-ganged hand.

### Erastus 23 — Tilly Con and Rising Hostility

Rak and Tilly continued their gambling / arm-wrestling partnership.

The con against Crimson and others worked only partly.

Rak's poor bluffing or conduct created hostility with Maheem, Fipps, and Syl.

Tilly became nervous and warned Rak to avoid Maheem, Chumlett, and Syl for a while.

This is the clean pre-Man's Promise placement for Rak's tension with Fipps and Syl.

## Man's Promise History

### Erastus 25 — Man's Promise Boarding

Rak joined Sho's aft boarding party during the Man's Promise capture.

Rak killed at least one pleading or downed Rahadoumi sailor.

Sho objected and tried to impose restraint.

Rak's harsher violence and Sho's attempts to control it became a major moral split.

Rak was involved in a lifeboat incident on the starboard side; exact casualty result remains unresolved.

A private whisper preserves Rak's impulse to get rid of Giffer / toss her over.

### Giffer Tibbs — GM-Only Boundary

GM truth currently has Giffer unconscious but stable, then Rak intentionally stomped / trampled her while moving through the fog, destabilizing her and causing death.

This must remain GM-side unless magic, confession, or equivalent proof exposes it.

Do not tell Rak's player as a revealed archive fact unless the GM intends that knowledge to become explicit. Rak-facing material can preserve his known conduct and consequences without exposing hidden adjudication text.

### Erastus 26 — Harvus Suspicion Exchange

During the post-battle revel aftermath, Harvus privately asked Rak whether Rak had anything to do with Giffer no longer being with them.

Rak said he saw Giffer lying on the ground while moving toward another boat to stop escapees, but was not sure whether she was alive or dead.

Harvus accepted Rak's answer at the time.

This is a suspicion boundary, not proof. Keep it separate from GM truth.

### Erastus 27 — Grok Separation and Transfer

During the ship split, Kroop told Rak to help pack because they were heading with the new ship.

Grok did not come with Rak.

Grok held back tears and gave Rak a present before he left.

Rak's separation from Grok is both emotional and item-relevant.

### Erastus 28 — Borrowed Fiddle to Mirei

On the first Plugg-command workday, Mirei lacked her harp and needed an instrument for Plugg's ordered performance.

Rak lent Mirei the fiddle he had received from Grok.

Mirei used the borrowed fiddle for the first Plugg performance.

Rak knows he helped Mirei perform; he does not necessarily know the full private coercion inside Plugg's cabin.

### Erastus 29-30 — Course Change / Rickety's Squibs Channel

Kroop called Rak for a private talk in the galley and included Sandara.

Kroop said Plugg should not be expected to follow orders now that Harrigan was out of sight.

Sandara confirmed the ship was going east.

Kroop identified Rickety's Squibs as the likely destination and explained squibbing a ship.

Sandara told Rak and Kroop to keep the information between them and trust no one.

This places Rak inside an early anti-Plugg information channel before Bonewrack makes Plugg's betrayal more obvious.

## Storm / Bonewrack

### Erastus 31 / Arodus 1 — Storm and First Island Supply Mission

During storm / grindylow chaos, Rak took heavy damage and needed healing.

Sho checked whether Rak was all right.

Sho later reported that the grindylows seemed unusually interested in Rak.

Plugg sent Rak by rowboat with Sho, Mirei, and Harvus to gather fresh water and supplies.

Plugg told Kroop / Ambrose that the water barrels were ruptured and the ship needed supplies.

Do not assume Rak knows Mirei's hidden Plugg task or private alchemist-fire placement.

### Arodus 5 — Syl Supply Party Branch

Scourge questioned Rak about food, water, and routes over the swamp.

Rak reported the broken-bridge route.

Scourge changed the supply plan: Syl would lead the recovery team, the ship would sail and meet them at Coconut Beach, and the team would not stop at the caverns.

Scourge ordered Syl to take Jape, Shivikah, Fipps, and Rak.

Rak led the group to the broken bridge and fell into the water during the crossing.

Frogs attacked Rak and Fipps.

Shivikah helped Rak by cutting the frog's tongue loose.

Syl coordinated enough to keep Rak alive because she needed him.

Fipps panicked; Jape eventually landed the final hit.

The party reached the burned tree around 12:28 PM.

Around 1:30 PM, Rak argued for caution before the cornfields and resisted Syl's demand to push recklessly.

The party passed Coconut Beach; Syl ordered Fipps to stay there because the ship should meet them.

Rak, Syl, Shivikah, and Jape pushed inland.

Current endpoint: Rak hears something ahead at Arodus 5, 2:28 PM.

This is Rak's current cold-open branch. Do not import Riptide rescue confession or Sho/Kahina bargain into Rak's knowledge.

## Relationships

### Ambrose Kroop

Kroop is Rak's cook-side mentor and a practical survival contact.

Kroop included Rak in the Rickety's Squibs / course-change information channel with Sandara.

### Cut-Throat Grok

Grok remained aboard the Wormwood during the Erastus 27 split.

She gave Rak a parting gift and held back tears before he left.

Rak's separation from Grok should remain emotionally and item-relevant.

### Sho Astor

Sho has fought alongside Rak and benefited from his rescue actions.

She is also disturbed by his violence.

After the Man's Promise battle, Sho does not trust Rak. She blames him for direct atrocities during the battle, including the killing of a downed or pleading sailor and the unresolved lifeboat incident.

Sho does not know Rak's hidden Giffer intent unless later evidence exposes it.

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus privately asked Rak about Giffer after the Man's Promise battle and accepted Rak's ambiguous answer at the time.

Harvus later killed the Whale with Rak's spear. How Rak feels about Harvus using / losing that spear remains open.

### Mirei

Rak lent Mirei Grok's fiddle for her first Plugg-command performance aboard the Man's Promise.

Rak does not know Mirei's Arodus 5 confession unless later communication occurs.

### Slippery Syl Lonegan

Syl does not trust Rak and does not respect his authority.

She does, however, recognize his usefulness as a guide and repeatedly says she needs him alive.

The current relationship is coercive dependency: Syl needs Rak alive, but not free.

### Shivikah

Shivikah is with Rak's current branch and helped him during the frog fight by cutting the frog's tongue loose.

Whether that changes Rak's attitude toward her remains open.

### Jaundiced Jape

Jape is with Rak's current branch and landed the final hit in the frog fight.

His silence and hostile pressure remain relevant.

### Fipps Chumlett

Fipps was part of Rak's current branch until Syl left him at Coconut Beach to wait for the ship.
