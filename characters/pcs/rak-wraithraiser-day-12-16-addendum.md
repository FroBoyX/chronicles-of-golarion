## Summary

The recovered Day 12-16 cache adds Rak material around Tilly, rum, Kroop, Grok, Jack Scrimshaw, the boarding drill, Scourge's new rum-threat leverage, and Rak's social strategy aboard the Wormwood.

## New / Sharpened Beats

### Day 12 — Tilly and Drinking Contest

- Rak approaches Tilly looking to make more friends.
- Tilly says he should drink with her when they will not get beaten for slacking and invites him to join her that night.
- Tilly suggests improving the grub could earn Rak points.
- During the drinking setup, Rak tries to improve the rum and borrows from Tilly to buy in.
- Tilly likes Rak after the contest, even though she says he owes her.
- This supports Rak's sheet note that Tilly is helpful / friendly and gives a concrete social route: food, drink, and boldness.

### Day 12 — Kroop / Caulky / Galley Role

- Caulky comes to the galley for officers' food and tells Kroop to make it quick before Rak speaks.
- Kroop says he may work out Rak delivering food instead because Caulky is tired of coming down there.
- Kroop warns Rak not to push his luck with Caulky because she has the captain's ear.
- This sharpens Rak's galley access and Caulky's irritated-but-recurring exposure to him.

### Day 14 — Boarding Drill and Throwing Skill

- Rak participates in Riaris Krine's boarding drill with Sho, Mirei, Harvus, Shivikah, and Tam Tate.
- Rak joins the object-throwing betting game and performs well as a thrower.
- Riaris eventually praises Rak's throwing arm and says he should throw like that in real combat for extra portions.
- Rak still struggles with actual boarding/climbing, but the drill identifies him as useful in a fight through throwing and resilience.
- This reinforces Rak's practical combat usefulness without softening his chaotic table/character voice.

### Day 14 — Rum Discipline / Kroop Leverage

- Rak initially avoids confession when Scourge asks who failed to drink rum, then later confesses if that will end the scene.
- Scourge decides Rak does not take punishment seriously enough.
- Scourge orders that Kroop must ensure Rak drinks his ration.
- Scourge threatens to beat Kroop nearly to death if Rak fails, explicitly because Kroop's life belongs to Harrigan.
- Scourge also orders Rak to receive two rum rations for the remainder of the trip.
- This creates a major Rak/Kroop leverage point: Rak's rum refusal or avoidance now threatens Kroop directly.

### Day 15 — Kroop Confronts Rak

- Kroop confronts Rak about putting Kroop's neck on the line.
- Kroop says he has orders: Rak drinks, or Kroop gets whipped with him.
- Kroop says he does not mind sticking his neck out, but not at the cost of his skin.
- Kroop drinks with Rak while cooking to preserve the status quo.
- Rak asks whether Kroop or Grok can drink his share, but Kroop warns the ship has eyes.
- Grok offers to look for something that can take the edge off the rum, likely for a price.
- This strengthens Kroop as practical mentor and Grok as supplier / workaround contact.

### Day 15 — Jack Scrimshaw Reads Rak's Position

- Rak approaches Jack looking for friends because he has enough enemies.
- Jack tells Rak that the PCs could become big, but not if they are dead.
- Jack says Rak might do better without the dull monk and broken bird, because association with them is dangerous.
- Jack notes Rak is close with Grok and Fishguts, but Scourge clearly dislikes Rak and dislikes Sho/Harvus even more.
- Jack shows Rak a carving of the six PCs at the mast and frames it as either the humble beginning of famous captains or fools who did not understand ship etiquette.
- Jack makes clear he does not dislike Rak but does not want to be lumped in with him.
- This supports Rak's player-sheet note that Jack was friendly-ish but cautious, and gives Jack a concrete role as social observer.
