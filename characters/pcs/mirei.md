## Overview

Mirei is an aasimar Sea Singer bard from the River Kingdoms, devoted to Desna.

She has silver hair, emerald eyes, and a notably graceful or otherworldly appearance.

Her personal premise is player-led: she came to Port Peril seeking destiny and a deeper understanding of herself. Player discovery established that Mirei grew up on the river performing with her family, naturally drawing larger crowds, and eventually left because she could no longer ignore the call of the sea. Her first emotional response to the open ocean was wonder.

At the current relaunch point, Mirei is returning from Riptide Cove with Harvus G'ron, Sandara Quinn, Tilly Brackett, and Goyle Onórach. She has confessed that Plugg tasked her with killing the other PCs to prove loyalty, but she did not act on that order.

## Character Premise

Mirei is not simply a shipboard performer. She uses performance as contact, protest, memory, and survival.

Her gift for drawing crowds is player-confirmed background. The Wormwood and Man's Promise convert that gift into a dangerous resource: morale control for captains, a target for enforcers, a reason for people to gather, and a means for Mirei to make pain audible when direct resistance is impossible.

Her current arc is about whether her instinct to survive through song, social intuition, and careful compromise can remain hers after Plugg tries to make those same traits serve him.

## Wormwood History

### Early Performance / Harp Return

Mirei's early shipboard performances are central to how crew start noticing her.

The Day 2 performance is resolved as Erastus 6 / Day 2 night, roll 21. Harvus praised her, Rak praised her, and Badger complimented her and asked where she learned.

Whether that performance was the first actual performance after the harp return or only the first recovered performance remains unresolved.

### Day 3 — Sho Disguise Discovery

Mirei was the first recovered PC to detect that Sho's presentation was false.

The Sho player confirmed this created tension at first but quickly turned to trust once Mirei proved she could and would keep the secret.

Sho directly asked Mirei to keep the secret, and Mirei agreed.

This is a major trust foundation between Sho and Mirei.

### Storm-Window Harrigan Invitation Intercepted by Plugg

During an early Wormwood storm window, Mirei attempted to invite **Captain Harrigan** to her performance that night.

Plugg answered through the door instead, responded with hostility, ordered her below before the storm hit, and told her to give a performance that would pacify the `land-lubber scum`.

This should not be written as Mirei intentionally seeking Plugg out. Plugg intercepted or answered her attempt to reach Harrigan and converted it into an order.

### Rosie Rescue Messages

Mirei was involved in the wider response to Rosie's loss and the aftermath around rescue attempts.

Exact local placement remains tied to broader Rosie / Harvus / Sho reconstruction, but the event matters because it contributes to the crew sorting itself into those who treat others as people and those who treat loss as discipline or opportunity.

### Erastus 16 — Reef Injury

Mirei was part of the Erastus 16 reef / crab task with Sho, Harvus, Rak, and Badger.

She was injured by reef creatures or sea monsters, and Sho later apologized when she realized Mirei had faced that danger while Sho had missed part of it.

That night, Sandara approached Sho and Mirei with Harvus after hearing they had been hurt during or around the reef task.

## Late Wormwood Chronology

### Erastus 19 — Boarding Drill and Mast Punishment

Riaris Krine included Mirei in the grappling / boarding drill.

Mirei was treated as the performer / bard and criticized when she struggled, though she did manage at least one successful hit or throw.

Scourge called for confessions from those who avoided rum. Mirei confessed that she had not wanted to drink her rum.

Scourge halved the punishment because she confessed but still punished her severely.

Slippery Syl Lonegan grabbed Mirei and took her to the mast.

Scourge framed Mirei through the value of her voice / song and warned against losing it.

This punishment gives the next night's hurt performance its meaning.

### Erastus 20 — Sandara / Sho Aftermath and Hurt Performance

Sandara warned the PCs that drawing too much attention could get them killed before they could gain control.

Mirei said she would try not to draw attention but kept ending up on the mast.

Sandara called Mirei soft for a girl in the Shackles and warned that softness could get her killed.

Sho privately checked on Mirei and explained she had barely managed to check whether Mirei lived before Peppery and Syl cut her off.

Mirei chose to play her harp that night so the crew could hear how badly they had hurt her.

Ratline heard the damage in her playing.

This resolves the player performance-log `Night 15`: it is the post-lashing hurt performance, not ordinary entertainment.

### Erastus 21-22 — Recovery, Influence, and Social Contamination

Mirei remained badly injured and recovering.

Mirei attempted to influence Giffer Tibbs and warmed Giffer slightly.

Conchobhar warmed somewhat in the Day 16 span but later avoided Mirei because she was too close to Sho / the PCs.

Ratline became a receptive contact.

Sandara's warning about polarization is mirrored in how crew avoid association with Sho and those close to her.

Mirei's gift for contact remains real, but association with visible defiance becomes dangerous.

### Erastus 24-25 — Sho Hot-Box Message and Boarding Setup

Mirei magically messaged Sho while Sho was in the hot-box and asked whether she was all right.

Sho said she was fine and might have a way not to die.

Mirei clarified that she did not want Sho to die, meaning ever.

Sho asked Mirei to join the aft boarding party because the fog made the situation feel wrong and Sho would rather have Mirei close than separated.

Mirei agreed and received a dagger and scroll from Grok.

During the boarding approach, Mirei was badly wounded and delayed / constrained by injury.

## Man's Promise History

### Erastus 26-27 — Revel Danger and Transfer

During the revel, Sho saw drunken crew crowding around a fallen Mirei and challenged them.

Rak removed Mirei from the dangerous scene and carried her below.

Tam prevented the scene from becoming open violence.

When Harrigan split the crews, Plugg selected Mirei for the Man's Promise as personal entertainment / morale support.

The revel danger should be handled carefully and not sensationalized. Plugg's selection is coercive and instrumental, not romance or privilege.

### Erastus 28 — First Plugg Cabin Control Scene

Scourge announced no rum, no entertainment, and no above-deck presence after supper except night shift.

Mirei was ordered to report to Plugg with an instrument.

Mirei lacked her harp; Rak lent her the fiddle he had received from Grok.

Mirei performed extremely well for Plugg.

Plugg offered a hammock near him, then shifted into a cold weapon/pouch search and cabin-control rules.

Plugg took her pouch, allowed her to keep a potion, and said her life would improve if she kept him entertained.

Plugg ordered her not to touch anything without permission, to play each night, and not to speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.

Do not frame this as voluntary advancement. It is coercion and isolation inside Plugg's command space.

### Erastus 29-30 — Mistress Mirei, Tam / Jack, and Syl Knife Threat

Sandara, Tilly, Samms, Kroop, Rak, and/or Harvus pick up evidence that the ship is not headed correctly for Port Peril.

Plugg's chart behavior in the cabin increases suspicion.

Mirei becomes food-taster and controlled symbol in Plugg's cabin.

Plugg pressures her into agreeing to be his entertainer, gives her the former captain's clothing and belongings, and threatens to give her to Scourge if she disobeys.

Mirei proposes `Mistress Mirei` as the role-name.

Plugg orders her to dress and act as his voice among the crew.

Sandara and Sho react with alarm and pointed formality.

Mirei approaches Tam Narwhal Tate and Jack Scrimshaw; Tam challenges her role and warns her not to promise what she cannot keep.

Syl stops Mirei at the stairs, presses a knife into her back, threatens her voice, accuses her of mishandling Plugg's orders, draws blood, and says she and Scourge will see Mirei in the morning.

Session 6 is the cleanest contiguous source for this sequence. Jack Scrimshaw is preserved as the second sailor with Tam.

## Storm / Bonewrack

### Erastus 31 / Arodus 1 — Storm, Plugg Pressure, and Hidden Island Action

During storm / grindylow chaos, Sho catches Mirei before she hits the deck.

After Syl reports on Mirei's conduct, Plugg confronts Mirei and says she went off mission by talking to Sho / the monk.

Plugg tells Mirei the ship is out of food and water and that he is sending her less cooperative friends to the island.

Plugg gives Mirei a whispered loyalty task. The exact wording is not preserved in the current summary.

On the island, after the first rest, Mirei privately places an alchemist's fire in a tree while trying not to leave signs.

The outer shape of the Plugg task is confirmed by Arodus 5 confession, but exact wording remains unresolved. The alchemist-fire placement is GM-only unless exposed.

### Arodus 5 — Rescue-Side Confession

After Riptide rescue, Goyle says the returning group should expect no warm welcome and that Plugg knocked him overboard with the Tidal Cutlass.

Goyle says he overheard talk that the PCs would be killed when they returned if not already dead.

Mirei confesses that the trip was supposed to be one-way for most of them.

Mirei says she was tasked with killing the others to prove she was on Plugg's side.

Mirei says she did not do it because it did not feel right.

This confession is known to the Riptide rescue-side branch: Harvus, Tilly, Goyle, and likely Sandara depending consciousness / staging.

Do not expose this to Rak or Sho packets until in-game communication happens.

Plugg attempts to turn Mirei's voice, beauty, social skill, and performance into tools of command.

### Slippery Syl Lonegan
