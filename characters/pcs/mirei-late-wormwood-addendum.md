## Addendum Beats For Main Mirei Dossier

### Erastus 19 — Boarding Drill and Mast Punishment

- Riaris Krine included Mirei in the grappling / boarding drill.
- Mirei was treated as the performer / bard and criticized when she struggled, though she did manage at least one successful hit or throw.
- Scourge called for confessions from those who avoided rum.
- Mirei confessed that she had not wanted to drink her rum.
- Scourge halved the punishment because she confessed but still punished her severely.
- Slippery Syl Lonegan grabbed Mirei and took her to the mast.
- Scourge framed Mirei through the value of her voice / song and warned against losing it.

**Merge note:** This should be placed before the Day 15 hurt performance. It is the punishment that gives that performance its meaning.

### Erastus 20 — Sandara / Sho Aftermath and Hurt Performance

- Sandara warned the PCs that drawing too much attention could get them killed before they could gain control.
- Mirei said she would try not to draw attention but kept ending up on the mast.
- Sandara called Mirei soft for a girl in the Shackles and warned that softness could get her killed.
- Sho privately checked on Mirei and explained she had barely managed to check whether Mirei lived before Peppery and Syl cut her off.
- Mirei chose to play her harp that night so the crew could hear how badly they had hurt her.
- Ratline heard the damage in her playing.

**Merge note:** This resolves the player performance-log `Night 15` meaning.

### Erastus 21-22 — Recovery, Influence, and Social Contamination

- Mirei remained badly injured and recovering.
- Mirei attempted to influence Giffer Tibbs and warmed Giffer slightly.
- Conchobhar warmed somewhat in the Day 16 span but later avoided Mirei because she was too close to Sho / the PCs.
- Ratline became a receptive contact.
- Sandara's warning about polarization is mirrored in how crew avoid association with Sho and those close to her.

**Merge note:** This is the right placement for social contamination: Mirei's gift for contact remains real, but association with visible defiance becomes dangerous.

### Erastus 24-25 — Sho Hot-Box Message and Boarding Setup

- Mirei magically messaged Sho while Sho was in the hot-box and asked whether she was all right.
- Sho said she was fine and might have a way not to die.
- Mirei clarified that she did not want Sho to die, meaning ever.
- Sho asked Mirei to join the aft boarding party because the fog made the situation feel wrong and Sho would rather have Mirei close than separated.
- Mirei agreed and received a dagger and scroll from Grok.
- During the boarding approach, Mirei was badly wounded and delayed / constrained by injury.

**Merge note:** Keep this as Mirei's perspective on Sho's hot-box and boarding setup, not as full Sho command-state prose.

### Erastus 26-27 — Revel Danger and Transfer

- During the revel, Sho saw drunken crew crowding around a fallen Mirei and challenged them.
- Rak removed Mirei from the dangerous scene and carried her below.
- Tam prevented the scene from becoming open violence.
- When Harrigan split the crews, Plugg selected Mirei for the Man's Promise as personal entertainment / morale support.

**Merge note:** The revel danger should be handled carefully and not sensationalized. Plugg's selection is coercive and instrumental.

### Erastus 28 — First Plugg Cabin Control Scene

- Scourge announced no rum, no entertainment, and no above-deck presence after supper except night shift.
- Mirei was ordered to report to Plugg with an instrument.
- Mirei lacked her harp; Rak lent her the fiddle he received from Grok.
- Mirei performed extremely well for Plugg.
- Plugg offered a hammock near him, then shifted into a cold weapon/pouch search and cabin-control rules.
- Plugg took her pouch, allowed her to keep a potion, and said her life would improve if she kept him entertained.
- Plugg ordered her not to touch anything without permission, to play each night, and not to speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.

**Merge note:** Do not frame this as voluntary advancement. It is coercion and isolation inside Plugg's command space.

### Erastus 29-30 — Mistress Mirei, Tam/Jack, and Syl Knife Threat

- Sandara, Tilly, Samms, Kroop, Rak, and/or Harvus pick up evidence that the ship is not headed correctly for Port Peril.
- Plugg's chart behavior in the cabin increases suspicion.
- Mirei becomes food-taster and controlled symbol in Plugg's cabin.
- Plugg pressures her into agreeing to be his entertainer, gives her the former captain's clothing and belongings, and threatens to give her to Scourge if she disobeys.
- Mirei proposes `Mistress Mirei` as the role-name.
- Plugg orders her to dress and act as his voice among the crew.
- Sandara and Sho react with alarm and pointed formality.
- Mirei approaches Tam Narwhal Tate and Jack Scrimshaw; Tam challenges her role and warns her not to promise what she cannot keep.
- Syl stops Mirei at the stairs, presses a knife into her back, threatens her voice, accuses her of mishandling Plugg's orders, draws blood, and says she and Scourge will see Mirei in the morning.

**Merge note:** Session 6 is the cleanest contiguous source for this sequence. Jack Scrimshaw should be preserved as the second sailor with Tam.

### Erastus 31 / Arodus 1 — Storm, Plugg Pressure, and Hidden Island Action

- During storm / grindylow chaos, Sho catches Mirei before she hits the deck.
- After Syl reports on Mirei's conduct, Plugg confronts Mirei and says she went off mission by talking to Sho / the monk.
- Plugg tells Mirei the ship is out of food and water and that he is sending her less cooperative friends to the island.
- Plugg gives Mirei a whispered loyalty task; exact wording is not preserved in the current summary.
- On the island, after the first rest, Mirei privately places an alchemist's fire in a tree while trying not to leave signs.

**Merge note:** The outer shape of the Plugg task is confirmed by Arodus 5 confession, but exact wording remains unresolved. The alchemist-fire placement is GM-only unless exposed.

### Arodus 5 — Rescue-Side Confession

- After Riptide rescue, Goyle says the returning group should expect no warm welcome and that Plugg knocked him overboard with the sword.
- Goyle says he overheard talk that the PCs would be killed when they returned if not already dead.
- Mirei confesses that the trip was supposed to be one-way for most of them.
- Mirei says she was tasked with killing the others to prove she was on Plugg's side.
- Mirei says she did not do it because it did not feel right.
- This confession is known to the Riptide rescue-side branch: Harvus, Tilly, Goyle, and likely Sandara depending consciousness / staging.

**Merge note:** Do not expose this to Rak or Sho packets until in-game communication happens.
