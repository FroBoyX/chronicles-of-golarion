## Summary

The recovered Day 12-16 cache adds important Harvus material around his relationship with Sandara, his failure to contain rage under Scourge, his interaction with Sho's secret, the boarding drill, and Ratline's revenge counsel.

## New / Sharpened Beats

### Day 12 — Sandara and Wing Care

- Harvus asks Sandara to examine his wing dressing.
- Sandara says she has done what she can and is keeping an eye on him.
- This reinforces Sandara as ongoing caregiver for the broken-wing nerf, not just a one-time healer.

### Day 12 — Harvus Funds Mirei's Drinking Contest Buy-In

- During Rak and Tilly's drinking contest setup, Mirei asks Harvus for money.
- Harvus contributes coin for Mirei's buy-in and states it is for the aasimar.
- This is a small but useful early party-support beat: Harvus helps Mirei join the social activity.

### Day 13 — Harvus Learns Sho's Secret

- Harvus bluntly tells Sho he has deduced she is a woman.
- Sho reacts in alarm and asks him to keep it quiet.
- Harvus says it became apparent during one of Sho's whippings and promises to keep her secret.
- This adds Harvus as a PC who knew Sho's secret before the Man's Promise split.
- It also shows Harvus can be protective/honorable about the secret despite his tactlessness.

### Day 14 — Boarding Drill Failure

- Harvus participates in Riaris Krine's boarding drill.
- He struggles with the grappling / boarding exercise, in part because of his broken wing.
- Riaris explicitly criticizes him for the broken wing making him less useful.
- Harvus responds that if Riaris dislikes the broken wing, she should take it up with Scourge.
- This adds another public humiliation point attached to the broken-wing nerf.

### Day 14 — Scourge Escalation

- After the drill, Scourge uses the failed boarding and rum discipline to stage a broad punishment.
- Harvus insults Scourge when Scourge frames silence as insubordination.
- Scourge presses Harvus to explain whether he failed to drink rum, but Harvus maintains that he has displayed no insubordination.
- Harvus then uses Strix or non-common speech to insult Scourge while pretending the word was respectful.
- Scourge escalates and says Plugg will decide their fate.
- Tam Tate escorts Harvus to the mast and warns him to learn to shut his mouth before more of him ends up broken.

### Day 14 Bloody Hour — Plugg Punishes Harvus

- Plugg punishes Harvus directly with the cat, framing it as a lesson in Shackles customs and language.
- Plugg suggests that Harrigan, being more traveled, might understand Harvus' culture, using the idea as another layer of mockery.
- Harvus remains silent through the end of Plugg's exchange and slumps at the mast.
- Scourge later notices Harvus did not receive all of the failure lashes and finishes the punishment, complimenting his changed attitude with more lashes.
- Harvus drops fully after being unchained.
- This is a major pre-Man's Promise rage-control failure: Harvus had been warned to keep rage hidden, failed under provocation, and nearly paid with his life.

### Day 15 — Sandara Rage-Control Counsel Repeated

- Sandara confronts Harvus after the punishment and says he must keep his head down.
- Harvus admits he did what Sandara warned him not to do: his rage burned, and he failed to keep it in check.
- Sandara tells him to hide it, store it, and hold it back until Besmara willing he can unleash it on his enemies.
- She frames Besmara's chaos and rage as tools for gain, not excuses to lose.
- Harvus says the slip was a good lesson and that he will keep it in check.
- This directly reinforces the existing Harvus/Sandara rage arc.

### Day 15 — Ratline's Revenge Counsel

- Ratline speaks with Harvus after hearing the harp and noticing Harvus becoming heated.
- Ratline tells a story about losing fingers under a cruel boatswain, surviving, waiting, and eventually taking his own chance at revenge.
- Ratline warns Harvus not to let anger or thirst for revenge steal away his chance.
- Harvus accepts the advice and leaves for bed.
- This gives Harvus a second non-Sandara voice telling him to bide his time.
