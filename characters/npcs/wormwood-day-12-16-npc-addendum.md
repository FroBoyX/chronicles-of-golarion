## Summary

The recovered Day 12-16 cache strengthens multiple Wormwood NPC portraits. It is especially useful for Sandara Quinn, Master Scourge, Mr. Plugg, Riaris Krine, Slippery Syl, Peppery Longfarthing, Ratline Rattsberger, Jack Scrimshaw, Tilly Brackett, Owlbear Hartshorn, Tam Tate, Shivikah, and Ambrose Kroop.

## NPC Updates

### Sandara Quinn

- Continues tending Harvus' broken wing and tells him she is keeping an eye on him.
- Celebrates when none of the PCs get in trouble for a night.
- After the Day 14 mass mast punishment, confronts the PCs and says seeing six of them at the mast made her feel helpless for the first time aboard the Wormwood.
- Warns that the PCs need to draw less attention or they may never gain control.
- Tells Mirei and Harvus, in different ways, that sometimes appearing helpless or holding rage back preserves future options.
- Reveals Scourge once made a pass at her and she slapped him hard, but now she must keep her own head down.
- Warns Mirei that she is soft for the Shackles and may not survive long like that.
- Reinforces Sandara as compassionate but pragmatic, not merely a healer or romantic interest.

### Master Scourge

- Uses the boarding drill failure and rum-ration discipline as a broad punishment opportunity.
- Frames silence as insubordination when he asks for confessions.
- Specifically says his definition of insubordination is anything that does not go as he says.
- Punishes Mirei while emphasizing her voice/song value, warning they would hate to lose her voice.
- Uses Kroop as leverage against Rak: if Rak fails to drink, Kroop will be beaten nearly to death with him.
- Doubles Rak's rum ration for the remainder of the trip.
- Continues to provoke Harvus around the broken wing and punishes him after Plugg's cat lesson.
- This cache sharpens Scourge as a sadistic disciplinarian who uses rules as mutable tools of domination, not stable law.

### Mr. Plugg

- Lets Scourge escalate the mass punishment but takes over select punishments with the cat.
- Punishes Sho directly, framing the blows as lessons in command and understanding.
- Mocks Sho when she collapses, noting the absence of the tough girl.
- Punishes Harvus directly and uses the language of Shackles custom to humiliate him.
- Supports Scourge's framing that some PCs think they are above their station.
- This adds to Plugg's pattern of making discipline symbolic and identity-targeted.

### Riaris Krine

- Runs a harsh boarding drill with the PCs and selected crew.
- Treats the drill as practical preparation, not cruelty for its own sake.
- Criticizes weak boarding and poor climbing because failed boarding gets sailors killed.
- Praises Rak's throwing arm when he proves useful.
- Tells the group to stay alive, not rush forward and try to beat everyone alone, and use defense when overwhelmed.
- Begins an active-defense / blocking drill before Scourge interrupts the scene.
- This strengthens Riaris as brutal but competent; she is harsh because she expects boarding to matter.

### Slippery Syl Lonegan

- Grabs Mirei and brings her to the mast during the Day 14 punishment.
- Whispers for Harvus to slip up during Scourge's provocation.
- Later appears with a dagger when Sho tries to check Mirei after the mast punishment.
- Threatens to report Sho to Plugg in the morning and enjoys the leverage.
- This supports Syl as an observer-predator who watches for social weakness and uses report-threats and knives as tools.

### Peppery Longfarthing

- Orders Sho below after the Day 14 mast punishment and refuses to allow her to openly check the injured PCs.
- Says she has no intention of feeding into Plugg's thuggish attitude, but also refuses to protect Sho if Sho keeps pushing.
- Softens only enough not to report repeated defiance, then orders Sho to bed.
- Identifies herself as the Wormwood sailing master and as someone with no need to prove ego like Scourge or Plugg.
- This clarifies Peppery as disciplined, dangerous, and not sadistic. She may quietly choose not to escalate, but she is not safe.

### Ratline Rattsberger

- Notices Mirei's post-lashing harp performance and says she sounds worse for the lashings.
- Later speaks with Harvus and gives a personal story about surviving abuse on a prior ship, losing fingers, waiting for the right moment, and taking revenge when the chance came.
- Warns Harvus not to let anger or thirst for revenge steal away his chance.
- Ratline becomes a significant voice of patient survival and revenge timing.

### Jack Scrimshaw

- Tells Rak the PCs could become big, but not if they die.
- Says association with the PCs is dangerous while they keep getting punished.
- Notes Rak's ties to Grok and Fishguts but also Scourge's dislike.
- Shows Rak a carving of the six at the mast, framing it as either the beginning of future captains or fools who failed to understand ship etiquette.
- Makes clear he does not dislike Rak but refuses to be visibly lumped in with him.
- Jack's cache role is cautious social analyst and observer-carver.

### Tilly Brackett

- Invites Rak to drink with her and suggests improving the grub could earn points.
- Organizes or helps run a high-stakes rum drinking contest with buy-in and winner-takes-pot rules.
- Loans Rak money for the buy-in and later tells him he owes her, but likes him.
- Tilly's role here is social connector, gambler, and practical risk-taker.

### Owlbear Hartshorn

- Speaks with Sho while chained.
- Indicates that Plugg keeps him chained because Plugg worries about what happens if Owlbear is loose.
- Frames the rats as company and is receptive when Sho offers to visit and talk.
- This reinforces Owlbear as lonely captive/tool rather than simple brute.

### Tam "Narwhal" Tate

- Participates in the boarding drill with Shivikah and Riaris.
- Applies harsh practical logic: if someone cannot learn to handle thrown objects in training, real boarding will be worse.
- Warns Harvus to shut his mouth before more of him ends up broken.
- Maintains a rough respect / competitiveness around Rak's throwing and the boarding drill.

### Shivikah

- Joins the boarding drill object-throwing game.
- Pressures Sho to participate and frames it as realistic rather than friendly.
- Threatens to throw Sho over or cut a rope if the drill is not taken seriously.
- Shows the same hard practical streak later seen on Bonewrack with Rak's supply party.

### Ambrose "Fishguts" Kroop

- Is made leverage against Rak by Scourge's rum discipline.
- Confronts Rak afterward, saying Rak has put Kroop's neck and skin on the line.
- Drinks with Rak while cooking, trying to preserve the status quo and avoid more punishment.
- Warns that the ship has eyes when Rak looks for ways around drinking his own rum.
- This adds direct pressure to Rak/Kroop: Kroop likes Rak but cannot afford Rak's defiance.

### Cut-Throat Grok

- Appears during the Day 15 galley/rum conversation.
- Says the rum or the whip is the choice and offers to look for something that can take the edge off the rum, probably for a price.
- This supports Grok as a practical supplier and ally-adjacent workaround contact.
