## Overview

Mr. Plugg is the Wormwood's former first mate and the self-declared captain of the Man's Promise.

He is young, ambitious, cruel, insecure, and more brittle than he wants anyone to see. His authority rests on discipline, fear, punishment, delegated cruelty, and the borrowed legitimacy of Captain Barnabas Harrigan.

At the current relaunch point, Plugg is the immediate political and physical antagonist aboard the Man's Promise. His control is real, but not stable. The ship is damaged, supplies are critical, his betrayal is becoming visible, and multiple branches now hold information that can undermine him.

## Appearance

Plugg is bald except for a black ponytail and has a long, narrow beard.

He somehow appears angry, miserable, and uncomfortable at the same time.

He wears an immaculate coat with pearl buttons, keeps himself polished and controlled, and carries a cat-o'-nine-tails as a constant symbol of authority.

His teeth are gray and yellow, and his face is fixed in an almost constant grimace.

## Canon / Established

### Baseline Adventure Path Role

Plugg is the main villain of **The Wormwood Mutiny**.

At first he is practically unassailable because he is protected by Harrigan's authority and pirate discipline aboard the Wormwood.

After the PCs are placed aboard the Man's Promise, Plugg's authority becomes more exposed and more brittle, creating the conditions for mutiny.

The campaign's current dramatic question is not only whether Plugg is defeated, but what kind of crew replaces him.

### Motivation

Plugg wants his own ship and his own legend.

He joined the Wormwood for his own advancement and rose rapidly through devotion to Harrigan's orders, blackmail, violence, and opportunism.

He is painfully aware that Harrigan's true inner circle excludes him, and that his title exceeds his actual competence.

He masks insecurity by pointing out weakness in others.

### Psychology

Plugg is angry, but not uncontrolled.

His rage simmers rather than explodes. When he loses his temper, he often regains control quickly and redirects anger into punishment or revenge.

He is sadistic, but his sadism is also strategic. He uses cruelty to define status and to remind others that only his judgment matters.

He is brave in combat but not loyal. If survival requires abandoning allies, crew, or captain, he will do so.

### Authority Model

Plugg's authority comes from:

- Harrigan's appointment and implied protection aboard the Wormwood,
- control of daily discipline,
- alliance with Master Scourge,
- fear of punishment,
- possession of magical and coercive tools,
- delegated enforcers such as Slippery Syl Lonegan,
- and the absence of a stable rival aboard the Man's Promise at the moment of transfer.

His legitimacy is weaker than his power. This distinction is central to the relaunch.

## Campaign History

### Wormwood Period

Plugg was first mate aboard the Wormwood and second-in-command to Captain Harrigan.

He administered the tests that assigned the newly press-ganged PCs to shipboard roles.

He assigned Harvus and Conchobhar as riggers, assigned Rak as cook's mate, and placed others into lower crew roles.

He repeatedly targeted PCs and their allies through discipline, indirect violence, and manipulation.

During the early Wormwood storm window, Mirei attempted to invite **Captain Harrigan** to her performance that night. Plugg answered through the door instead. He answered with hostility, then ordered her below before the storm hit and told her to give a performance that would pacify the `land-lubber scum`.

This shows Plugg recognizing Mirei's performance as a tool for crew morale control before the later Man's Promise manipulation arc. It should not be written as Mirei intentionally seeking Plugg out; he intercepted or answered her attempt to reach Harrigan.

On the morning of Erastus 16, after Ratline called land ho, Plugg summoned Harvus, Rak, Sho, and Mirei to deck, then ordered Scourge to fetch Badger as a fifth. Plugg stood with buckets at his feet and said the captain wanted crabs. He ordered the five to swim to the reef to fore and starboard, fill each bucket with crabs, and swim back.

This scene is the direct setup for the reef / crab task.

### Late Wormwood Pressure

By Erastus 22, Plugg had taken specific notice of Sho's defiance and was tracking who interacted with her.

The Erastus 24 bilges ambush and hot-box setup should be understood within this larger pressure pattern: Plugg and Scourge use crew hostility, assignment control, and punishment structures to isolate and break troublesome new hands.

Sho's interrupted keelhauling on Erastus 25 is now tied directly to the Man's Promise sighting and capture date, not to Man's Promise Day 1.

### Man's Promise Capture and Split

The Wormwood captured the Man's Promise on **Erastus 25, 4712 AR**.

This is the capture date, not Man's Promise Day 1.

After the Erastus 26 revel and the Erastus 27 prisoner/split sequence, Harrigan assigned Plugg to command a skeleton crew aboard the prize ship.

Man's Promise Day 1 begins on **Erastus 28, 4712 AR**, the first full Plugg-command workday after the split and separation.

### First Man's Promise Command Speech

On Erastus 27, after Harrigan assigned the skeleton crew, Plugg gathered the new Man's Promise crew and demanded to be addressed as **Captain Plugg**.

He named Scourge first mate and threatened disrespect with whip or knife.

He appointed Slippery Syl Lonegan as boatswain and ordered the crew to obey Syl's commands as if they came directly from him.

Plugg's public stated task was to sail back to Port Peril and then return to the Wormwood.

He ordered Sho to report to the poop deck of the Man's Promise and corrected her that he was now `Captain`.

He ordered Mirei to report as personal entertainment to keep morale up.

This speech establishes Plugg's first public model of command on the Man's Promise: title, fear, delegated cruelty, and no tolerance for dissent.

### First Night With Mirei in the Cabin

On the first recovered night aboard the Man's Promise, Scourge ordered Mirei to report to the captain's cabin with her instrument.

The room was still marked by the boarding. The Man's Promise captain had been killed in that cabin, and blood remained splashed across the floor, desk, and walls.

Mirei performed for Plugg with a borrowed fiddle and performed well enough to please him. Plugg offered her a hammock in the cabin area, saying someone with her skill did not belong with the lower crew.

When Mirei accepted because it would be quieter, Plugg's face went cold. He required a weapon check, asked if she trusted her captain, then pushed her against the bulkhead, turned her around, and ordered her to stand still.

The search was slow and coercive. Plugg took a pouch from her, allowed her to keep a potion, and whispered that if she kept him entertained, her life would improve.

After Mirei returned with the hammock, Plugg gave strict rules: she could set up where ordered, touch nothing without permission, play for him each night, and not speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.

This is a major escalation in Plugg's control of Mirei. It begins as privileged placement, but functions as isolation, surveillance, coercion, and possession. The bloodied cabin makes that offer more openly threatening: Plugg's claimed private authority space is also the visible site of the former captain's death.

### Course Change

The Man's Promise changed course away from Port Peril.

Multiple characters came to suspect that Plugg intended to betray Harrigan and take the ship for himself, likely toward Rickety's Squibs or another place where the ship could be altered and hidden.

This makes the Erastus 27 Port Peril statement important: it is either the public order Plugg later abandons or an early lie.

On Erastus 29, Sandara and Tilly already knew the course had changed, and Plugg quickly folded a chart when Mirei entered the locked cabin with food.

### Erastus 29 Mirei Duties and Agreement

On Erastus 29, Plugg expanded Mirei's duties.

When Mirei brought his food, Plugg quickly folded a chart, ordered her to bring the food over, then required her to taste both dishes. After she did, he declared food-tasting part of her duties.

When Scourge called Mirei `wench` and Mirei objected, Plugg stopped Scourge as if defending her respect, then struck Mirei and corrected the term to `Captain's wench`.

Later that night, Mirei returned to entertain him. Scourge opened the door, greeted her as `Captain's wench`, and walked out.

Plugg accepted another fiddle performance and told her to prepare a song for the next day. When Mirei asked what kind of song, Plugg answered that they would cross that bridge **if** it came.

After the performance, Plugg returned to the `unpleasant` wench business.

He said that if Mirei agreed to be his entertainer, she would stay there, no longer work as a swab, and be treated with the respect of an officer on the Man's Promise.

Mirei said she would not mind still helping around the ship because she feared respect without work would be sarcastic and fake. Plugg rejected labor as the source of her new respect. He told her she would earn respect through voice and presence, that he would raise her in station with him as a lady of a pirate lord, and that she must sing his deeds.

When his voice went cold and he demanded an answer, Mirei agreed.

Plugg then set the terms: only he would call her wench; everyone else would need a name for her, which she had to choose by morning; the dead female captain's clothing and belongings were now hers; obedience was mandatory; if she did his bidding, she would earn respect; and if she did anything wrong, he would give her to Scourge to do with as Scourge pleased.

Until then, Plugg said, she had his protection.

This is Plugg's clearest recovered attempt to turn Mirei into a renamed, officer-adjacent, protected possession and public singer of his future legend.

### Erastus 30 Mistress Mirei Pressure

Mirei chose or proposed `Mistress Mirei` as the name for the role Plugg imposed.

Plugg ordered her to dress and carry signs of station, including station-appropriate clothing, jewelry, and a jeweled sword, and to go among the crew to gauge where they stood.

Plugg also used Syl as an observer of Mirei's performance. Syl later intercepted Mirei, wounded her with a knife, and threatened that she and Scourge would see Mirei in the morning.

This shows Plugg building a control system around Mirei: elevation, isolation, surveillance, delegated threat, and the expectation that she prove loyalty by reporting on or manipulating the crew.

### Storm / Bonewrack Pressure

During the storm and grounding at Bonewrack Isle, Plugg's leadership became harsher and more unstable.

The current storm-abduction structure is fixed: Sandara Quinn and Tilly Brackett were the initial Erastus 31 storm-abduction victims. Goyle Onórach was not part of that initial abduction.

Goyle was captured later, during a subsequent attack while the PCs were away from the Man's Promise. Goyle testifies that Plugg used the **Tidal Cutlass** to wash him and a grindylow overboard while Goyle was fighting one of the boarders.

Sho was beaten unconscious after resisting or provoking Scourge's shore party and was later moved aboard the Man's Promise, waking in the hot-box.

Mirei was drawn into Plugg's orbit and pressured into proving loyalty by acting against the other PCs. The exact whispered command is unresolved, but the outer shape is confirmed by Arodus 5 confession: Mirei says she was tasked with killing the others to prove loyalty and did not do it.

## Relationships

### Barnabas Harrigan

Plugg served Harrigan and benefited from his protection.

He appears to want Harrigan's kind of power while lacking Harrigan's deeper command presence.

If Harrigan learns Plugg lost the Man's Promise while surviving, Harrigan is expected to be unforgiving.

### Master Scourge

Scourge is Plugg's right-hand man and enforcer.

Plugg gives direction and protection. Scourge carries out discipline and feeds hostility toward the PCs.

On Erastus 27, Scourge accepted or performed the role of first mate in Plugg's first public command structure aboard the Man's Promise.

On the first recovered Man's Promise night, Scourge delivered Mirei to Plugg's authority and later brought a hammock to the cabin after Plugg demanded she sleep there.

On Erastus 29, Plugg explicitly threatened that if Mirei did anything wrong, he would give her to Scourge to do with as Scourge pleased.

At Arodus 5, Scourge has directed or shaped the shore/supply-party pressure and remains the likely immediate enforcer of Plugg's regime.

### Slippery Syl Lonegan

Plugg appointed Syl as boatswain in his first Man's Promise command speech.

Her orders were to be treated as Plugg's orders.

On Erastus 30, Plugg used Syl as an observer of Mirei's loyalty / performance.

Syl is useful to Plugg because she converts his authority into intimate threat and delegated violence.

### Mirei

Plugg treated Mirei as a prize and tried to turn her into an asset against the other PCs.

Before that later pressure, he had already seen or used Mirei's performances as a way to pacify crew morale during a storm.

On Erastus 27, Plugg ordered Mirei to report to the Man's Promise as personal entertainment to keep morale up.

On the first night, Plugg converted that entertainment role into cabin isolation: performance in the bloodied captain's cabin, a hammock near him, a coercive search, seizure of a pouch, strict speech rules, and a promise that her life would improve if she kept him entertained.

On Erastus 29, he escalated again: food-tasting duty, a hidden chart, a title correction into `Captain's wench`, a demanded entertainer agreement, a new name by morning, the dead captain's clothing and belongings, and a direct threat to hand her to Scourge if she disobeyed.

He gave her a privileged but dangerous position and expected betrayal as proof of loyalty.

### Sho Astor

Sho's defiance drew Plugg's attention.

He tracked her interactions and ensured pressure was placed on anyone associated with defiance.

The Erastus 24 bilges ambush / hot-box sequence and Erastus 25 interrupted keelhauling sit inside that pressure pattern.

On Erastus 27, Plugg ordered Sho to the poop deck of the Man's Promise and insisted she call him Captain.

At Arodus 5, Sho is confined in or around the hot-box and has become a possible lever for Kahina and the Man's Promise survivor bloc.

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus is a dangerous subordinate and a potential threat to Plugg's authority.

Harvus' rescue of Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle may undermine Plugg's control if revealed effectively.

### Rak Wraithraiser

Rak's violence, confidence, and practical competence make him useful but difficult to control.

Plugg's ability to retain authority over Rak is uncertain.

Rak is currently in the Syl-led supply branch rather than aboard ship.

### Sandara Quinn

Sandara is a threat because she offers the PCs friendship, healing, pirate religious legitimacy, and another model of crew loyalty.

Her rescue is likely dangerous to Plugg's position.

### Goyle Onórach

Goyle is politically dangerous to Plugg.

He can testify that Plugg used the Tidal Cutlass to wash him overboard during a later attack while the PCs were away.

If Goyle speaks before neutral, survivor-aligned, or wavering crew, he can turn Plugg's legitimacy problem into a public accusation.

### Kahina Bukekayo and Man's Promise Survivors

Kahina represents a survivor-facing path to shipboard resistance that does not require forgiving Sho or the PCs.

The Man's Promise survivor bloc may oppose Plugg without becoming loyal to the PCs.

This is dangerous to Plugg because he depends on fear and utility, not earned legitimacy.

Plugg's signature weapon.

Older notes may say `tidewater cutlass`; current normalized name is **Tidal Cutlass**.

It can cast hydraulic push once per day and has been used to make violence look like shipboard accident.

Campaign-relevant use: pushing Goyle and a grindylow overboard during the Bonewrack sequence.

### Cat-o'-Nine-Tails

Constant symbol of disciplinary authority.

### Shackles of Compliance

A coercive magic item that reinforces Plugg's dependence on intimidation and enforced submission.

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Plugg is not just a combat boss. He is the current illegitimate command structure.

His defeat, survival, escape, or exposure determines what kind of crew politics follow.

Possible relaunch functions:

- Immediate antagonist if the return branches expose him.
- Political target if Goyle, Mirei, Sho, Kahina, or Sandara make accusations public.
- Combat threat if he uses the Tidal Cutlass, cat, or shackles to retain control.
- Flight risk if command collapses.
- Harrigan liability if he survives but loses the Man's Promise.
