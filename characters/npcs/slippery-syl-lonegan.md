# Slippery Syl Lonegan

## Document Status

- Type: NPC dossier
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Hostile Wormwood pirate, Man's Promise pressure point, current fork threat

## Overview

Slippery Syl Lonegan is an unhinged murderer who fled to sea to escape the gallows.

At the current relaunch point, she is with Rak Wraithraiser's moving supply party on Bonewrack Isle alongside Shivikah and Jaundiced Jape.

She is one of the most immediate sources of danger at the fork because she is violent, opportunistic, and present when Harvus' rescued group approaches.

## Identity

- Name: Slippery Syl Lonegan
- Type: NPC
- Baseline role: Wormwood rigger / hostile crew
- Baseline alignment: Chaotic Evil
- Current location: Bonewrack Isle, with Rak's moving group
- Current status: Alive

## Appearance / Presence

Precise appearance should be recovered from logs or player memory.

Her table role is already clear: she should feel sharp, unstable, and predatory rather than merely loud.

## Baseline Adventure Path Role

Syl begins as one of the hostile lower-crew pirates aligned with Plugg and Scourge.

She participates in the early harassment of the PCs and is difficult or impossible to redeem once polarized.

In the published structure, hostile NPCs like Syl help determine how hard the final mutiny becomes.

## Campaign Role

Syl is one of the lower-crew faces of the old order.

Where Scourge represents abusive authority, Syl represents the kind of sailor who thrives under that abuse because it lets her hurt others.

She is also tactically relevant. She is not only wild; she can read situations and preserve useful assets when necessary.

## Campaign History

### Wormwood Harassment

Syl was part of the early hostile crew pressure against the PCs under Scourge's influence.

### Man's Promise

Syl transferred to the Man's Promise prize crew and aligned with Plugg's harsher command structure.

She threatened Mirei with a knife and observed her in connection with Plugg's attempt to use Mirei as a spy or compromised figure.

### Bonewrack Supply Party

Syl is currently with Rak, Shivikah, and Jape on Bonewrack Isle.

Although hostile to Rak, she needs him alive because he knows the island route and hazards.

This makes the current dynamic tense rather than simply violent.

## Relationship With Rak

Syl does not trust Rak and does not respect his authority.

She does, however, recognize his usefulness as a guide.

This means she may tolerate him until his usefulness ends or until a better opportunity appears.

## Relationship With Mirei

Syl threatened Mirei with a knife during the Man's Promise phase.

She saw Mirei as weak, interesting, or compromised, and recognized that there was at least some fire behind Mirei's fear.

If Syl sees Mirei returning alive with rescued captives, that may alter her assumptions.

## Relationship With Plugg and Scourge

Syl is aligned with Plugg and Scourge's hostile camp, but her loyalty should not be assumed to be principled.

She may serve cruelty, advantage, or survival more than formal loyalty.

## Relationship With Shivikah and Jape

Syl is likely the strongest personality in the current moving group other than Rak.

Shivikah may be more cautious or movable.

Jape is dangerous but less socially expressive.

Syl may try to dominate both if the fork scene turns uncertain.

## Current State

- Location: Bonewrack Isle, near the fork route
- Companions: Rak, Shivikah, Jape
- Not with group: Fipps, currently at the beach
- Immediate objective: Recover food and water / obey Scourge's mission
- Immediate risk: Encountering Harvus, Mirei, Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle alive

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Syl is the test of whether the fork becomes a fight.

She may respond to rescued witnesses by:

- attacking to silence them,
- backing away to report,
- threatening Rak,
- trying to seize Sandara or Goyle,
- accusing Mirei,
- or reassessing if the power balance changes.

## What Not To Assume

Do not assume Syl fights to the death.

Do not assume she obeys Plugg if Plugg appears doomed.

Do not assume she is stupid.

She is violent and unstable, but can still be tactically self-preserving.
