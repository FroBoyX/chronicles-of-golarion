## Overview

Slippery Syl Lonegan is a CE human pirate knife-fighter and murderer who fled to sea to escape the gallows.

She is not merely loud, wild, or impulsive. The current GM characterization frames her as tall, slender, cold, calculating, predatory, and socially unsettling. Her blind spot is not lack of thought; it is that she tends to understand fear, violence, and killing as universal solutions.

At the current relaunch point, Syl is with Rak Wraithraiser's moving supply party on Bonewrack Isle alongside Shivikah and Jaundiced Jape. Fipps Chumlett has been left behind at Coconut Beach under her order.

Syl is one of the most immediate sources of danger at the fork because she is violent, present, armed with authority from Plugg/Scourge, and capable of tactical command under pressure.

## Appearance / Presence

Precise visual details still need final GM lock, but the current characterization should guide future descriptions:

- Tall and slender.
- Cold, calculating, and predatory rather than manic.
- Sinisterly beautiful in a socially unsettling way.
- Calm because she has already decided where the knife will go.
- Near-compulsive relationship with one of her knives; she often touches or uses the knife as social punctuation.
- Practical Skull & Shackles pirate dress: weathered leather, worn shirt or sleeveless layer, trousers, sash, boots, wrist wraps, knife belt.

Do not write Syl as random comic chaos. She can be unstable, but her instability expresses through controlled threat, fixation, and a belief that violence solves ambiguity.

## Canon / Established

### Baseline Adventure Path Role

Syl begins as one of the hostile lower-crew pirates aligned with Plugg and Scourge.

She participates in early hostile crew pressure against the PCs and is difficult or impossible to redeem once polarized.

In the published structure, hostile NPCs like Syl help determine how hard the final mutiny becomes.

### Wormwood Harassment

Syl was part of the early hostile crew pressure against the PCs under Scourge's influence.

On Erastus 19, after Mirei confessed to avoiding rum, Syl was the sailor who grabbed Mirei and took her to the mast for punishment.

This is the first currently bridged direct placement of Syl as an officer-side handler for Mirei's pain / discipline arc.

### Rak / Tilly Con Fallout

On Erastus 23, Rak and Tilly's con worked only partly.

Rak's poor bluffing or conduct created hostility with Maheem, Fipps, and Syl. Tilly became nervous and warned Rak to avoid Maheem, Chumlett, and Syl for a while.

This gives Syl's later Arodus 5 coercive hostility toward Rak a pre-Man's Promise social foundation.

### Man's Promise Transfer and Boatswain Appointment

Syl transferred to the Man's Promise prize crew and aligned with Plugg's harsher command structure.

On Erastus 27, during Plugg's first command speech aboard the Man's Promise, Plugg appointed `Ms. Sly` / Slippery Syl as boatswain.

Plugg ordered the crew to treat Syl's orders as if they came directly from him.

After Scourge reinforced the threat of quick discipline, Syl took command of immediate work assignments, ordered everyone to keep their Wormwood jobs, and threatened slackers with being put on the mast under her care.

This appointment formalizes Syl's authority aboard the Man's Promise before the later Bonewrack supply mission.

### First Man's Promise Nightshift

On the first recovered night aboard the Man's Promise, Syl demanded attention below decks and called for five volunteers for night watch.

She treated reluctance as consent and assigned people as `volunteered`.

She named Fipps Chumlett as boatswain for nightshift and told the crew they would follow his every order.

Syl then assigned Harvus to the rigging with Ebony and assigned Sho and Goyle as swabs.

This scene shows Syl immediately translating her new boatswain authority into delegated night labor, surveillance, and intimidation.

### Erastus 30 Observation of Mistress Mirei

On Erastus 30, Plugg ordered Mirei to move through the crew as **Mistress Mirei**, gauge where people stood, and report back. Plugg also told Syl to observe how Mirei handled herself.

After Mirei refused to report Tam's disrespectful challenge to Plugg, Syl intercepted Mirei on the stairs up from below deck.

Syl pressed a knife into Mirei's back and said she did not know what Mirei had said to Plugg, but Mirei did not look or sound like a pirate mistress.

She threatened Mirei's voice directly, imagining what Mirei would sound like if her vocal folds were cut out.

Syl said Plugg had told her to see how Mirei handled herself, and from Syl's vantage, Mirei had failed. She also noticed Mirei whispering with Sho and said Scourge would have a field day with her.

When Mirei insisted she had only done what the captain asked, Syl said Plugg had told Mirei to find out **where** people stood and had told Syl to observe her.

Syl was not impressed. She threatened that the night would likely be Mirei's last, then dug the knife in until it drew blood.

Mirei pushed back, saying that if she were lying, the captain would not trust her with what he had so far, and told Syl to back off.

Syl stepped back and reassessed her. She recognized some spark of life behind the farce and contrasted it with the docility she had seen in the cabin.

Syl's own creed is that she does not bark with words; she barks with knives.

Syl then said she and Scourge would see Mirei in the morning.

This scene proves Syl was the one who reported or escalated Mirei's handling of the Tam exchange to Plugg's side, not Mirei herself. It also shows Syl testing whether there is actual pirate fire beneath Plugg's imposed Mistress persona.

### Scourge's Arodus 5 Supply Order

After Rak reported the broken bridge route, Scourge changed the plan and assigned Syl to lead the food and water recovery team.

Her orders were:

- take Jape, Shivikah, Fipps, and Rak,
- do not stop at the caverns,
- recover food,
- bring water from the spring down to the beach,
- and prepare for the ship to meet the team at the south / Coconut Beach for supplies.

This makes Syl the current on-island arm of Plugg/Scourge discipline in Rak's branch.

### Broken Bridge / Frog Fight

Syl ordered Rak to cross first because he had crossed before.

When Rak fell into the water, she called him a stupid snake but still recognized that the group needed him alive.

During the frog fight, she moved across the pillars, threw an axe, and later leapt from a post to slash a frog with her cutlass.

This confirms she is not only threatening from behind authority; she can act in the fight when necessary.

### Botfly / Formation Sequence

When Rak wanted to run ahead from the swarms, Syl called him back and insisted the group move together.

She explicitly said she needed him alive and that if she did not, he would already be dead.

She produced flasks, distributed them, and set formation:

- Jape in the lead,
- Fipps in the middle,
- Shivikah guarding Fipps,
- Rak in the rear.

This proves Syl can preserve assets and impose order even while threatening them.

### Coconut Beach

At Coconut Beach, Syl said the ship should be meeting them there soon.

She ordered Fipps to stay at the beach and pushed everyone else on toward the stronghold.

This resolves Fipps' separation as Syl's order.

## Relationships

### Rak Wraithraiser

Syl does not trust Rak and does not respect his authority.

She does, however, recognize his usefulness as a guide and repeatedly says she needs him alive.

She threatens his weapon, his hide, and his survival if he disobeys.

Rak's sheet lists Syl as unfriendly, which is consistent with her behavior.

The current relationship is coercive dependency: Syl needs Rak alive, but not free.

### Mirei

Syl threatened Mirei with a knife during the Man's Promise phase.

She saw Mirei as weak, interesting, compromised, or all three, and recognized that there was at least some fire behind Mirei's fear.

On Erastus 30, Syl was tasked by Plugg to observe how Mirei handled herself as Mistress. She judged Mirei's crew interaction harshly, suspected her whispers with Sho, wounded her with a knife, and threatened that Scourge would have a field day with her.

If Syl sees Mirei returning alive with rescued captives, that may alter her assumptions and force an immediate tactical choice.

### Mr. Plugg

Syl is aligned with Plugg's hostile camp, but her loyalty should not be assumed to be principled.

Plugg publicly elevated her as boatswain and ordered the crew to obey her as they would obey him.

On Erastus 30, Plugg used Syl as an observer of Mirei's loyalty / performance.

Syl may serve Plugg's authority because it gives her license to hurt people, not because she has ideological loyalty.

### Master Scourge

Syl follows Scourge's supply order and acts as his on-island enforcement arm in Rak's branch.

Her threats and discipline style align naturally with Scourge's regime.

Whether she is more loyal to Scourge, Plugg, herself, or violence remains open.

### Fipps Chumlett

Syl delegated nightshift boatswain authority to Fipps on the first recovered Man's Promise night.

She later ordered Fipps to stay at Coconut Beach as the ship rendezvous contact or guard.

This suggests Fipps is a subordinate tool she will use for delegated coercion when useful.

### Shivikah and Jape

Syl is the immediate commander of the current moving group.

Shivikah follows but appears nervous during the Rak/Syl confrontation.

Jape appears disinterested in the verbal exchange but follows the movement.

Syl may try to dominate both if the fork scene turns uncertain.

### Goyle Onórach

Syl's current branch does not yet know, or at least is not confirmed to know, that Goyle survived Riptide.

If she learns Goyle can testify against Plugg and the Tidal Cutlass incident, he becomes a direct political threat to the Plugg axis.

### Sho Astor

Syl has repeatedly watched Sho's association with others become dangerous. She noticed Mirei whispering with Sho during the Mistress sequence and treated that connection as evidence against Mirei.

Whether Syl knows Sho's exact current hot-box status at Arodus 5 is unresolved.

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Syl is the immediate pressure point where Plugg's shipboard tyranny reaches the island branch.

She can turn the approaching branch collision into:

- a fight,
- a threat-and-retreat,
- a hostage attempt,
- a report back to Plugg,
- a moment of fracture if Goyle's testimony makes Plugg look weak,
- or a temporary tactical compromise if supplies / survival matter more than loyalty.

Syl should not be reduced to a disposable combatant. She is a violent, self-preserving enforcer with enough tactical sense to create complications.

## Player-Facing Caution

- Do not reveal Syl's full role in observing Mirei unless the character has reason to know it.
- Do not expose split-branch Riptide rescue knowledge to Rak/Syl branch before in-game contact.
- Do not frame Syl as comic crazy. She is threatening because she can be calm and deliberate.
- Do not assume she fights to the death.
- Do not assume she remains loyal to Plugg if Plugg appears doomed.
- Do not assume she is stupid because she is violent.
