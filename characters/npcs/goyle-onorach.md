# Goyle Onórach

## Document Status

- Type: NPC dossier
- Maturity: Draft, expanded with Arodus 5 split-scene log
- Scope: Man's Promise survivor, rescued captive, anti-Plugg witness

## Overview

Goyle Onórach is a Man's Promise sailor rescued from Riptide Cove alongside Sandara Quinn and Tilly Brackett.

His survival matters because he is not only another rescued prisoner. He is a witness.

He can testify to Plugg's dishonor, Plugg's use of the tidewater cutlass, and the plan to eliminate troublesome PCs.

## Identity

- Name: Goyle Onórach
- Type: NPC
- Origin: Man's Promise crew
- Baseline temperament: Gruff, honor-conscious, sailorly
- Current location: Bonewrack Isle, returning from Riptide Cove
- Current status: Alive, rescued
- Current stance: Casts his lot with Harvus' group after rescue

## Campaign Role

Goyle is a bridge between the original Man's Promise crew and the emerging anti-Plugg faction.

He gives the PCs a living Man's Promise witness who can oppose Plugg without necessarily forgiving the Wormwood pirates.

His worldview appears more honor-bound than sentimental.

## Pre-Capture Role

Goyle served aboard the Man's Promise before the Wormwood captured it.

Recovered context suggests he may have had a quartermaster or practical shipboard role, but this should be confirmed.

## Capture

Goyle was captured after a later grindylow attack during the Bonewrack repair period.

He was grappling with a grindylow when Mr. Plugg used his tidewater cutlass to push both Goyle and the grindylow overboard.

Goyle describes Plugg blasting them off the ship with the sword.

He calls Plugg a cowardly bastard, no pirate captain, and a man without honor.

This was framed or could be framed as battlefield confusion, but Goyle understands it as betrayal and dishonor.

## Riptide Cove

Goyle was held in the grindylow lair and rescued by Harvus and Mirei during the same event that saved Sandara and Tilly.

After Tilly feared he had been taken by the sea, Goyle surfaced coughing, angry, and pulling a tentacle from his arm.

He insisted he was no one's supper and warned there were still more creatures below.

Harvus helped him ashore.

## Information Value

Goyle overheard Plugg and/or Scourge planning to kill the PCs when they returned if the island did not kill them first.

He describes this as an ambush and says some of the PCs were considered more trouble than they were worth.

This makes him politically dangerous to Plugg.

If he speaks in front of neutral or hostile sailors, he may shift the legitimacy question from rumor to testimony.

## Post-Rescue Conversation

Goyle asks why only Harvus and Mirei came back when there had been four.

After Harvus explains that Rak and Sho went back to report food and water while Harvus and Mirei came to rescue the captives, Goyle curses and says if they were friends, pray they have Besmara's luck.

When Mirei confesses she was tasked with killing the PCs to prove loyalty, Goyle says she is as good as dead and that the other two might already be dead.

This positions Goyle as blunt, urgent, and unwilling to soften the danger.

## Relationships

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus helped rescue Goyle from Riptide Cove and says he was not about to leave anyone behind because "we are crew."

Goyle may treat this as an honor debt, life debt, or practical alliance.

He casts his lot with Harvus' group after the rescue.

### Mirei

Mirei helped in the rescue operation.

Goyle was present when Mirei confessed she had been tasked to kill the PCs and refused.

His immediate assessment is that this makes her as good as dead if Plugg's faction remains in power.

### Sandara Quinn

Goyle was rescued alongside Sandara.

He refers to her as the priestess and asks whether she survived.

### Tilly Brackett

Tilly was rescued alongside him and showed strong concern for whether Goyle survived.

Tilly says Goyle helped her through the last day of captivity.

Their relationship should be treated as meaningful, though exact nature remains open.

### Mr. Plugg

Goyle has direct cause to accuse Plugg of dishonorable or attempted murder.

Plugg's use of the tidewater cutlass to push him overboard is a critical event.

### Master Scourge

Goyle's warning implicates Scourge in the plan to kill or eliminate troublesome PCs.

### Man's Promise Survivors

Goyle can become a rally point for surviving Man's Promise crew, especially if they distrust both Plugg and the PCs.

## Current State

- Location: Bonewrack Isle, with Harvus group
- Status: Alive, recently escaped Riptide Cove
- Condition: Exhausted, coughing, angry, but able to move; exact injury state needs confirmation
- Immediate leverage: Can expose Plugg's plan and Plugg's direct role in his capture
- Current declared stance: Casts his lot with Harvus' group

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Goyle should not be treated as automatic party follower.

He has reasons to ally with the PCs against Plugg, but he may still judge them by their conduct during and after the Man's Promise battle.

If the ship's future is debated, Goyle's voice can represent:

- honor,
- shipboard legitimacy,
- survivor anger,
- and distrust of pirate opportunism.

## Possible Stances After Return

Goyle may support:

- Harvus personally, because Harvus rescued him.
- Any anti-Plugg action, because Plugg betrayed him.
- A fair council or election.
- separation of Man's Promise survivors from Wormwood pirates.
- refusal to serve under certain PCs if he blames them for the boarding battle.
