## Overview

Goyle Onórach is one of the tracked Man's Promise survivors and a former quartermaster of the Man's Promise.

At the current relaunch point, he has been rescued from Riptide Cove by Harvus and Mirei alongside Sandara Quinn and Tilly Brackett. His survival matters because he is not only another rescued prisoner. He is a witness who can testify that Plugg used the **Tidal Cutlass** to wash him overboard during a later grindylow attack and that Plugg's faction intended to eliminate troublesome PCs when they returned.

Goyle is therefore a bridge between the original Man's Promise crew and any emerging anti-Plugg faction. He can oppose Plugg without automatically forgiving the Wormwood pirates or the PCs for the original capture.

### Former Quartermaster

Sho's PCGen notes state that Goyle used to be quartermaster of the Man's Promise.

The first recovered Man's Promise night confirms this directly. When assigned to nightshift swab work with Sho, Goyle identified himself as the former quartermaster and told Sho to follow his lead.

This should now be treated as strongly confirmed table evidence. The only remaining nuance is what practical authority his quartermaster status gives him among the surviving Man's Promise crew after the capture.

### First Man's Promise Night

On the first night under Plugg, Syl assigned Sho and Goyle as swabs under Fipps Chumlett's nightshift authority.

A scarred Rahadoumi sailor asked whether Sho was Sho. Sho guessed he was Goyle. Goyle attempted something between a snarl and a smile through fresh battle scars, identified himself as the former quartermaster, and told Sho to follow his lead.

Fipps then ordered the two of them below to clean the hold from the fire.

This gives Sho and Goyle an early direct work contact before Goyle's later capture and rescue.

### Erastus 29 Nightshift

On Erastus 29 nightshift, Sho and Goyle were again close enough to speak while doing repairs.

Sho tried to start a conversation, but Jaundiced Jape glared silently at them. Sho looked toward Jape, sighed, and deferred the conversation. Goyle accepted the deferral.

This preserves a second attempted Sho / Goyle contact and shows why it did not develop: hostile surveillance made candid conversation unsafe.

### Storm / Island Separation Context

Goyle was **not** one of the two sailors taken during the Erastus 31 storm attack.

GM short-form chronology locks the Erastus 31 storm-abduction victims as:

- Sandara Quinn
- Tilly Brackett

Goyle's capture happened later, during the island period while the PCs were away from the Man's Promise.

### Later Capture

During a subsequent grindylow attack on the Man's Promise, Goyle was fighting one of the boarders.

Mr. Plugg used the **Tidal Cutlass** to wash Goyle and the boarder overboard.

Goyle describes Plugg blasting them off the ship with the sword. He calls Plugg a cowardly bastard, no pirate captain, and a man without honor.

This could be framed by Plugg's faction as battlefield confusion, but Goyle understands it as betrayal and dishonor.

This also means Harvus and Mirei know about the Tidal Cutlass' ability through Goyle's account after the rescue.

### Riptide Cove Rescue

Goyle was held in the grindylow lair and rescued by Harvus and Mirei during the same rescue operation that saved Sandara and Tilly.

After Tilly feared he had been taken by the sea, Goyle surfaced coughing, angry, and pulling a tentacle from his arm. He insisted he was no one's supper and warned there were still more creatures below.

Harvus helped him ashore.

### Information Value

Goyle overheard Plugg and/or Scourge planning to kill the PCs when they returned if the island did not kill them first.

He describes this as an ambush and says some of the PCs were considered more trouble than they were worth.

This makes him politically dangerous to Plugg. If he speaks in front of neutral or hostile sailors, he may shift the legitimacy question from rumor to testimony. His quartermaster status may increase that effect.

### Post-Rescue Conversation

Goyle asks why only Harvus and Mirei came back when there had been four.

After Harvus explains that Rak and Sho went back to report food and water while Harvus and Mirei came to rescue the captives, Goyle curses and says that if they were friends, the rescuers should pray they have Besmara's luck.

When Mirei confesses she was tasked with killing the PCs to prove loyalty, Goyle says she is as good as dead and that the other two might already be dead.

This positions Goyle as blunt, urgent, and unwilling to soften the danger.

## Relationships

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus helped rescue Goyle from Riptide Cove and says he was not about to leave anyone behind because `we are crew`.

Goyle may treat this as an honor debt, life debt, or practical alliance. He casts his lot with Harvus' group after the rescue.

### Mirei

Mirei helped in the rescue operation.

Goyle was present when Mirei confessed she had been tasked to kill the PCs and refused. His immediate assessment is that this makes her as good as dead if Plugg's faction remains in power.

Mirei also hears or learns Goyle's account of Plugg using the Tidal Cutlass to wash him overboard.

### Sandara Quinn

Goyle was rescued alongside Sandara.

He refers to her as the priestess and asks whether she survived.

### Tilly Brackett

Tilly was rescued alongside him and showed strong concern for whether Goyle survived.

Tilly says Goyle helped her through the last day. Their relationship should be treated as meaningful, though the exact nature remains open.

### Mr. Plugg

Goyle has direct cause to accuse Plugg of dishonorable attempted murder.

Plugg's use of the Tidal Cutlass to push him overboard is a critical event.

### Master Scourge

Goyle's warning implicates Scourge in the plan to kill or eliminate troublesome PCs, but the exact words and degree of Scourge's direct involvement remain source-limited.

### Sho Astor

Sho's notes preserve Goyle's former quartermaster role, and the first-night direct log confirms Goyle introduced himself to Sho as quartermaster.

Sho may therefore view Goyle as more than a generic Man's Promise survivor.

During the first night under Plugg, Sho trusted Goyle's direction when Fipps sent the two of them below to clean the burned hold.

On Erastus 29 nightshift, Sho again tried to speak with Goyle, but Jaundiced Jape's glare made her defer the conversation.

### Jaundiced Jape

Jape's silent glare during the Erastus 29 nightshift stopped Sho and Goyle from speaking more openly.

This may indicate Jape was supervising, listening, or simply near enough to make candid conversation unsafe.

### Man's Promise Survivors

Goyle is one of the six tracked Man's Promise survivors and may become a rally point for surviving Man's Promise crew, especially if they distrust both Plugg and the PCs.

His former quartermaster role may increase that function.

## Campaign Function at Relaunch

Goyle should not be treated as an automatic party follower.

He has reasons to ally with the PCs against Plugg, but he may still judge them by their conduct during and after the Man's Promise battle.

If the ship's future is debated, Goyle's voice can represent:

- honor,
- shipboard legitimacy,
- survivor anger,
- quartermaster practical authority,
- and distrust of pirate opportunism.

## Possible Stances After Return

Goyle may support:

- Harvus personally, because Harvus rescued him.
- Any anti-Plugg action, because Plugg betrayed him.
- A fair council or election.
- Separation of Man's Promise survivors from Wormwood pirates.
- Refusal to serve under certain PCs if he blames them for the boarding battle.
