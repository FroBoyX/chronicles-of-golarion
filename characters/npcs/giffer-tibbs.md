# Giffer Tibbs

## Document Status

- Type: NPC dossier
- Maturity: Draft, created after recovered Man's Promise tactical map evidence
- Scope: Wormwood crew casualty, Mirei performance connection, Man's Promise battle death
- Source basis: Recovered chat summaries, current Man's Promise battle reconstruction, Sho notes, and recovered tactical map evidence

## Overview

Giffer Tibbs was a Wormwood crew member who died during the battle for the Man's Promise.

Her death matters because she was not only an anonymous Wormwood casualty. She had appeared in earlier social context around Mirei's performances, crossed during the boarding, and then died amid the confused central-deck fight.

## Identity

- Name: Giffer Tibbs
- Type: NPC
- Origin: Wormwood crew
- Current status: Dead
- Death event: Battle of the Man's Promise, Erastus 25, 4712 AR
- Death location evidence: Below the port-side stairs on the Man's Promise tactical map

## Campaign Role

Giffer helps mark the Man's Promise battle as costly on both sides.

The Wormwood took the ship, but not cleanly. Man's Promise sailors died, and at least one Wormwood-side sailor who crossed onto the ship also died.

## Known Campaign History

### Mirei's Performances

Giffer was notably fond of Mirei's supper performances and praised her playing after warming to her.

This makes Giffer more than a casualty token; she was part of the social texture forming around the new hands before the Man's Promise battle.

### Battle of the Man's Promise

Giffer grabbed a grappling rope and asked who was going first during the boarding.

Sho said she would go first.

During the approach, Giffer was hit by arrows but made it across with Harvus.

Earlier reconstruction had uncertainty around whether Giffer died immediately during the fight or later from injuries. The recovered tactical map confirms her death location during the Man's Promise fight: below the stairs on the port side.

This should now be treated as strong placement evidence for Giffer dying during the battle itself.

## Death / Foul Play Uncertainty

Existing notes preserve concern that Rak may have directly caused or worsened Giffer's fatal condition, including a GM note that Giffer was silent because Rak had ensured she was downed and another note that Rak intended to stomp Giffer or reopen her wounds while moving past.

Sho's PCGen notes also record possible foul play with Giffer's body.

The map confirms where Giffer died, but not yet the exact death mechanism.

## Relationships

### Mirei

Giffer liked Mirei's performances and praised her playing.

If Mirei learns or remembers Giffer's death clearly, that may make the Man's Promise casualty list more personal.

### Sho Astor

Sho's notes preserve possible foul play with Giffer's body.

Sho also had command-failure guilt from the Man's Promise battle, though Giffer's death may be a different category from the Man's Promise survivor deaths because Giffer was on the Wormwood side.

### Rak Wraithraiser

Rak's exact role in Giffer's death remains unresolved.

There is concerning evidence that he may have worsened or ensured her downed state during the battle, but the final mechanism needs reconstruction.

## Campaign Function

Giffer's death can serve as a bridge between two kinds of Man's Promise cost:

- the Man's Promise crew killed by the pirate attack,
- and the Wormwood crew members consumed by the same violent machinery.

She also keeps the battle from being remembered only through PC moral guilt. The lower-crew casualties matter too.
