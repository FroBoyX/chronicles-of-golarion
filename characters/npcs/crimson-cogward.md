# Crimson "Cog" Cogward

## Document Status

- Type: NPC dossier
- Maturity: Draft, created from source extract, GM clarification, Erastus 26 revel evidence, and Man's Promise work-distance scene
- Scope: Voluntary Wormwood recruit, swab, Man's Promise / Wormwood crew member

## Overview

Crimson "Cog" Cogward is a gravely serious but devout sailor who is quick to start a fight and turns dark red when angry.

Unlike the five press-ganged new hands, Cog volunteered to join the crew of the Wormwood. Current GM clarification places him in the early Wormwood trials with the press-ganged new hands, but he should not be treated as press-ganged.

## Identity

- Name: Crimson "Cog" Cogward
- Type: NPC
- Role: Swab
- Listed locations / tags: The Wormwood, The Man's Promise
- Faith: Pharasma
- Current location: Likely Man's Promise, pending confirmation
- Current status: Presumed alive unless later evidence says otherwise

## Appearance and Bearing

Cog is a loner, prone to incredible rages and dark moods.

He wears a blue Varisian scarf tied tight around his shaven head to hide the scar left when his ear was bitten off in a bar fight.

His nickname comes from the color his neck turns when he is angry.

Cog gets pleasure from little other than combat and is quick to get into fistfights, whether because of a chance remark or simple bad luck.

## Faith

Cog is a devout follower of Pharasma.

He prays regularly, particularly after combat, and participates in all Pharasmin religious festivals if possible.

## Wormwood Entry

Cog volunteered to join the crew of the Wormwood.

He is part of the Erastus 5 crew-intake / early-trials context, but not part of the press-ganged group.

The five press-ganged new hands remain Mirei, Harvus, Rak, Sho, and Conchobhar.

## Skills / Crew Function

Cog seems to have a way of acquiring items and goods for barter and trade.

His source tag lists him as a swab.

## Combat / Morale

Cog fights like a cornered boar, caring little for his own safety.

Cog does not fight to lose. He fights to win, or dies trying.

## Campaign History

### Early Wormwood

Crimson was part of the Erastus 5 intake / trial context but volunteered rather than being press-ganged.

He watched the Day 2 lower-crew confrontation rather than taking part.

### Man's Promise Boarding Aftermath

During the immediate aftermath of the Man's Promise boarding, Grok was gathering loot from corpses and sending Crimson back to the stores with it.

This places Crimson in a practical loot-hauling / stores support role after the battle.

### Erastus 26 Revel

During the revel after the Man's Promise capture, Crimson was drunk and present around collapsed or unconscious Mirei.

When Sho challenged the group, Crimson claimed he was only checking Mirei's pulse and told Sho to bug off. Sho objected that checking a pulse did not require a hand in Mirei's robes.

Crimson later quietly walked away after rummaging through much of Mirei's clothing.

This is a negative / suspicious beat and should not be softened into harmless drunken concern unless later evidence clarifies what he was doing or taking.

Later in the same broad revel period, Crimson reacted to Harvus with initial hostility, spitting when he saw him, but then brightened at the idea of battle stories. He offered to trade stories with Harvus at the celebration and said they were all brothers tonight.

This leaves Crimson socially complicated: he can be predatory or opportunistic in a drunken crowd, but also responds to battle-story camaraderie.

### Man's Promise Work Distance

During an unknown work day aboard the Man's Promise, Harvus attempted idle chit-chat with Tam and Crimson.

Crimson kept distance and framed the Man's Promise as a dangerous new start:

> "I really don't want trouble, this is a new start... you're not exactly popular."

When Mirei later tried friendly conversation during work, Crimson rejected the risk more directly:

> "YOU are not worth the risk."

Mirei suggested he might feel that way because Sho was outspoken. Crimson answered that Sho was more like stupid.

He clarified that he did not have anything against Mirei personally, but he did not want anything to do with her.

This scene is important because Crimson's distance is not simple hatred. It is self-protection. He sees association with the PCs as risky under the Man's Promise regime.

## Relationships

### Sho Astor

Sho confronted Crimson during the Erastus 26 revel because he was crowding / handling Mirei while claiming to check her pulse.

This may become relevant if Sho later remembers or pursues what happened to Mirei during the party.

During the later Man's Promise work-distance scene, Crimson called Sho stupid because of her outspokenness. This reinforces that Crimson views Sho's defiance as dangerous to those around her.

### Mirei

Crimson was part of the dangerous drunken crowd around collapsed Mirei during the Erastus 26 revel and appeared to rummage through her clothing before walking away.

The exact intent and whether anything was taken remain open.

During a later Man's Promise work scene, Crimson told Mirei that he had nothing against her, but she was not worth the risk and he wanted nothing to do with her.

### Harvus G'ron

Crimson spat on seeing Harvus during the post-battle revel, then offered to trade battle stories and said everyone was brothers tonight.

During the Man's Promise work-distance scene, Crimson told Harvus he did not want trouble, that this was a new start, and that Harvus was not exactly popular.

This suggests Harvus' battlefield presence may have opened a temporary social route with Crimson during the revel, but not enough to make Crimson risk association under Plugg's prize-crew regime.

### Cut-Throat Grok

Grok sent Crimson back to the stores with looted material after the Man's Promise battle.
