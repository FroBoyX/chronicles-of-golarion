# Barnabas Harrigan

## Document Status

- Type: NPC dossier
- Maturity: Draft, seeded with Erastus 16 reefclaw reward scene
- Scope: Wormwood captain, strategic antagonist, reward-and-terror authority model
- Source basis: AP scaffold, recovered table evidence, current campaign reconstruction

## Overview

Captain Barnabas Harrigan is the captain of the Wormwood and the strategic antagonist behind the early campaign.

He is cruel, powerful, and dangerous, but not random. He rewards competence and punishes weakness or defiance when it suits his authority.

The Erastus 16 reefclaw dinner scene gives a direct recovered example of this: Harrigan rewards Rak for bringing him good food, even while Plugg and Scourge dislike Rak.

## Identity

- Name: Barnabas Harrigan
- Type: Pirate captain
- Vessel: Wormwood
- Role: Captain and ultimate authority over Plugg, Scourge, and the press-ganged crew during the Wormwood period
- Campaign status: Alive

## Campaign Role

Harrigan is not the immediate bully in most early scenes. That role belongs to Scourge, with Plugg as political officer.

Harrigan's importance is larger: he defines the order of the Wormwood and demonstrates what a successful Free Captain's power looks like when filtered through fear, reward, violence, and appetite.

## Erastus 16 Reefclaw Reward

On Erastus 16, Plugg sent Harvus, Rak, Sho, Mirei, and Badger to a reef to gather crabs for Harrigan's supper.

Rak returned with the notable reefclaw / "big one." After the cooking, Caulky Tarroon brought Rak before Harrigan as the source of dinner.

Harrigan rewarded Rak directly by instructing Grok to return anything taken from Rak from ship stores.

> "I smile on those who go above and beyond!"

Harrigan also told Rak that doing right by him could bring money and infamy.

This scene matters because it establishes Harrigan's reward logic on-screen. He can be openly approving of useful performance while remaining a dangerous captain whose approval does not make him safe.

## Relationships

### Mr. Plugg

Plugg serves under Harrigan and benefits from Harrigan's authority.

Plugg's later ambition aboard the Man's Promise should be measured against Harrigan's more established command presence.

### Master Scourge

Scourge operates under the protection of Harrigan's shipboard order, even when Harrigan is not personally present.

### Rak Wraithraiser

Harrigan rewarded Rak for catching the big reefclaw / source of dinner and ordered Grok to return his confiscated gear.

This gives Rak a direct example of Harrigan rewarding useful, above-and-beyond service.

### Ambrose "Fishguts" Kroop

Kroop's life belongs to Harrigan because of a lost card game.

Harrigan keeps Kroop because Kroop remains useful as cook.

### Caulky Tarroon

Caulky serves close enough to Harrigan to bring dinner and introduce Rak as the source of the reefclaw meal.

### Riaris Krine

Riaris was present at the reefclaw dinner and judged Rak's catch good enough.

### Peppery Longfarthing

Peppery was present or nearby during the reefclaw dinner scene and remarked on Rak's strangeness.

## Campaign Function

Harrigan's function is to show the PCs that pirate command can be effective without being moral.

He is a model of power that can reward useful behavior and still be part of the system the PCs may ultimately reject.
