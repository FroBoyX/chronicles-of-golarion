## Overview

Ambrose "Fishguts" Kroop is the Wormwood's cook and technically one of the ship's officers, though he has far more in common with the press-ganged sailors than with the cruel officers who command them.

He is a broken but fundamentally kind man: drunk, filthy, cynical, and ashamed, yet still capable of loyalty, practical wisdom, and real courage when protecting friends.

In the current campaign, Kroop is one of the most important potential stabilizers after the Man's Promise crisis because he understands both shipboard survival and Shackles culture.

## Appearance

Kroop is a large, heavyset man over 200 pounds and only about 5 feet tall.

He gives off an air of neglect and despair, with bushy tufts of black hair, rotting teeth, and a filthy apron.

He walks with a wallowing waddle when drunk, but he is stronger than he appears.

His back is so scarred by lashes that he claims he barely feels the bite anymore.

## Baseline Adventure Path Role

Kroop begins as a washed-up drunk who dreams of a better life but believes himself trapped.

He is a potential friend and ally of the PCs, especially because Rak becomes his cook's mate.

His role is to introduce the possibility of mutiny, provide useful knowledge, and serve as a voice of reason alongside Sandara Quinn.

If befriended, Kroop becomes a long-term ally. If betrayed after tasting hope, he can become bitterly hostile.

## Campaign Role

Kroop represents the possibility of recovery.

He is not heroic in the abstract. He is tired, compromised, and often drunk. But he knows what a better life might look like and can recognize it when the PCs begin to create it.

He is also one of the few figures who can help translate raw freedom into practical shipboard survival: food, crew routines, ports, rumors, personalities, and the lived culture of the Shackles.

For the island relaunch, Kroop is important because he can help answer the question: **what does the crew do now if Plugg falls?**

## Motivation

Kroop wants escape, but fear and despair have made him cautious.

He loathes Harrigan and the Wormwood's cruelty, but he is not naturally reckless.

Friendship with the PCs can restore his sense of purpose.

Once he believes in them, he may be willing to risk himself for them.

## Psychology

Kroop is cynical because hope has repeatedly hurt him.

His drinking is not merely indulgence; it is resignation.

Still, he has not become cruel. His instinct remains protective, especially toward his cook's mate and those who show him genuine kindness.

He can be brave when protecting friends, but he prefers survival, quiet manipulation, and waiting for the right moment over open defiance.

## History

### Before the Wormwood

Kroop earned the nickname "Fishguts" not from bad cooking, but from competitive eating in Port Peril's low bars.

He was once trained as a professional chef at the Lobster's Armor, one of Port Peril's better restaurants.

After being dismissed, he worked aboard various Shackles vessels before joining the Wormwood.

### Wormwood Period

Kroop served as ship's cook aboard the Wormwood for roughly three years.

Two years before the campaign, he drunkenly bet his life against Captain Harrigan in a card game and lost.

Since then, his life has effectively belonged to Harrigan.

Harrigan despises him, but keeps him alive because Kroop remains a better cook than anyone else aboard.

### Relationship With Rak

Rak Wraithraiser was assigned as Kroop's cook's mate after the initial Wormwood tests.

This made Kroop one of Rak's earliest daily contacts aboard ship.

### Man's Promise Period

Kroop was moved to the Man's Promise with the prize crew.

Away from the sour atmosphere of the Wormwood, Kroop's spirits appear capable of improving.

He also retained or secured a small barrel of rum from Cut-Throat Grok before leaving the Wormwood.

## Relationships

### Rak Wraithraiser

Rak is Kroop's cook's mate.

This relationship is one of the clearest practical bridges between a PC and the lower workings of ship life.

Kroop may protect Rak from mistakes or punishments when he can.

### Cut-Throat Grok

Grok is Kroop's best friend aboard the Wormwood.

She encourages his taste for rum, but their friendship is genuine and important.

### Sandara Quinn

Kroop and Sandara both function as potential voices of reason and mutiny-sympathetic allies.

Both understand that open action too early can get people killed.

### Captain Harrigan

Harrigan owns Kroop's life by virtue of the lost card game.

Kroop loathes the captain and longs for escape from him.

### Mr. Plugg

Plugg is part of the oppressive officer structure Kroop wants to escape.

Kroop is likely to oppose Plugg if he believes the PCs have a real chance.

## Knowledge and Usefulness

Kroop knows the Shackles well.

He can provide:

- regional knowledge,
- sailor gossip,
- pirate custom,
- shipboard routine,
- food and supply sense,
- contacts and rumor context,
- and practical advice about mutiny risk.

He is not a commander, but he is one of the most useful surviving adults in the room if the Man's Promise loses its officers.
