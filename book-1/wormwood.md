# The Wormwood

## Document Status

- Type: Book One location and social-state document
- Maturity: Draft, expanded with Erastus 17-25 gap dates
- Scope: The Wormwood from press-ganging through capture of the Man's Promise

## Overview

The Wormwood is where the campaign teaches captivity, hierarchy, and pirate survival.

It is not a dungeon. It is a moving social machine.

The ship's purpose in Book One is to force the PCs to learn that survival at sea depends on labor, discipline, reputation, allies, timing, and restraint.

## Campaign Function

The Wormwood is Act I: Captivity.

The central question is:

> Can the PCs survive long enough to matter?

This question is answered through relationships, not combat.

## Physical Ship

The Wormwood is a three-masted pirate ship commanded by Captain Barnabas Harrigan.

Key spaces:

- Main deck, where work, punishment, and public authority occur.
- Poop deck, where Harrigan and Plugg display command.
- Officers' quarters, dangerous and trapped.
- Captain's cabin, inaccessible and lethal to low-ranking sailors.
- Quartermaster's store, controlled by Cut-Throat Grok.
- Galley, controlled by Ambrose Kroop.
- Crew berths, where politics and fatigue accumulate.
- Bilges, where punishment, danger, and secret violence occur.

## Authority Structure

### Captain Barnabas Harrigan

Harrigan is the unreachable strategic authority.

His presence establishes scale: the PCs are not meant to defeat him. They are meant to survive his ship.

### Mr. Plugg

Plugg is the first mate and immediate political authority.

He assigns jobs, watches for failure, and turns the daily routine into a pressure system.

### Master Scourge

Scourge is the boatswain and master-at-arms.

He is the face of discipline, humiliation, and cruelty.

### Cut-Throat Grok

Grok controls gear, barter, and store access.

She can be dangerous or helpful depending on trust.

### Ambrose "Fishguts" Kroop

Kroop controls the galley and becomes a practical ally through the cook's mate role.

He is broken by the Wormwood but not without kindness or knowledge.

## Daily Structure

The Wormwood's rhythm matters.

A normal day includes:

1. Dawn bell.
2. Work assignment.
3. Hard labor under supervision.
4. Small opportunities to influence, sneak, shop, or recover gear.
5. Bloody Hour.
6. Evening meal.
7. Rum ration.
8. Gambling, entertainment, social maneuvering, or sleep.

This repetition teaches the players what oppression feels like in practical terms.

The PCs do not hate Plugg and Scourge because of a single scene. They hate them because they live under them.

## Shipboard Culture

The Wormwood introduces the campaign's pirate culture:

- punishment is public,
- work is survival,
- rum is control,
- theft is normal but getting caught matters,
- reputation affects treatment,
- allies can save lives,
- enemies become future battlefield math,
- officers are not safe to challenge early.

## Campaign-Specific PC Entrances

All PCs were taken from or through the Formidably Maid in Port Peril.

This was a tavern or drinking establishment, not a ship.

### Harvus G'ron

Harvus was baited after being insulted as a "hawk that can talk." He was drawn out, ambushed, and taken. Aretta delivered a disabling blow, and Scourge later twisted or damaged Harvus' wing.

This makes Scourge personal for Harvus.

### Rak Wraithraiser

Rak was caught in a blur of drink, flirtation, and bad timing. He woke aboard the Wormwood and eventually became Kroop's cook's mate.

Rak developed a personal relationship with Grok before the Man's Promise split.

### Sho Astor

Sho was drugged after pursuing a map lead in Port Peril.

She became one of the most openly defiant PCs aboard ship and drew repeated attention from Plugg and Scourge.

### Mirei

Mirei was also taken from the Formidably Maid and had her harp seized.

Sandara helped recover the harp through Grok, establishing one of Mirei's early trust links.

## Major Campaign Beats

### Press-Ganged Awakening

The PCs wake aboard the Wormwood robbed of most equipment and forced into service.

This establishes captivity and removes normal adventuring agency.

### Jakes Magpie's Keelhauling

The execution of Jakes Magpie demonstrates the stakes of theft, disobedience, and Harrigan's shipboard order.

### Scourge's Harassment Party

Aretta, Fipps, Jape, and Slippery Syl try to delay and beat the new sailors under Scourge's encouragement.

This creates early enemy lines and gives the PCs their first taste of lower-crew politics.

### Sandara's Early Aid

Sandara returns important gear, helps recover Mirei's harp, and teaches the principle that friends matter aboard ship.

### Bilge Rats

The bilge rat incident gives the PCs an early shared danger belowdecks and an opportunity to scrounge.

### Owlbear Fight

Plugg brings Owlbear Hartshorn out as sport.

Sho defeats him but refuses to kill him, establishing a moral contrast between performance cruelty and controlled strength.

### Storm and Rosie

During the storm, Rosie Cusswell is lost at sea despite rescue efforts.

This is one of the first major losses aboard the Wormwood and should not be treated as a generic hazard.

### Reef / Crab Mission

On Erastus 16, the PCs and Badger are sent to collect crabs and face reefclaws.

Rak catches the notable reefclaw / dinner source and is rewarded by Harrigan, who orders Grok to return anything taken from Rak from ship stores.

This scene builds competence and gives Harrigan or the officers reason to notice them.

### Erastus 17-18 Heatwave

After the reef task, a heatwave hits the Wormwood and refuses to let up.

The boiling sun adds environmental pressure after the storm and reef danger.

### Erastus 19 Boarding Drills

When the weather lets up, Harrigan assigns Riaris Krine to put everyone through boarding drills in rotations.

This begins the direct combat preparation that leads toward the Man's Promise.

### Erastus 20-21 Normal Work Days

The next two days are currently reconstructed as normal work days.

No direct chat logs are currently recovered for these dates.

### Erastus 22 Plugg Watches Sho

Plugg takes notice of Sho's defiance and begins watching who she interacts with.

He also takes it as his personal task to ensure defiance is corrected.

### Erastus 23 Bilge Murder Attempt / Aretta Death

On Erastus 23, Sho is assigned to the bilges with Aretta and Fipps.

The working understanding is that they are there with orders to make sure she cannot continue being an issue.

The confrontation leads to Aretta's death.

This is a major escalation: a hostile lower-crew enemy dies because of PC conflict before the Man's Promise battle.

### Erastus 24 Hot-Box and Sails on the Horizon

Sho is in the hot-box to be keelhauled at Bloody Hour.

In the late afternoon, the Man's Promise is sighted and the ship begins preparations for piracy.

### Erastus 25 Battle for the Man's Promise

In the early morning, the Wormwood overtakes the Man's Promise.

All hands, including Sho, take part in a bloody and violent battle for control of the ship.

This battle ends the Wormwood captivity act and creates the conditions for Plugg's prize-crew crisis.

## Relationship Development

### Harvus and Sandara

Harvus develops courtly affection and devotion toward Sandara.

This thread becomes central on Bonewrack Isle.

### Rak and Grok

Rak and Grok first hook up on Erastus 16 after the reefclaw reward sends Rak to Grok's store for gear return.

Rak later begins bunking with her, and they share a sentimental goodbye when he is transferred to the prize crew.

### Sho and Harrigan / Peppery

Sho's defiance and competence draw attention from higher authority.

Her warning to Harrigan during the boarding battle later earns recognition and reward.

### Mirei and Sandara

Sandara's help recovering Mirei's harp becomes one of Mirei's earliest support relationships.

## Campaign Divergence From AP

The campaign did not substantially rewrite the Wormwood structure.

It deepened it.

The published framework provided:

- daily routine,
- NPC influence,
- punishments,
- social danger,
- and eventual boarding.

The campaign added:

- PC-specific abduction truths,
- Harvus' wing injury and Scourge vengeance,
- Rak/Grok intimacy,
- Sho's distinct moral defiance,
- Mirei's harp and later vulnerability to Plugg's attention,
- and richer NPC emotional continuity.

## Current Relevance

Although the current campaign is no longer aboard the Wormwood, the ship remains relevant because:

- Harrigan is still alive.
- Grok remains aboard and matters to Rak.
- Harrigan will likely react to any theft, mutiny, or loss of the Man's Promise.
- The Wormwood contains unresolved NPCs and future hooks.
- The culture learned aboard the Wormwood shapes what the PCs think command means.

## Do Not Flatten

Do not reduce the Wormwood to "the place where the PCs were press-ganged."

The Wormwood is the pressure-cooker that made the later decisions meaningful.

Without the Wormwood, the mutiny is only a fight.
