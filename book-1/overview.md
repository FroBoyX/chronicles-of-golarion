# Book One Overview

## Document Status

- Type: Book framework
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Opening arc of the revived Skulls & Shackles campaign
- Canon stance: Campaign continuity takes precedence over published adventure structure

## Working Title

The published adventure is **The Wormwood Mutiny**.

For Chronicles of Golarion, the opening arc is better understood as the campaign's transition from captivity to self-determination.

## Core Question

Book One is not ultimately about whether the PCs defeat Mr. Plugg or Master Scourge.

The central question is:

> When coercive authority collapses, who—if anyone—has the legitimacy to command the ship, and what kind of crew chooses to remain?

## Social Acts

The published adventure is divided geographically into the Wormwood, the Man's Promise, and Bonewrack Isle. The campaign is more usefully understood in three social acts.

### Act I — Captivity

**Location:** The Wormwood

**Question:** Can the PCs survive?

The PCs awaken as press-ganged sailors aboard the Wormwood. Their survival depends less on combat and more on learning shipboard culture, earning allies, avoiding fatal punishments, and understanding the ship's hierarchy.

The Wormwood teaches the rules of pirate life under coercive authority.

### Act II — Fracture

**Location:** The Man's Promise and Bonewrack Isle

**Question:** Who can be trusted?

Harrigan leaves. Plugg claims command. The crew divides physically and politically. The PCs and NPCs split into separate scenes, and personal loyalties become more important than formal assignments.

This act contains the course change, the island wreck, Sho's captivity, Mirei's compromise, Rak's leadership under pressure, and Harvus' rescue of Sandara.

### Act III — Identity

**Location:** Bonewrack fork, Man's Promise, and whatever follows

**Question:** Who are we now?

The campaign reaches its true climax after the island, not simply after a fight.

Plugg and Scourge may live or die. The PCs may take the ship or fail to. The crew may unify or fracture. The Man's Promise may become a pirate vessel, a merchant vessel, a prize, a refuge, or an abandoned wreck.

The first free decision is the climax.

## Published Structure

### Part 1 — The Wormwood

The PCs wake in the hold of the Wormwood and are forced into pirate life.

Major functions:

- Introduce Harrigan, Plugg, Scourge, Sandara, Kroop, Grok, and the lower crew.
- Teach the daily structure of pirate life.
- Establish punishment, rum, shipboard jobs, and influence.
- Turn the crew from scenery into a political field.
- Build hatred of Plugg and Scourge without allowing early resolution.

### Part 2 — The Man's Promise

The Wormwood captures a merchant vessel and assigns a prize crew under Plugg.

Major functions:

- Give the PCs their first act of piracy.
- Split the original crew.
- Remove Harrigan from immediate authority.
- Let Plugg attempt actual command.
- Seed mutiny, course change, and Rickety's Squibs.

### Part 3 — Bonewrack Isle

The Man's Promise wrecks on Bonewrack Isle. The PCs must find water, supplies, and missing crew.

Major functions:

- Force island survival.
- Expose Plugg's willingness to abandon or eliminate inconvenient people.
- Provide the chance to rescue Sandara and other captives.
- Transform the conflict from resentment into open political crisis.

## Campaign Divergence

The campaign remains close to the published structure through most of the Wormwood and Man's Promise phases.

The major divergence is not a rewritten plot but an emergent one.

Bonewrack Isle became a character-driven climax with multiple simultaneous fronts:

- Harvus and Mirei return to Riptide Cove and rescue Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle.
- Harvus kills the Whale in a legendary one-on-one moment that routs the Brinebrood Queen.
- Rak guides a hostile recovery party across the island.
- Sho wakes in the hot-box aboard the Man's Promise and begins speaking with Kahina.
- Mirei's betrayal order becomes known before it is acted upon.
- The crew's future becomes unresolved rather than assumed.

## Current End State

The campaign is paused at the fork on Bonewrack Isle and aboard the Man's Promise.

### Bonewrack — Harvus Group

- Harvus G'ron
- Mirei
- Sandara Quinn
- Tilly Brackett
- Goyle Onórach

This group is returning from the successful rescue at Riptide Cove.

### Bonewrack — Rak Group

- Rak Wraithraiser
- Shivikah
- Slippery Syl Lonegan
- Jaundiced Jape

This group is moving through the island toward supplies and the known route.

### Man's Promise — Sho

- Sho Astor
- Kahina Bukekayo nearby or recently guarding her

Sho is imprisoned in the hot-box aboard the Man's Promise and has opened dialogue with Kahina.

## Primary Decision Space

The relaunch should preserve the following possibilities:

- The PCs take the Man's Promise.
- Plugg retains command.
- Plugg or Scourge survive and become hooks.
- The crew fractures and the ship becomes impossible to hold.
- The PCs decline command or leave the ship.
- An NPC is chosen or emerges as captain.
- Leadership is decided by force, election, custom, bargaining, or immediate necessity.
- The ship is abandoned, claimed later, or transformed into a different kind of vessel.

## Important Principle

The PCs are not automatically a party.

They were press-ganged together. That creates shared trauma and shared opportunity, but not automatic loyalty.

Book One ends when the survivors choose what they are—not when an encounter is won.
