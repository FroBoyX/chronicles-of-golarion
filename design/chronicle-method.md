# Chronicle Method

The Chronicle Method is the working archival method for **Chronicles of Golarion**.

It treats the campaign as a historical record supported by evidence, corrected by GM truth, and maintained through version control.

## Core Principles

### GM Truth Is Final

The GM/product owner decides canon.

Evidence informs the decision, but does not replace it.

### Evidence Is Preserved Separately From Synthesis

Raw logs, pages, journals, and notes are evidence.

Chronology, dossiers, and current-state files are synthesis.

### Character Perspective Matters

Player journals and in-character writing preserve what characters thought, felt, and believed.

They should not be flattened into neutral summary and discarded.

### Objective History Matters Too

The archive must distinguish what happened from what a character believed happened.

Both are valuable.

### Contradictions Are Productive

Contradictions reveal missing context, perspective bias, or later table drift.

They should be recorded until resolved.

### Git Is the Archive Spine

Version control allows the archive to grow without losing the history of how the record developed.

## Archive Flow

```text
Recovered Evidence
        ↓
Evidence Classification
        ↓
Canon Reconciliation
        ↓
Entity and Timeline Updates
        ↓
Derived Player / GM Material
```

## Document Classes

- **Chronology:** What happened and when.
- **Current State:** What is true at the present campaign moment.
- **Entity Dossier:** A stable record for one character, ship, location, faction, or object.
- **Evidence Record:** Raw or lightly indexed source material.
- **Design Note:** Archive process and meta-structure.

## Relaunch Priority

The immediate archive goal is to support a playable campaign relaunch at the paused Book 1 finale state.

Perfect reconstruction is less important than reliable playable continuity.

The archive should therefore prioritize:

1. current locations,
2. current character state,
3. immediate threats,
4. relationship context,
5. unresolved secrets,
6. cold-open usability.
