# Diseases

## Document Status

- Type: Campaign rules reference
- Category: Health and Damage
- Status: Seeded from recovered campaign rule page

## Purpose

This campaign uses progression tracks for diseases rather than relying only on default Pathfinder disease effects.

The intent is to make diseases progress more believably and to prevent simple ability-score damage from being the only meaningful disease model.

## Core Procedure

When a victim fails the initial save against a disease, they enter the first disease track state: **Latent/Carrier**.

This replaces the disease's normal effects unless the GM decides a disease has additional symptoms or starts further along the track.

Effects from the track are cumulative.

When the victim fails a disease save, they move one step farther down the track.

When they satisfy the disease's cure condition, usually by succeeding on one or more saves, they move one step back toward healthy.

Once they reach **Healthy**, they are cured.

## End States

Most disease tracks default to **Dead** as the final end state.

Some diseases may have less severe end states at GM discretion.

Once a disease reaches its end state, the victim keeps the current effects, stops progressing, and can no longer attempt ordinary saving throws to recover.

At that point, only powerful magic such as **miracle** or **wish** may remove the final effects.

## Magic

Usually:

- **Remove disease** immediately moves the victim to Healthy.
- **Heal** also works against diseases.

If a disease has reached its end state, ordinary remove disease is no longer enough unless the GM rules otherwise.

## Condition Immunities

Some disease-track states mimic conditions such as sickened, exhausted, or comatose.

Immunity to those ordinary conditions does not automatically prevent the disease-track effect.

Only effects and immunities that apply to disease apply unless the GM rules otherwise.

## Physical Disease Track

```text
Healthy → Latent/Carrier → Weakened → Impaired → Disabled → Bedridden → Comatose → Dead
```

### Healthy

The character is not affected by the disease.

### Latent/Carrier

The character has the disease and may pass it on if contagious, but suffers no ill effects.

### Weakened

The character suffers the effects of both **sickened** and **fatigued**.

### Impaired

The character also suffers the effects of **exhausted**.

Whenever they take a standard action, they must succeed at a Fortitude save against the disease DC or lose the action and become **nauseated** for 1 minute.

### Disabled

The character gains the **disabled** condition.

If they take a standard action, their hit points drop by 1 or to -1, whichever is worse.

### Bedridden

The character is awake and can converse, but cannot stand on their own or take standard or move actions.

### Comatose

The character is unconscious and feverish.

They cannot be woken while they remain in this disease-track state.

### Dead

The disease overcomes the body and the character dies.

The corpse may still be contagious, and some diseases may have unusual postmortem effects.

## Mental Disease Track

```text
Healthy → Latent/Carrier → Weakened → Impaired → Befuddled → Deranged → Comatose → Dead
```

### Healthy

The character is not affected by the disease.

### Latent/Carrier

The character has the disease and may pass it on if contagious, but suffers no ill effects.

### Weakened

The character suffers the effects of **shaken**.

The DCs of their spells and spell-like abilities decrease by 2.

If they are a spellcaster, they can no longer cast their highest level of spells.

### Impaired

The character no longer adds mental ability score modifiers to uses per day of pools, class abilities, or bonus spells.

Their DCs decrease by an additional 2.

If they are a spellcaster, they can no longer cast their two highest levels of spells.

### Befuddled

The character is losing their grasp on thought, reality, and self.

Each round, they have a 50% chance to take no relevant action, instead babbling, wandering, or speaking to unseen things.

### Deranged

The character is almost entirely disconnected from reality.

Their mind filters and twists external stimuli into strange forms.

### Comatose

The character has lost all grip on reality and entered an inner world of dreams.

They cannot be woken while they remain in this disease-track state.

### Dead

The disease has harmed the character's brain beyond repair, killing them.

The corpse may still be contagious, and some diseases may have unusual postmortem effects.
