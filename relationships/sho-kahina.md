# Sho Astor and Kahina Bukekayo

## Document Status

- Type: Relationship record
- Maturity: Draft, updated with Sho player-memory answer on repentance, judgment, and blood limits
- Scope: Arodus 5 hot-box confrontation aboard the Man's Promise

## Summary

Sho Astor and Kahina Bukekayo are currently tied by a prison-side moral bargain.

Kahina does not forgive Sho for the Man's Promise dead.

Sho expresses remorse and asks for help against Plugg and Scourge.

Kahina's response is not trust. It is a test:

> Their lives, or Sho's.

Sho's player-memory answer clarifies that Sho experiences Kahina as both judge and mirror: judge of what Sho directly participated in or failed to prevent aboard the Man's Promise, and mirror of Sho's own path into the Wormwood through coercion and violence.

## Scene Context

Sho is locked in the hot-box aboard the Man's Promise after being beaten unconscious by Scourge's shore party.

Kahina is a Man's Promise survivor and guard.

The ship is moving or preparing to move toward the far shore for supplies, destination unknown afterward.

When Sho wakes or speaks from captivity, she is focused and planning rather than broken or passive. She is angry that she did not get to strike Scourge, but Kahina's look makes her suspect something deeper is happening and that she needs to be ready.

## Kahina's Accusation

Kahina accuses Sho of hypocrisy when Sho asks why Kahina would blindly follow those in command of a massacre.

Kahina asks what Sho was doing when she attacked the Man's Promise and how long Sho blindly followed those same men before being chained.

Kahina says she saw Sho lead a group onto the ship and that more than thirty people died.

Sho does not like the accusation, but from Sho's side she feels it is fair, even though the circumstances surrounding the battle were anything but fair.

This accusation helps set Sho on a path of redemption.

## Sho's Remorse

Sho says she regrets what happened, does not expect Kahina to believe her, and cannot make it right.

Sho says she wants to live, escape, and kill the people in charge.

She asks Kahina to help her and says that if they succeed, Kahina can decide what to do with Sho afterward.

Sho's player-memory answer clarifies that Sho does not think killing Plugg or Scourge will undo what has already been done. She believes it can stop them from doing more harm, free those still shackled under their influence, and serve punishment for their deeds.

Sho does not feel she deserves forgiveness. She seeks trust, cooperation, and a chance to act and do right moving forward.

## Kahina's Test

Kahina says Sho's words mean nothing.

But if Sho truly repents, Kahina asks whether Sho would kill for it, whether she would kill those who torment them, and whether she would lose her life to end theirs.

Sho says she will do everything in her power.

Kahina rejects that phrasing and narrows the test:

> Their lives, or Sho's.

If Sho hesitates, Kahina will ensure Sho dies for her sins.

Sho accepts.

Sho's acceptance is not only strategy. It is a mixture of justice, rage, repentance, and survival. Survival remains in the back of Sho's mind because she still has a promise to keep with Agatha.

## Limits Around Blood

Sho's willingness to shed blood depends on whose blood Kahina or the situation demands.

A random crewmember would cause Sho to hesitate.

Mirei or Sandara would cause serious hesitation. Harvus would cause some hesitation, though Sho would understand the moral complication more than she would for an uninvolved crewmember.

Rak would cause little to no hesitation from Sho at this point.

Plugg, Scourge, and their lackeys would also cause little to no hesitation.

Do not write Sho as indiscriminately willing to kill. Her line is target-specific and tied to culpability, threat, and the chance to stop further harm.

## Relationship State

This is not friendship.

This is not alliance.

It is conditional, hostile cooperation under moral threat.

Kahina may help Sho if Sho proves herself, but Kahina has made clear that she reserves the right to kill Sho if Sho fails the test.

Sho is not seeking forgiveness from Kahina. She is seeking enough trust and cooperation to act, and a chance to do right moving forward.

## Campaign Function

This relationship gives the ship-side scene real stakes before the island party returns.

Sho's captivity is not passive.

She now has:

- a possible route into Man's Promise survivor politics,
- a demand for action,
- a direct threat if she hesitates,
- a moral mirror for the deaths caused during the boarding,
- and a repentance path that must become action rather than words.
