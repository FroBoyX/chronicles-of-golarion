# Sho, Rak, and Harvus — Man's Promise Command Fracture

## Document Status

- Type: Relationship / conflict record
- Maturity: Draft, seeded from Sho player answers and Man's Promise battle reconstruction
- Scope: Sho's failed command, broken surrender promise, distrust of Rak and Harvus, and implications for later leadership
- Source basis: Sho player answers relayed by GM; recovered Man's Promise battle reconstruction
- Spoiler scope: Character-private / GM-facing until revealed in play

## Summary

The Man's Promise battle created a serious fracture between Sho Astor and the other PCs she was supposed to coordinate.

Sho believed she could reduce the bloodshed by promising the Man's Promise defenders that surrender would prevent further harm. When Rak and Harvus continued or caused violence despite that effort, Sho experienced it as a failure of command and as a broken promise made in her name.

Despite working with Rak and Harvus, Sho does **not** trust either of them after this battle.

## Sho's Command State

Sho felt confused and trapped by being given a command / strike-team role.

She suspected the role might be another impossible task designed for her to fail, allowing the officers to justify killing her.

Once in the fight, her working assumption was simple: if the ship was taken and the crew surrendered, no further harm needed to happen.

Her promise to the defenders was sincere.

## Rak

Sho blames Rak for direct atrocities during the battle.

The recovered battle log already shows Rak killing a pleading or downed sailor and Sho objecting.

Sho's later player memory also emphasizes the lifeboat incident: Man's Promise crew ran for a lifeboat, and Rak hit the switch / release before it was ready, causing it to crash into another lifeboat and potentially killing or injuring people.

The exact casualties are unresolved, but Sho's character truth is that Rak's actions helped turn her attempted surrender / containment into a disaster.

## Harvus

Sho also blames Harvus.

Her player memory preserves an infamous moment in which Sho demanded that a crew member put down their weapon and promised no harm would come, only for Harvus to smash another crew member with a flail who was not even looking to attack.

The exact target state remains unresolved, but from Sho's perspective this directly undermined her authority and her promise.

## Responsibility and Guilt

Sho distributes blame in three directions:

- Rak and Harvus for their direct actions,
- Harrigan for attacking the Man's Promise in the first place,
- and herself for not doing more to stop it.

This is a major reason she feels guilt for the Man's Promise dead.

Sho did not like the attack, but believed that if she convinced defenders to surrender, she could stop most of the bloodshed. When that failed catastrophically, she took much of the guilt onto herself.

## Leadership Consequence

The battle taught Sho that command without power to enforce consequences is not enough.

If she is going to take charge of anyone, she believes she must become powerful enough to hold people to their actions.

This matters for any later leadership question. Sho's problem is not only whether she wants command. It is whether she believes she can actually make command mean something.
