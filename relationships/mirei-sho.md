# Mirei and Sho Astor

## Document Status

- Type: Relationship record
- Maturity: Draft, seeded from recovered disguise-discovery whispers and Sho player memory
- Scope: Early Wormwood suspicion, disguise truth, and later trust/confiding thread

## Summary

Mirei was the first recovered character to notice that Sho Astor was not what she appeared to be aboard the Wormwood.

On Erastus 7 / Wormwood Day 3, Mirei noticed through deception / Bluff context that something about Sho was off. By the following night, the GM clarified that Mirei had noticed the previous day that Sho was actually a woman.

Sho later remembered Mirei as the person who noticed early and became someone Sho confided in.

## Discovery Sequence

### Initial Suspicion

Recovered GM whispers show that Mirei sensed something was wrong with Sho's presentation.

> "You sensed that something isn't quite what it appears with Chas"

The GM asked whether Mirei wanted to hide that she noticed.

Mirei initially chose not to hide the suspicion:

> "no, I dont want to hide. Already on the path to asking him what the f is up"

### Exact Realization

The next night, Mirei asked whether she got a new check while talking with Sho during dinner, or whether the prior check still stood.

The GM clarified:

> "you noticed that chas is actually a woman.... are you trying to hide that you noticed. YESTERDAY."

The GM further said:

> "she is lying to you, clearly."

Mirei then chose to hide that she had figured out the exact truth:

> "Yes, I want to hide that I figured exactly out what was up. Chas knows I am suspicious at this point."

## Relationship Meaning

This creates a layered early trust problem:

- Mirei notices that Sho is hiding something.
- Sho knows Mirei is suspicious.
- Mirei eventually knows the exact truth but chooses to hide that she knows.
- Sho later confides in Mirei, making Mirei one of the earliest keepers of Sho's secret.

This should not be treated as a simple reveal. It is a slow recognition / suspicion / concealment / trust sequence.

## Knowledge Boundaries

- Mirei knows early that Sho's male presentation is false.
- Sho knows Mirei is suspicious before the exact knowledge is openly resolved.
- It is not yet confirmed exactly when Sho openly confided in Mirei.
- It is not yet confirmed whether anyone else noticed before or after Mirei.

## Campaign Function

This relationship matters because both characters are navigating secrecy under coercive shipboard power.

Sho's disguise is a survival boundary.

Mirei's knowledge of the disguise makes her dangerous to Sho if mishandled, but potentially safe if trusted.

Later, Mirei's own Plugg pressure and betrayal assignment create a mirror: Sho once trusted Mirei with a dangerous secret, and Mirei later feared disappointing Sho when forced into her own dangerous secret.
