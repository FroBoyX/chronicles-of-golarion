# Harvus G'ron and Master Scourge

## Document Status

- Type: Relationship record
- Maturity: Draft, expanded from Harvus light timeline evidence
- Scope: Press-ganging, Wormwood punishments, and unresolved vengeance

## Summary

Harvus G'ron and Master Scourge have one of the most direct revenge relationships in Book One.

Scourge is not only a cruel officer to Harvus. He is the person Harvus understands as responsible for the damage to his wing and for the humiliation of his forced service.

## Origin Wound

Harvus was ambushed after the Formidably Maid setup.

Aretta Bansion delivered a disabling blow from behind.

Scourge later dragged Harvus aboard or supervised his capture and twisted or damaged Harvus' wing.

This made the press-ganging personal.

## Recognition of the Press Gang

Harvus' light timeline records that he recognized Jape, Aretta, and Maheem on waking aboard the Wormwood.

Maheem later told Harvus that Scourge was responsible for the wing injury.

This gave Harvus a clear object for revenge.

## Calistria

Harvus prays to Calistria for revenge against Scourge.

This matters because Calistrian vengeance is not simply uncontrolled rage. It can be personal, precise, and deliberately repaid.

## Wormwood Escalation

During boarding exercises or defensive combat training, Harvus' rage slipped.

He insulted Scourge, was lashed, nearly remained conscious, was whipped again, and went down.

This shows Harvus could not always contain the wound Scourge represented.

## Sho Boxed

Harvus later learned from Sho that Scourge had tried to have her killed and that she had been boxed.

Harvus' light notes record that the thought of Scourge's villainy made his blood boil and that he would see Scourge's blood spilled.

This expands the vengeance thread beyond Harvus' own injury. Scourge is also threatening people Harvus sees as comrades.

## Campaign Significance

Scourge corrupts discipline.

Harvus' revenge against him therefore risks becoming either justice or another form of violence.

The campaign should not flatten this into "Harvus kills Scourge."

The real question is what Harvus believes vengeance requires when Scourge is finally vulnerable.

## Current State

- Scourge is alive.
- Harvus is returning from Riptide Cove.
- Harvus likely does not yet know Sho is currently locked in the hot-box again.
- Goyle carries warning that Plugg and Scourge intended to eliminate troublesome PCs.
- Harvus' rage has just been redirected into rescue, not revenge.

## Possible Resolution Modes

Harvus may seek:

- death,
- humiliation,
- confession,
- wing-for-wing injury,
- formal pirate justice,
- Calistrian repayment,
- or restraint despite opportunity.

None are canon yet.

## Open Questions

- What does Harvus believe Calistria actually wants from him?
- Does Harvus prioritize rescuing Sho over revenge if he learns she is boxed?
- Does Sandara counsel restraint, vengeance, or pirate justice?
- Does Scourge try to flee if Plugg falls?
- Does Harvus want Scourge dead in combat, executed, humiliated, or exposed?
- Would Harvus accept someone else killing Scourge?
