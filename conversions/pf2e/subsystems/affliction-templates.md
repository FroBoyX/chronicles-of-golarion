# PF2e Affliction Templates

## Document Status

- Type: PF2e campaign subsystem conversion note
- Maturity: Draft / GM-originated conversion templates
- PF2e status: Conversion candidate templates, not approved relaunch mechanics
- Spoiler scope: Player-safe rules draft
- Created: 2026-07-07

## Purpose

This document preserves the table's disease and poison progression intent for possible use in a PF2e relaunch of *Chronicles of Golarion*.

These are not direct ports of Pathfinder 1e disease or poison rules. They are PF2e-native templates for building diseases, poisons, shipboard infections, fevers, toxins, alchemical accidents, supernatural afflictions, and similar hazards using PF2e's existing affliction rules.

This file does not rewrite PF1 historical evidence, prior rolls, prior outcomes, or campaign canon.

## PF1 Historical / Homebrew Basis

The table used disease and poison progression tracks because the basic PF1 disease and poison rules did not always mimic believable worsening afflictions.

The historical design problems were:

- diseases could be trivialized by common restoration or removal magic;
- afflictions that did not damage Constitution often could not kill;
- ability damage could be unrealistically powerful or swingy;
- physical disease, mental disease, and different ability-targeting poisons needed distinct collapse patterns;
- poisons and diseases should sometimes worsen progressively, reach end states, and leave victims recovering after the toxin or sickness is removed.

The uploaded PF1-era poison rule included separate Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma poison tracks. The previous table disease rule included separate physical and mental disease progression tracks.

## PF2e Source Basis Checked

Rules basis checked against Archives of Nethys PF2e pages on 2026-07-07:

- Afflictions — Player Core pg. 430; diseases and poisons are afflictions that can progress through stages.
- Affliction initial saves — failed initial save advances to stage 1; critical failure can advance to stage 2.
- Affliction stages — success reduces stage by 1, critical success by 2, failure increases stage by 1, and critical failure by 2.
- Damage and conditions — stage effects can include damage, conditions, penalties, and other effects.
- Multiple exposures — repeated poison exposure can increase the poison's stage.
- Virulent afflictions — harder-to-remove afflictions can require consecutive successful saves.
- Removing afflictions — Medicine and cleanse affliction are the normal PF2e removal/treatment hooks.
- Conditions — clumsy, drained, enfeebled, stupefied, confused, sickened, slowed, paralyzed, unconscious, and related conditions provide the PF2e condition vocabulary for these templates.

## Design Position

PF2e already has staged afflictions.

Therefore, do not port PF1 ability damage, ability penalties, staggered/full-round wording, flat-footed wording, disabled wording, automatic social-control rules, or old spell-level loss directly.

Use PF2e's native affliction rules and preserve the old table intent through template design:

- physical disease attacks the body over time;
- mental disease attacks cognition, perception, memory, will, identity, or reality-testing;
- poisons are more acute and often target a specific physical or mental axis;
- severe afflictions may have end states such as death, coma, permanent ruin, or transformation;
- ordinary afflictions may cap at an intermediate stage.

## General Conversion Policy

When converting an old disease or poison:

1. Identify the affliction's fiction first.
2. Choose whether it is a disease, poison, curse, alchemical toxin, supernatural infection, or mixed affliction.
3. Use PF2e's normal affliction format: level, save, onset, maximum duration if any, stages, interval, and cure/removal notes.
4. Choose one of the templates below as the stage spine.
5. Adjust stages for the affliction's level, lethality, tone, and campaign importance.
6. Do not require every affliction to reach death. Many should end at impaired, bedridden, deranged, catatonic, unconscious, or permanent injury.
7. Record contagious carrier status, visible symptoms, and shipboard exposure hazards when relevant.
8. Treat these as draft templates until tested in play.

## Disease Templates

### Physical Disease Progression

Use this for diseases that primarily attack the body: fever, rot, plague, infection, parasites, wound sickness, starvation-related sickness, bilge fever, or supernatural bodily corruption.

**Track:** Healthy -> Latent / Carrier -> Weakened -> Impaired -> Disabled -> Bedridden -> Comatose -> Dead / Permanent Ruin

#### Healthy

The creature is not currently affected by the disease.

#### Latent / Carrier — Stage 1

The creature carries the disease but suffers no mechanical penalty.

Possible effects:

- contagious but asymptomatic;
- detectable by Medicine or magic;
- visible only as mild symptoms if the disease is advanced or supernatural.

#### Weakened — Stage 2

The disease begins visibly affecting the body.

Possible PF2e effects:

- sickened 1;
- fatigued, if the disease's fiction supports persistent exhaustion;
- minor disease damage at stage entry.

#### Impaired — Stage 3

The disease interferes with physical action and endurance.

Possible PF2e effects:

- sickened 2;
- drained 1 or enfeebled 1;
- slowed 1 during strenuous scenes or after failed Fortitude saves;
- GM-imposed complications for climbing, swimming, labor, or forced marches.

#### Disabled — Stage 4

The creature can still act, but action carries risk.

Possible PF2e effects:

- drained 2;
- slowed 1;
- recurring disease damage;
- flat check required before strenuous actions, with failure causing lost action, sickened increase, or collapse.

#### Bedridden — Stage 5

The creature is awake and can converse but cannot function physically without aid.

Possible PF2e effects:

- slowed 2 or unable to take move actions except Crawl;
- prone and unable to stand unaided;
- requires care to eat, drink, travel, or avoid exposure complications.

#### Comatose — Stage 6

The disease drives the creature into fevered unconsciousness.

Possible PF2e effects:

- unconscious;
- cannot be woken by ordinary means while at this stage;
- Medicine, magic, or successful affliction recovery can still reduce the stage unless the affliction says otherwise.

#### Dead / Permanent Ruin — Stage 7

The disease reaches its final state.

Choose one:

- death;
- permanent organ damage;
- lasting weakness;
- supernatural transformation;
- contagious corpse or post-death effect, if appropriate.

### Mental Disease Progression

Use this for diseases, infections, fevers, psychic afflictions, supernatural curses, or toxins that attack cognition, perception, memory, will, identity, or reality-testing rather than the body.

**Track:** Healthy -> Latent / Carrier -> Disturbed -> Impaired -> Befuddled -> Deranged -> Comatose -> Dead / Permanent Ruin

#### Healthy

The creature is not currently affected by the disease.

#### Latent / Carrier — Stage 1

The creature carries the disease but suffers no mechanical penalty.

Possible signs:

- strange dreams;
- irritability;
- faint sensory distortions;
- memory slips;
- contagious but asymptomatic behavior.

#### Disturbed — Stage 2

The disease begins clouding thought, mood, or perception.

Possible PF2e effects:

- stupefied 1;
- or a -1 status penalty to Perception and mental skill checks if stupefied is too strong for the disease.

Narrative signs:

- intrusive thoughts;
- irrational suspicion;
- poor sleep;
- difficulty focusing;
- emotional volatility;
- mild hallucinations under stress.

#### Impaired — Stage 3

The disease interferes with reasoning, spellcasting, judgment, and disciplined action.

Possible PF2e effects:

- stupefied 2;
- -1 status penalty to initiative checks that rely on Perception unless the disease's fiction suggests heightened paranoia instead;
- complex concentration, recall, navigation, command, or social-pressure checks may require a flat check or suffer a circumstance penalty at GM discretion.

Narrative signs:

- obvious confusion;
- repeated questions;
- distorted priorities;
- inability to track plans;
- difficulty distinguishing memory from present events.

#### Befuddled — Stage 4

The creature is losing coherent control of action under pressure.

Possible PF2e effects:

- stupefied 2;
- at the start of each encounter, or during a high-stress scene, attempt a flat check:
  - success: act normally;
  - failure: lose 1 action on the first round as the creature babbles, freezes, misreads the scene, or follows a false impulse;
  - critical failure, if using a d20 flat check house interpretation: confused until the end of its first turn.

Alternative harsher version:

- at the start of each turn during encounter mode, attempt a DC 5 flat check; on a failure, lose 1 action that turn.

Narrative signs:

- talking to unseen people;
- wandering attention;
- mistaking allies for strangers;
- fragmented speech;
- acting on imagined orders or threats.

#### Deranged — Stage 5

The disease has deeply compromised the creature's grasp on reality and self-direction.

Possible PF2e effects:

- stupefied 3;
- at the start of each encounter, attempt a Will save against the disease DC:
  - success: the creature is disoriented but can act;
  - failure: confused for 1 round;
  - critical failure: confused for 1 minute, with a new save at the end of each turn to reduce or end the episode if the GM wants more player agency.

The GM may impose scene-specific false perceptions, but should tell the player what the character perceives rather than simply taking control whenever possible.

Narrative signs:

- persistent hallucinations;
- inability to recognize friends;
- compulsive behavior;
- delusional certainty;
- violent or terrified misinterpretation of ordinary events;
- loss of command reliability.

#### Comatose — Stage 6

The disease has driven the creature into an unreachable inner state.

Possible PF2e effects:

- unconscious;
- cannot be woken by ordinary noise, shaking, or pain while it remains at this stage;
- Medicine, appropriate magic, or successful affliction recovery can still reduce the stage unless the disease says otherwise.

Narrative signs:

- fever dreams;
- muttering;
- vacant staring before collapse;
- dream-locked consciousness;
- supernatural visions;
- intermittent lucid fragments, if useful for the story.

#### Dead / Permanent Ruin — Stage 7

The disease reaches its final state.

Choose one:

- death;
- permanent mental injury;
- memory fracture;
- personality collapse;
- spiritual possession;
- madness mark;
- supernatural transformation;
- other GM-defined consequence.

This stage should be reserved for severe diseases, supernatural afflictions, or plot-significant infections. Ordinary mental diseases may end at Befuddled, Deranged, or Comatose instead.

## Poison Templates

Poisons should generally feel more acute than diseases.

Use poisons for venom, alchemical toxins, poisoned weapons, contaminated food, paralytics, nerve agents, rotgut additives, animal toxins, and supernatural toxic substances.

Poison design principles:

- use fast onset or immediate onset unless fiction says otherwise;
- use a maximum duration unless the poison is supernatural, cursed, or especially tenacious;
- multiple exposure can worsen the stage using PF2e's native multiple-exposure rule;
- recovery after the poison ends can be handled narratively or through lingering conditions if the toxin is severe;
- most poisons should cap before death unless deliberately lethal.

### Strength Poison — Muscle Failure

Use this for poisons that attack muscle strength, striking power, lifting, climbing, swimming, and gross motor force.

**PF1 intent:** weakness -> impairment -> staggered action -> immobility -> death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Weakened -> Enfeebled -> Slowed -> Paralyzed / Immobile -> Dead

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: enfeebled 1.
- Stage 2: enfeebled 2; possible Speed penalty or reduced carrying capacity.
- Stage 3: enfeebled 3 and slowed 1.
- Stage 4: paralyzed or immobilized, depending on poison severity.
- Stage 5: death, only for lethal poisons.

### Dexterity Poison — Nerve / Motor Failure

Use this for poisons that attack reflexes, coordination, fine motor control, balance, and bodily responsiveness.

**PF1 intent:** sluggish reactions -> stiffness -> staggered action -> immobility -> death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Sluggish -> Stiffened -> Slowed -> Paralyzed / Immobile -> Dead

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: clumsy 1.
- Stage 2: clumsy 2; off-guard if the poison significantly compromises defensive movement.
- Stage 3: clumsy 3 and slowed 1.
- Stage 4: paralyzed or immobilized.
- Stage 5: death, only for lethal poisons.

### Constitution Poison — Systemic Failure

Use this for poisons that attack blood, breath, organs, immune response, metabolism, heart function, or general vitality.

**PF1 intent:** Fortitude collapse -> repeated poison damage -> disabled/shock -> unconsciousness -> death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Sickened -> Drained -> Shocked -> Unconscious -> Dead

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: poison damage and sickened 1.
- Stage 2: poison damage, sickened 2, and drained 1.
- Stage 3: poison damage, drained 2, and slowed 1.
- Stage 4: unconscious or dying 1, depending on poison severity.
- Stage 5: death, only for lethal poisons.

### Intelligence Poison — Reasoning Collapse

Use this for poisons that attack memory, reasoning, learned skill, planning, language, spell formulae, or analytical thought.

**PF1 intent:** reasoning impairment -> spellcasting / pool impairment -> animalistic state -> coma -> brain death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Dulled -> Impaired -> Animalistic -> Comatose -> Dead / Cognitive Ruin

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: stupefied 1; penalty or complication for Recall Knowledge, navigation, tactical planning, or complex crafting.
- Stage 2: stupefied 2.
- Stage 3: stupefied 3; cannot use complex tactics or prepared plans; possible confused episode under stress.
- Stage 4: unconscious / comatose; cannot be woken normally.
- Stage 5: death or permanent cognitive ruin.

### Wisdom Poison — Perception / Reality Collapse

Use this for poisons that attack awareness, judgment, sensory processing, instinct, will, spiritual perception, or reality-testing.

**PF1 intent:** awareness impairment -> Will weakness -> confusion -> coma -> death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Unsettled -> Impaired -> Confused -> Comatose -> Dead / Reality Fracture

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: stupefied 1 or frightened 1, depending on poison fiction.
- Stage 2: stupefied 2; possible penalty or complication for Perception, Sense Motive-style judgment, or Will-like decisions.
- Stage 3: confused for 1 round on failed flat check or failed Will save under stress.
- Stage 4: unconscious / comatose; cannot receive coherent sensory input.
- Stage 5: death or permanent reality fracture.

### Charisma Poison — Self / Agency Collapse

Use this for poisons that attack identity, ego, force of personality, confidence, command presence, emotional coherence, or social resistance.

**PF1 intent:** weakened sense of self -> impaired charisma and magic -> pliability -> catatonia -> death.

**PF2e template:** Healthy -> Unsteady Self -> Impaired Will -> Pliable -> Catatonic -> Dead / Ego Collapse

Possible stages:

- Stage 1: stupefied 1; penalty or complication for Deception, Diplomacy, Intimidation, Performance, command, or morale checks.
- Stage 2: stupefied 2; worsened resistance to emotion, fear, command, or social-pressure effects if the poison's fiction supports it.
- Stage 3: pliable; the creature has reduced agency and social resistance. Do not use automatic social control by default. Instead, apply a status penalty to resist Coerce, Request, mental influence, intimidation, command pressure, or similar effects.
- Stage 4: catatonic; stunned, slowed, unconscious, or unable to initiate actions depending on poison fiction.
- Stage 5: death, ego collapse, possession vulnerability, or supernatural transformation.

## Implementation Notes

### Use PF2e Conditions First

Preferred condition mapping:

- Strength poisons: enfeebled, slowed, immobilized, paralyzed.
- Dexterity poisons: clumsy, off-guard, slowed, immobilized, paralyzed.
- Constitution poisons: poison damage, sickened, drained, slowed, unconscious, dying.
- Intelligence poisons: stupefied, action restrictions for complex thought, confused episodes, unconscious.
- Wisdom poisons: stupefied, frightened, confused, unconscious.
- Charisma poisons: stupefied, penalties to social resistance, controlled-like vulnerability only with caution, stunned/slowed/catatonic states.
- Physical diseases: sickened, fatigued, drained, enfeebled, slowed, unconscious.
- Mental diseases: stupefied, confused, action loss, false perception, unconscious.

### Avoid Over-Control

Mental disease, Wisdom poison, and Charisma poison can remove agency if written too aggressively.

Prefer:

- telling the player what the character perceives;
- limited action loss;
- short confused episodes;
- penalties to resist influence;
- player-facing hallucination prompts;
- GM control only in severe stages or brief episodes.

### Shipboard Use

For *Skull & Shackles*, afflictions should support:

- filthy ship conditions;
- bilge exposure;
- bad food and rotgut;
- untreated wounds;
- island parasites;
- venomous wildlife;
- alchemy and mutagen fallout;
- magical or cursed infections;
- contagion among confined crew;
- pressure to find medicine, rest, clean water, or magical treatment.

## Recommended Pilot

Do not approve these templates as campaign-wide relaunch mechanics until a few afflictions are built and tested.

Suggested pilot afflictions:

- one physical disease from shipboard filth or island exposure;
- one mental disease / supernatural fever;
- one Strength or Dexterity poison from wildlife venom;
- one Constitution poison from alchemy, rotgut, or toxin;
- one mental-axis poison relevant to Rak, alchemy, or occult/supernatural threats.

Success criteria:

- afflictions feel progressive and dangerous;
- PF2e stage movement remains easy to track;
- Medicine and magic matter without deleting all tension;
- conditions do not overwhelm play;
- mental/agency effects create story pressure without becoming punitive or consent-hostile.
