# PF2e Stamina / Combat Tricks Conversion Policy

## Document Status

- Type: PF2e campaign subsystem conversion note
- Maturity: Draft / deferred conversion policy
- PF2e status: Do not port directly; preserve as table-history and revisit only if martial play needs it
- Spoiler scope: Player-safe rules draft
- Created: 2026-07-07

## Purpose

This document preserves the table's Pathfinder 1e Combat Stamina / Stamina Pool rule as conversion evidence and records the current PF2e conversion decision.

The rule should not be treated as approved PF2e relaunch mechanics. It should not be ported directly into early PF2e pilots.

This file does not rewrite PF1 historical evidence, prior rolls, prior outcomes, or campaign canon.

## PF1 Historical / Table Basis

The table had a Stamina Pool rule active alongside Combat Tricks.

The PF1-era rule granted a stamina pool when a character had an ability that granted stamina points. The maximum pool was based on base attack bonus plus Constitution modifier. Stamina points could be spent during combat to perform Combat Tricks attached to combat feats. Spending stamina was not an action, but could not be done while unconscious, fatigued, or exhausted.

Other table-rule features:

- multiple Combat Tricks could be used on the same action or attack if the character had enough stamina;
- the same trick could not be used twice within its scope;
- dropping to 0 stamina caused fatigue until at least 1 stamina point returned;
- temporary Constitution increases did not increase stamina;
- permanent Constitution increases did adjust stamina;
- stamina recovered through uninterrupted rest at 1 point per minute;
- exertion, combat, physical checks, and several conditions prevented stamina recovery.

The design intent was to let martial characters push beyond normal limits, exploit openings, and express battlefield flexibility through grit and physical reserve.

## PF2e Source Basis Checked

Rules basis checked against Archives of Nethys PF2e pages on 2026-07-07:

- Actions — Player Core encounter rules; PF2e creatures normally get 3 actions and 1 reaction each turn.
- Hero Points — Player Core pg. 413; session-limited heroic resource used for rerolls and avoiding death.
- Stamina variant — Gamemastery Guide pg. 200; PF2e stamina is primarily a durability / healing-pacing variant using Stamina Points and Resolve Points, not a Combat Trick resource.
- Stamina Points — PF2e variant; damage is taken from Stamina before Hit Points, and Stamina recovers differently than Hit Points.
- Resolve Points — PF2e variant; grit/luck pool used with stamina actions such as Take a Breather and Rally.
- Other Creatures — PF2e stamina variant notes that monsters generally do not need Stamina Points unless recurring or fighting alongside PCs.

## Design Position

Do not port PF1 Combat Stamina directly.

PF2e already addresses much of the original problem through:

- 3-action economy;
- class feats;
- skill actions;
- reactions;
- Hero Points;
- proficiency-gated skill actions;
- more reliable encounter math;
- PF2e-native Stamina variant, if the GM wants a durability / pacing change.

PF1 Combat Stamina was a feat-linked martial trick resource. PF2e's official Stamina variant is a durability and recovery subsystem. They are not equivalent.

## Current Conversion Decision

Current recommendation:

- Do not include PF1 Stamina Pool / Combat Tricks in the first PF2e relaunch pilot.
- Do not create a universal martial stamina pool yet.
- Do not stack a new stamina trick economy on top of PF2e class feats, Hero Points, Facing, and Active Defense until the simpler conversion is tested.
- Preserve the table rule as historical design evidence.
- Revisit only if PF2e martial play feels too rigid after testing baseline PF2e, Facing Variant, and Active Defense.

## Why Direct Port Is Not Recommended

Risks:

- PF2e has no base attack bonus, so the old pool formula has no clean native anchor.
- Spending stamina without actions can overload PF2e's tight action economy.
- Combat Tricks tied to PF1 combat feats would require rebuilding many feats or writing a large custom feat library.
- A separate stamina economy may compete with Hero Points, class feats, focus spells, reactions, and Active Defense.
- Tracking stamina points for all PCs and martial NPCs adds bookkeeping.
- NPC generator work becomes more complex if every martial NPC needs stamina tricks.
- Foundry implementation may require custom effects or macros.

## Relationship to PF2e Stamina Variant

PF2e's official Stamina variant may still be considered separately.

That variant splits durability into Stamina Points and Hit Points and adds Resolve Points. It changes healing pacing and encounter endurance. It is not a Combat Trick subsystem.

Use PF2e Stamina only if the campaign wants:

- faster recovery between fights;
- shorter adventuring-day downtime;
- more cinematic endurance;
- less complete in-combat healing;
- recurring allies or enemies who need stamina-style durability.

For *Skull & Shackles*, caution is advised because shipboard scarcity, injuries, exhaustion, disease, and survival pressure are part of the campaign tone.

## Relationship to Active Defense

Active Defense preserves part of the same martial design goal in a more PF2e-native way:

- guarded survival;
- block and parry;
- riposte threat;
- tactical commitment;
- prepared defender deterrence.

If Active Defense works during testing, PF1 Stamina Pool may not be needed.

If martial play still feels too static later, a smaller exertion subsystem can be designed around specific gaps rather than porting the full PF1 Combat Tricks engine.

## Possible Future Minimal Exertion Variant

If needed later, consider a much smaller PF2e-native exertion rule:

- no BAB-based pool;
- no full PF1 Combat Trick library;
- limited uses per encounter or per rest;
- focuses on movement, facing correction, resisting forced movement, guarded recovery, or one-time martial surges;
- avoids flat attack bonuses;
- avoids stacking with Hero Point rerolls;
- does not replace class feats or reactions.

This is only a future fallback, not current relaunch mechanics.

## Recommended Pilot Position

Pilot order:

1. Baseline PF2e PC / NPC conversion.
2. Facing Variant.
3. Active Defense.
4. Reassess whether martials need additional exertion.
5. Only then consider a minimal stamina / exertion mechanic.

Success criteria for leaving Stamina aside:

- martial turns feel flexible enough through PF2e actions and feats;
- Facing and Active Defense provide the desired battlefield realism;
- Hero Points cover rare heroic pushes;
- NPC generation remains manageable;
- no player feels their PF1 table identity depended on Combat Tricks specifically.
