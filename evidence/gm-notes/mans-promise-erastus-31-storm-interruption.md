# GM Note — Erastus 31 Storm Interruption

## Document Status

- Type: GM truth / AP scaffold integration note
- Maturity: Canon-setting correction
- Scope: End of Erastus 30 nightshift, early Erastus 31 storm, unresolved Harvus / Sho / Mirei follow-through, and transition to Bonewrack crisis
- Source basis: GM clarification in current reconstruction conversation and summarized Adventure Path scaffold
- Spoiler scope: GM-facing / reconstruction-facing

## Correction / Clarification

The unresolved Erastus 30 nightshift threads do **not** resolve in ordinary shipboard play.

During night shift, in the early morning of **Erastus 31**, the storm hits.

This means the following threads are interrupted rather than completed:

- Harvus' possible attempt to sneak into the captain's or officers' quarters after Sandara's request.
- Any possible plan to use Sho to get information the rough way.
- Mirei's return report to Plugg after gauging the crew.
- Plugg's immediate response to Syl's report about Mirei's handling of Tam, Sho, and the crew.

The storm forces all hands into emergency ship work and transitions the Man's Promise toward the Bonewrack / grindylow crisis.

## AP Scaffold Summary

The Adventure Path scaffold supports the following structure:

- On the second day of the prize voyage, friendly NPCs begin whispering that Plugg may claim the Man's Promise for himself.
- The rumors become stronger once the course change is identified and the ship is understood to be heading east toward Bloodcove.
- In the evening, Kroop can identify the likely destination as Rickety's Squibs, a hidden dry dock near Bloodcove where ships are altered enough to become unrecognizable.
- On the fourth day, a storm strikes in the early hours.
- The storm drives the ship off course, calls all hands on deck, and puts all crew into rigging / storm work.
- Toward dawn, reefs and islets appear in the storm. The Man's Promise is approaching Bonewrack Isle.
- Grindylows use the storm and poor visibility to board, kidnap sailors, and then attack more openly.

## Campaign Mapping

Current reconstruction maps the AP structure as follows:

- Prize voyage Day 1: Erastus 28, after the Erastus 27 transfer / first night sequence.
- Prize voyage Day 2: Erastus 29, course-change whispers and Kroop's Rickety's Squibs warning.
- Prize voyage Day 3: Erastus 30, Mistress Mirei / crew-gauging / Sandara's attempted information-gathering plan.
- Prize voyage Day 4: early Erastus 31, storm during night shift, all hands called into rigging, approach to Bonewrack.

This preserves the AP tempo while aligning it with the recovered table scenes.

## Canon Impact

- Treat Harvus' attempted infiltration, Sho's possible rough information-gathering, and Mirei's report to Plugg as **interrupted by the storm**, not as forgotten open social scenes.
- Treat Erastus 31 night-shift storm as the hard transition from whisper-stage mutiny politics into crisis-stage survival.
- Treat the storm as the reason the crew does not get a clean morning of political consequences after Syl's report.
- Treat Bonewrack's grindylow abductions and attack as emerging from this storm window.
