- Maturity: Canon resolution note, updated with Sho investigation / burial clarification

- Scope: Giffer Tibbs death mechanism, Rak culpability, Sho knowledge limits, Man's Promise battle casualty accounting, evidence trail after burial

## Raw GM Resolution

> Giffer was mechanically unconscious but stable when Rak's player purposefully stomped on her when running through the fog in an attempt to kill her. This ultimately destabilized her leading to her death, and the only casualty for the Wormwood in the capture of the Man's Promise.
>
> Giffer initially went down due to a combination of the arrow, and melee action and likely should have stayed behind with Merie but when she choose to bored she went down. Sho initially solved that but left her to continue to try and hold Rak and Harvus back from doing more damage and ultimately that gave the opening for Rak that caused her demise in the fog.
>
> Nobody directly saw Rak stomp on her with intention, it was noticed that she was notably trampled.

## Raw GM Clarification — Investigation / Burial

> Sho attempted to investigate the death but came to know conclusions that allowed them to act on their player knowledge.
> Rak has not admitted to the kill.
> At this point, Giffer was given a pirates burial during the revel, so the only link would be magic that shows what occurred on the Man's Promise.

Interpretation note: the phrase "came to know conclusions" is recorded above as written. For integration, this is treated as "came to no actionable conclusions," because the surrounding clarification preserves the player/character knowledge wall.

## Canon Extraction

- Giffer Tibbs was the only Wormwood-side casualty in the capture of the Man's Promise.
- Giffer initially went down because of a combination of arrow damage and melee action during the boarding.
- Giffer likely should have stayed behind with Mirei rather than boarding, but chose to board and went down during the fight.
- Sho stabilized or otherwise solved Giffer's immediate dying condition.
- Sho left Giffer to continue trying to restrain Rak and Harvus from causing more harm.
- While moving through the fog, Rak's player intentionally had Rak stomp Giffer in an attempt to kill her.
- The stomp destabilized Giffer and ultimately led to her death.
- No character directly saw Rak intentionally stomp Giffer.
- The observable aftermath was that Giffer was notably trampled.
- Sho attempted to investigate Giffer's death.
- Sho came to no actionable conclusion that would allow her to act on player knowledge.
- Rak has not admitted to killing Giffer.
- Giffer received a pirate burial during the revel.
- After the burial, the remaining route to prove Rak's intent is magic or equivalent supernatural reconstruction that shows what occurred on the Man's Promise.

## Knowledge Split

### GM / Canon Truth

Rak intentionally stomped Giffer while she was unconscious but stable, destabilizing her and causing her death.

### Sho Character Knowledge

Sho knew or could know that Giffer had been stable when Sho left her, then later died. Sho did not directly witness Rak's intentional stomp and did not have proof of Rak's intent.

Sho attempted to investigate the death, but found no actionable conclusion that would let her act on the player's knowledge that Rak was responsible.

### Witness / Crew Knowledge

No witness directly saw Rak's intent. Other characters may notice or learn only that Giffer had been trampled unless future evidence, confession, or magic exposes Rak's action.

### Rak Character Knowledge

Rak knows whether he acted intentionally. He has not admitted to killing Giffer.

## Body / Evidence Status

Giffer was given a pirate burial during the revel.

Because the body is gone, ordinary physical investigation is no longer a viable route to prove the killing. The remaining proof route is magic or equivalent supernatural evidence that can show what occurred on the Man's Promise.
