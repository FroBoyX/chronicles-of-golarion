# Wormwood Ship Areas and Geography

## Document Status

- Type: Evidence / AP scaffold extraction
- Maturity: Structured extract
- Source: GM-provided Wormwood map and area text in Narrative Reconstruction chat
- Scope: Wormwood physical layout, area identities, access restrictions, and recurring locations
- Canon status: Published/AP baseline scaffold unless table-specific events contradict it

## Ship Overview

- The Wormwood is a three-masted sailing ship, about 100 feet long and 30 feet wide amidships.
- It needs a minimum crew of 20.
- The ship is crowded with named pirates constantly moving through it.
- Interior rooms are usually dark or dim, with hooded lanterns at night.
- Many officer spaces, cabins, chests, and stores are locked or trapped.
- The Wormwood should not be treated as a static map. It is a crowded social machine.

## A1. Foredeck

- Raised deck about 10 feet above the main deck.
- Immediately behind the dragon-shaped bowsprit.
- Foremast rises above this deck.
- Narrative use: work, wind, spray, public exposure, crew traffic near the bow.

## A2. Poop Deck

- Raised deck about 15 feet above the main deck.
- Contains mizzenmast, bridge, and ship's wheel.
- Wheel is decorated with silver inlays and kraken-head bolts.
- Harmak Gruft's corpse hangs in an iron cage from the mizzenmast.
- Pluck the parrot perches on or near the cage and curses.
- Harrigan and Plugg spend much of their on-deck time here.
- Off-limits to non-officers when Harrigan is on deck.
- Narrative use: authority, command distance, public fear, grotesque warning, vertical hierarchy.

## A3. Main Deck

- Runs between foredeck and poop deck.
- Mainmast rises from the center and reaches to the crow's nest.
- Rigging links the masts and can be crossed by skilled crew.
- Thick ropes at the mainmast serve as a whipping post.
- The ship's clock hangs above the whipping post. It depicts worms moving through whale corpses.
- The clock bell marks dawn and dusk, beginning and ending the workday.
- Two large gridded hatches open to the middle hold below.
- Doors forward lead to officers' quarters.
- Doors aft lead to the captain's quarters.
- A sweatbox sits beneath the bridge.
- A jolly boat sits near the port rail.
- Narrative use: workday center, public punishment theater, crew musters, Bloody Hour, hierarchy visible from deck levels.

## A4. Officers' Quarters

- Cramped officer cabin with hammocks and sleeping mats.
- Stairs descend to the middle hold.
- Deck doors are locked and trapped.
- Officers' footlockers are locked.
- Plugg, Scourge, Riaris Krine, Peppery Longfarthing, and Habbly Quarne sleep here at night.
- At least one officer may often be found here during the day.
- Narrative use: forbidden pressure, officer proximity, whispered orders, danger of theft or intrusion.

## A4a. Armory

- Tiny, locked, trapped chamber of weapons and armor.
- Riaris Krine is meticulous and quickly notices obvious theft.
- Narrative use: forbidden violence, temptation, search risk, master gunner's domain.

## A4b. Peppery Longfarthing's Laboratory

- Cramped and locked laboratory.
- Filled with curious and unsettling objects.
- Protected by superior magical and mechanical security.
- Narrative use: strangeness, magic, night-watch isolation, reminder that the ship has powers beyond lash and blade.

## A5. Captain's Cabin

- Richly decorated cabin with kraken-and-whale carvings.
- Portholes admit light.
- Bed can convert into navigational or dining table.
- Superior locks and traps protect the cabin and stairs.
- Harrigan entertains officers here and sleeps here at night.
- Narrative use: inaccessible power, wealth, charts, hidden command decisions.

## A5a. Cabin Girl's Quarters

- Small cot for Caulky Tarroon.
- Narrative use: servant proximity to Harrigan, small-space vulnerability.

## A5b. Captain's Storage

- Locked captain's booty storage with trapped chests.
- Narrative use: inaccessible treasure, officer stakes, danger of theft.

## A6. Middle Hold

- Main cargo hold.
- Mostly empty early, except pigs that may escape their cages.
- Stairs connect officers' quarters, lower hold, galley side, and captain's quarters route.
- Owlbear Hartshorn is chained to the foremast to keep him from trouble and to keep crew out of officer spaces.
- Light siege equipment and oil barrels are stored near the mainmast.
- Narrative use: transition zone, pig chaos, Owlbear as living barrier, work traffic, overheard movement between decks.

## A7. Quartermaster and Cook's Cabin

- Tiny cabin shared by Cut-Throat Grok and Ambrose Kroop.
- Contains two beds and two footlockers.
- Also contains several of Kroop's chickens.
- Grok sleeps here at night.
- Kroop sleeps here and may be found here during the day if insensibly drunk.
- Narrative use: cramped intimacy, Grok/Kroop overlap, chickens, smell of galley life invading private space.

## A8. Galley

- Ambrose Kroop's domain.
- Cramped and chaotic kitchen with two worktables, cupboards, two small stoves, cauldrons, utensils, and many knives.
- Chickens and three goats wander freely.
- The kitchen is filthy, cluttered, and still functional.
- Stoves are perpetually lit.
- Spices mingle with rainwater, rum, biscuit, salted beef, sauerkraut, and fresh Port Peril vegetables.
- The galley functions as masterwork tools for cooking despite the squalor.
- Useful lost items may be found among the mess.
- Narrative use: Rak's first domain, heat, knife clutter, goats, chickens, Kroop's moods, food as survival.

## A9. Quartermaster's Store

- Grok's cramped, crowded storeroom.
- Locked with a superior lock and served through a small hatch.
- Acts as an unofficial shop and barter point.
- Technically the captain's goods, practically Grok's leverage.
- Usually open from dawn to about 3 p.m., before Grok starts drinking.
- Grok only opens after hours to friends.
- Grok carries rum rations to the main deck after supper and often forgets to lock the store or hatch.
- PCs' starting equipment begins in one of the lockers.
- Narrative use: barter, missing gear, Grok's gatekeeping, rum economy, after-hours opportunity.

## A10. Lower Hold and Crew Berths

- Spacious hold supported by sixteen pillars.
- Common pirates sleep here at night in hammocks tied between walls and pillars.
- Kipper and Patch Patchsalt claim the far forward section near the foremast and stairs.
- Trapdoor behind the mainmast opens to the bilges.
- Footlockers line the walls. Some are occupied, many are empty early.
- Narrative use: cramped common life, territorial sleeping areas, private conversations in public, bullying, theft anxiety, night sounds.

## A11. Bilges

- Lowest deck.
- Foul, damp, dark, with 1 to 2 feet of brackish stinking water.
- Cobwebs above, bilge pump near stern.
- Also serves as brig, with manacles fixed to bulkheads.
- Jakes Magpie is held here at the start before punishment.
- Bilge spiders may drop on workers.
- Useful discarded items may be found in dark water.
- Narrative use: punishment, rot, brig, hidden fear, spider threat, worst-work destination.

## Reconstruction Use

Use this file to seed physical and social geography in Day 2 and later Wormwood narrative:

- Area identity should recur.
- Ship movement should feel cramped and vertical.
- Work scenes should reveal geography before the Wormwood becomes routine.
- Do not introduce unnamed generic Wormwood sailors when a named crew member can fill the background role.
- Treat traps and locks as real boundaries even when not narratively foregrounded.
