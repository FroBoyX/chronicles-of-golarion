## Encounter Frame

Mr. Plugg brings **Owlbear Hartshorn** onto deck after the evening meal for sport.

Owlbear is normally kept belowdecks in the middle hold.

The fight is framed as bare-knuckle fists or wrestling only. No magic and no weapons are permitted. The last combatant conscious wins.

Plugg wagers money on Owlbear and uses the event as entertainment, intimidation, and a way to teach someone a lesson.

## Owlbear Description

Owlbear is a large, simpleminded man with meaty fists.

The crew often laughs at his expense. He clowns, laughs, and shouts random phrases because it makes people laugh.

He has signs of prior humiliation and abuse from the crew, including a recent tarring-and-feathering.

## Fight Mechanics Relevant to Sho

Owlbear is blind in one eye and slow.

A character who notices this can fight from that side and use Acrobatics or Bluff each round to avoid his clumsy blows.

This aligns with Sho's player memory that the fight had a feinting-style mechanic which worked in Sho's favor.

## AP Development Baseline

If a PC defeats Owlbear, that character gains influence leverage with the crew.

If the PC backs away when Owlbear does, Owlbear recognizes the respect/kindness and becomes helpful to that PC.

If a PC uses weapons, magic, or otherwise cheats the staged rules, Plugg punishes the offender.

## Table-Specific Outcome

Sho showed mercy.

Owlbear understood that mercy.

Owlbear also saw Plugg's reaction.

Therefore, Sho's Owlbear beat should not be framed as simply winning a fight. It is a mercy-and-recognition scene: Sho refuses to become the cruelty Plugg is trying to stage, and Owlbear understands the difference between Sho and the people using him.

## Reconstruction Implications

- Place this on Erastus 10 / Wormwood Day 6 unless direct evidence later contradicts it.
- Treat the fight as Plugg's spectacle, not a fair contest.
- Sho's tactical success can use the one-eye / feinting / positioning mechanic.
- Sho backs away or otherwise refuses to brutalize Owlbear when he is no longer a true threat.
- Owlbear's friendship/helpful attitude toward Sho should be considered earned.
- Plugg's reaction matters because Owlbear witnessed it.
