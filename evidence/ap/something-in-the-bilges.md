## AP Baseline Shape

The AP places this set-piece on the fourth day of the Wormwood voyage.

The structure is:

- PCs should not be assigned ordinary bilge duty as their regular job that day.
- Around mid-morning, Jack Scrimshaw rushes onto deck after rat-catching duty below.
- Jack is visibly frightened and reports that something large came out of the bilge water and bit him.
- Plugg looks for a way to send the PCs below and orders them to deal with whatever is in the bilges.
- The infestation consists of six dire rats.
- The rats came from a bad / cheap consignment of barley picked up in Port Peril.
- The bilges are brackish, very hot, cramped, foul, and difficult terrain for clean visibility.
- The water gives the rats concealment and makes the fight feel more dangerous and chaotic than the creatures alone would suggest.
- After the rats are dealt with, the PCs have the chance to search or recover gear in the bilges if the narrative needs it.
- If the PCs delay too long, fail to finish the job, or allow the rats to keep attacking crew, Plugg has an excuse for lashes.

## Retcon / Alignment Handling

Use the prior Rak / Grok bilges warning as a usable setup rather than treating it as an error.

Current alignment model:

- Jack's bite and fear happened the day before the bilges assignment was forced on the PCs.
- The officers did not make a public fuss at first.
- Plugg later decided the problem could be turned into a useful punishment or test.
- Plugg chose Rak and Sho because both had become visible troublemakers after the rum punishment and public challenge.
- Scourge jumped at the opportunity to add Harvus to the list.
- Do not frame the assignment as a fair call for volunteers.
- Do not imply the officers are trying to protect the crew. They are using the problem.
- Do not make Grok the cause of the assignment. Grok can have warned Rak because she heard enough to know bilges trouble was coming.

## Confirmed Creature / Hazard Elements

- Six dire rats.
- The rats came from a bad / cheap barley consignment from Port Peril.
- The bilges are hot, cramped, foul, and partially flooded.
- Rats use the water and clutter for concealment.
- PCs may be bitten several times.
- The fight can be hand-waved but should feel claustrophobic, ugly, and more epic than a simple rat cleanup.
- The PCs kill all six rats.

## Narrative Implications

This should not read as ordinary pest control.

It should read as:

- a delayed consequence of lower-ship danger;
- Plugg weaponizing an existing hazard;
- Scourge making sure the danger reaches the people he wants hurt;
- Rak, Sho, and Harvus being pushed into the hottest, foulest part of the ship after the previous day's public punishment;
- the Wormwood using even its rats as part of its discipline system.
