# AP Scaffold Summary — Man's Promise Whispers, Storm, and Bonewrack Transition

## Summary

The published Man's Promise voyage uses a short escalation structure between the prize-crew transfer and Bonewrack Isle.

### Day 2 — Whispers of Mutiny

Friendly or sympathetic NPCs begin quietly warning the PCs that Plugg may not intend to obey Harrigan's stated orders.

The stated public course is Port Peril, but rumors grow that Plugg may claim the Man's Promise for himself and attempt to become a pirate captain in his own right.

Those rumors strengthen once the course change is noticed and the ship is understood to be heading east toward Bloodcove.

If the PCs do not raise mutiny as an option themselves, friendly NPCs begin to raise it when timing allows.

During the evening, Kroop can suggest the likely destination: Rickety's Squibs, an isolated dry dock west of Bloodcove on the Slithering Coast. Rickety Hake can refit ships into "squibs," altering the ship's profile and details so former owners and observers no longer recognize it.

### Day 4 — A Storm and a Slithering Host

A storm strikes the Man's Promise in the early hours of the fourth day.

The wind drives the Man's Promise far off course. The ship is tossed hard on the high seas, and all hands are called to work on deck, including crew who would not normally be in the rigging.

The published scaffold assigns PCs random rigger tasks and increases all such task DCs by +2.

Toward dawn, as the sky begins to brighten, the storm redoubles. Dark shapes loom out of the sea around the ship: small islets and coral reefs around Bonewrack Isle.

The storm is a powerful storm with strong winds. Visibility is effectively near zero, making Perception checks almost impossible. The stormy water requires DC 20 Swim checks.

Unknown to the crew, grindylows from Bonewrack Isle approach the beleaguered ship. They use the storm and visibility collapse to climb aboard and quietly take sailors. In the AP baseline, two sailors are taken before the crew clearly understands what is happening.

The successful kidnapping emboldens the attackers. Six additional grindylows board in search of more prey, encounter the PCs, and attack.

The grindylows attempt to trip opponents and knock them overboard into the stormy water. Once half of them are slain, the survivors flee back over the side and vanish into the storm.

### Day 5 — Shipwrecked

As the storm begins to abate, the Man's Promise hits the coral reefs around Bonewrack Isle and runs aground.

At sunrise and low tide, the crew recognizes the predicament.

The published damage state:

- The Man's Promise is holed in the main hold on the starboard side near the ship's water barrel.
- A DC 15 Craft (ships), Profession (engineer), or Profession (sailor) check can determine that the ship is not in immediate danger of sinking.
- However, the ship will eventually sink unless repaired, even if the bilge pumps are operated around the clock.
- Plugg orders crew to begin dismantling the ship's deck to repair the vessel.

Early in the morning, Plugg calls roll.

The AP baseline says two sailors are discovered missing:

- Sandara Quinn.
- One other pirate chosen from among those most friendly to the PCs.

If the PCs search for clues, a DC 15 Perception check or DC 20 Survival check can find signs of where grindylows climbed aboard, along with Sandara's holy symbol of Besmara on deck, dropped when she was captured.

## Campaign Mapping

The current table reconstruction maps this scaffold to:

- Erastus 29: course-change warning, crew whispers, and Kroop identifying Rickety's Squibs.
- Erastus 30: a harsh but normal day of sailing, including Plugg's Mistress Mirei surveillance experiment and Sandara's attempt to obtain more proof of destination.
- Erastus 31: a large sudden storm blasts the Man's Promise, forces all hands to keep the ship afloat, tosses the ship in the waves, and drives it southwest off course.
- Erastus 31 storm attack: the GM followed the AP almost beat-for-beat; Tilly Brackett and Sandara Quinn are the two sailors taken first, then the grindylows attack openly.
- Arodus 1: the Man's Promise is blown onto a reef on the shore of an unknown island.
- Arodus 1 assessment: the fresh water is gone and the ship will take two days to repair.
- Arodus 1 late evening: a team is sent onto the island to search for food and water.
- Arodus 1 night: after making camp, the island team is attacked by ghouls.
- Later island period: Goyle Onórach is captured in a separate subsequent grindylow attack aboard the Man's Promise while the PCs are away on the island. Plugg washes him overboard with the Tidal Cutlass while Goyle is fighting a boarder.
