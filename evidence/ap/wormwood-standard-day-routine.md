## Standard Day Structure

Each standard day aboard the Wormwood before the Man's Promise fight follows a routine pattern.

## Work

The PCs spend the day at their assigned jobs.

This routine becomes the baseline pressure of the Wormwood: labor, fatigue, job checks, supervision, and the practical reality that the ship owns the characters' daylight hours.

## Bloody Hour

At dusk, the crew gathers on deck to witness punishments.

On the first night, this is demonstrated by the keelhauling of **Jakes Magpie**, a pirate caught stealing from the quartermaster's store the night before.

After a brief interrogation by **Mr. Plugg** and **Master Scourge**, Jakes admits his crime. He is brought up from the bilges to be slowly keelhauled before the evening meal.

The players notice the relish with which Plugg, in particular, tugs at the rope as Magpie is dragged under the ship.

What appears on the other side has been cut to shreds and is soon thrown overboard to feed the sharks.

## Leisure

After dinner and the rum ration, various pirate entertainments take place on deck or below for as long as people are sober enough to participate.

This is the space where influence, gambling, music, information, intimidation, and theft/recovery attempts happen.

## Rest

Provided they drink their rum rations, the PCs can retire to their hammocks in the lower hold whenever they wish.

If they have the energy, they can take additional nighttime ship actions.
