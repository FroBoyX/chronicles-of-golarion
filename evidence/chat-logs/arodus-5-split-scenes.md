1. Harvus, Mirei, Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle after the Riptide Cove rescue.

3. Sho locked aboard the Man's Promise and confronted by Kahina Bukekayo.

## Scene 1 — Post-Riptide Rescue

### Tilly Awakens

Tilly coughs and thanks Harvus, saying she did not think they would make it.

Harvus asks how she is and whether she can walk.

Tilly recognizes him in the cast light and asks how he found them.

She says she thinks she can walk and jokes weakly that she swam.

### Sandara's Condition

Tilly kneels next to Sandara and says Sandara took the worst of it.

Tilly says Sandara gave the grindylows hell when they were knocked off.

Sandara later coughs up water and breathes heavily but remains passed out or mostly unresponsive.

### Tilly's Concern for Goyle

Tilly says there was a man with them, one of the Man's Promise guards, and asks whether he made it.

Harvus fears Davy Jones may have taken him, but Tilly insists they have to check because he helped her through the last day.

Tilly weakens and Harvus catches her.

### Goyle Surfaces

Goyle surfaces coughing and angry, insisting he is no one's supper while peeling a tentacle from his arm.

Harvus helps him ashore.

Goyle asks how they found the captives and notes Harvus and Mirei should be back at the boat, but he is glad they found them first.

### Crew Framing

Harvus says they caught sign of the captives and that he was not about to leave anyone behind.

He says: "We are crew."

This is an important legitimacy line.

### Tilly and Goyle

Tilly limps over, says she is glad Goyle is okay, glomps him, and sinks against his chest.

Goyle reassures her that he told her they would figure it out, then asks about the priestess.

### Goyle's Warning

Goyle asks whether they plan to go back to the cursed ship.

He says they will not receive a warm welcome.

He says he will cast his lot with Harvus' group because he had no love for the other bastards.

He explains he was knocked from the ship about a day after the party set out.

The tentacled creatures attacked again while Goyle was grappling with one.

Goyle says Plugg took both him and the grindylow out with the sword, blasting them off the ship.

He calls Plugg no pirate captain and says he has no honor.

### The Missing Two

Goyle asks why there were only two rescuers when he thought there were four.

Mirei says the other two went back to look for the ship.

Harvus clarifies that Rak and Sho went back to report findings of food and water while Harvus and Mirei came to rescue the captives.

Goyle curses and says if they were friends, pray they have Besmara's luck.

### Ambush Information

Goyle says before Plugg knocked him into the water with the little water sword, he overheard them talking.

They planned to kill the PCs as soon as they got back, saying some of them were more trouble than they were worth.

He describes it as an ambush.

Mirei asks whether it meant all of them and who said it.

Goyle says he was not around long enough for details, but they expected the PCs to die if the island did not kill them first.

Tilly asks whether they expected the group to die.

### Mirei Confesses

Mirei says this was supposed to be a one-way trip for most of them.

Goyle points out that if everyone was expected to die, he could not figure out who would report back.

Mirei says they had tasked her with killing them all to prove she was on their side, but she did not do it because it did not feel right.

Tilly reacts with shock.

Goyle says Mirei is as good as dead and that the other two might already be dead.

The group agrees to make haste.

Harvus carries Sandara.

## Scene 2 — Scourge Orders the Supply Party

At 11:45am, Scourge questions Rak as "Master cook" about food and water.

Rak explains that the only path they saw led to a broken bridge they had to climb over.

Scourge changes the plan:

- Slippery Syl will lead the team to recover food.
- The ship will set sail and meet them on the south beach for coconuts.
- They will not stop at the caverns.
- Those in the caverns are lost if they cannot make it back.
- Syl is to take Jape, Shivikah, and Fipps.
- They must bring water from the spring down to the beach and recover as much food as possible.

This establishes the supply-party mission and the ship's intended south-beach rendezvous.

## Scene 3 — The Mire / Broken Bridge

Rak leads the group to the broken bridge and explains they can jump across posts or climb through trees.

Syl orders him to lead, because he has crossed before.

Rak says "PARKOUR" and then falls into the water, calling for help.

Syl calls him a stupid snake.

Fipps notices something in the water.

### Frog Fight

Shivikah dives in and stabs the frog.

Rak praises her.

Fipps is attacked by another frog and begins flailing for help.

Syl moves across the pillars and throws an axe at the frog.

A frog bites Rak in the neck and wraps its tongue around him.

Syl leaps from a post and slashes with her cutlass.

Shivikah cuts Rak free from the tongue.

Fipps continues screaming and flailing.

Jape struggles to fight in the water.

Fipps accidentally stabs a frog while flailing.

Jape finally swims well enough to land the final hit, then starts to sink or get stuck.

### Aftermath

Shivikah sneers at Rak after helping him, saying she expected a little more from him.

Syl orders the group to keep moving and warns Rak that things had better match his description better because he did not mention frogs.

## Scene 4 — Botflies / Formation Discipline

Rak wants the group to move quickly toward the burning tree.

Shivikah asks whether the sound ahead is the swarms Rak mentioned.

Rak says they should probably run faster.

Syl stops the group, calls Rak back, and says they are going as a group because she needs him alive.

She makes clear that if she did not need him alive, he would already be dead.

She produces flasks and hands two to each party member.

She orders careful formation:

- Fipps in the middle.
- Jape in the lead.
- Shivikah guarding Fipps.
- Rak in the rear.

This shows Syl as hostile but tactically organized.

## Scene 5 — Burning Tree and Cornfield Tension

At 12:28pm, the group reaches the burned tree.

Syl asks what happened there.

Rak answers with "Alchemy" and hissing.

Syl refuses to stop, orders double time, retrieves the flasks, and puts Rak in the lead again.

At around 1:30pm, Rak suggests resting because the cornfields ahead were not investigated and may be dangerous.

Syl refuses to rest until they make the stronghold.

Rak argues that he knows the island is dangerous and they should be cautious.

Fipps is visibly out of breath and sits, glad for the pause.

Syl moves her hand to her dagger and tells Rak she needs him alive, not his opinions.

Rak says if she needs him alive, he will go cautiously.

Jape is disinterested in the exchange.

Shivikah looks nervous.

Syl threatens insubordination and says if Rak wants to keep his weapon, he should follow orders.

She catches up and snarls that she will be right behind him and expects him to keep pace.

## Scene 6 — Coconut Beach and Fipps' Separation

At 2:15pm, the group passes Coconut Beach.

Rak gives a tour-guide style note that to the left is a beach of coconut trees.

Syl says they are going to the stronghold and that the ship should be meeting them here soon.

Syl orders Fipps to stay there.

Everyone else pushes on.

At 2:25pm, the group continues past Coconut Beach.

This resolves why Fipps is at the beach: Syl leaves him there because the ship is expected to meet at the beach, likely as beach-side contact, guard, or marker while the rest continue.

## Scene 7 — Sho and Kahina

Sho hears her guard hand off after being replaced.

Sho calls out to ask whether anyone is there.

Kahina Bukekayo, recognized as one of the Man's Promise guards, comes around the corner.

Sho asks what is going on aboard the ship.

Kahina says they have set sail for the far shore to take on supplies, then leave. Destination unknown.

Sho asks whether Kahina has other duties beyond guarding her.

Kahina says no one needs two guards because Sho is not going anywhere and notes Jake mentioned Sho had already strained against the chains.

### Kahina's Accusation

Kahina says Sho will share the fate of her crew.

She says Sho killed her kin not long ago and now will be killed by her pirate friends.

Sho says she regrets what happened on the Man's Promise, admits Kahina may not believe her, and asks why Kahina would blindly follow those in command of such a massacre.

Kahina reacts with anger at being accused of blindness.

She turns the charge back on Sho, asking how long Sho blindly followed these men before finding herself chained.

She says more than thirty people died and Sho led a group onto the ship.

Kahina whispers that Plugg and Scourge will find the justice of the sea, as will Sho.

### Sho's Offer

Sho says she feels remorse each day, tried and failed to control those on the boat, and is sorry.

She says all she can do is try to live, escape, and kill the cretins in charge.

She asks Kahina to help her, and says if they succeed, Kahina can decide what to do with Sho afterward.

### Kahina's Test

Kahina says Sho's words mean nothing.

But because Sho is dead anyway, if she truly repents, would she kill for it? Would she kill those who torment them? Would she lose her life to end theirs?

Sho says she will do everything in her power and gives her word.

Kahina corrects her: not everything in her power. Their lives, or Sho's.

If it comes to it and Sho hesitates, Kahina will ensure Sho dies for her sins.

Sho swallows hard and says understood.

Kahina tells her to stay put with almost a hint of jest, then walks away.

## Scene 8 — Final Approach to the Fork

At 2:27pm, the survivors stop to rest at their last checkpoint and glare at approaching botflies.

At 2:28pm, they continue on the path.

Rak hears something ahead.

This is the current fork collision point.
