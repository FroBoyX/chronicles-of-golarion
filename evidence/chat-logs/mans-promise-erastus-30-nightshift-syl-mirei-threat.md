## Extracted Facts

- Tam called after Mirei from below decks, continuing the frankness exchange: "As long as we are being frank. Good evening mistress."
- Fipps ordered the nightshift into the rigging because the wind was picking up.
- Syl stopped Mirei on the stairs up from below deck.
- Syl pressed a knife into Mirei's back.
- Syl said Mirei did not look or sound like a pirate mistress.
- Syl threatened to cut out Mirei's vocal folds and said Mirei would probably whimper about the same.
- Syl said Plugg told her to see how Mirei "handled" herself.
- Syl judged that Mirei had not handled herself well.
- Syl questioned Mirei's whispers with "the monk," meaning Sho, and said Scourge would have a field day with her.
- Mirei answered that Plugg had told her to talk to the crew and that her business with Sho was only that.
- Mirei pointed out that she had spoken to more than Sho.
- Syl clarified that Plugg had told Mirei to find out where people stood, and had told Syl to observe Mirei.
- Syl said she was not impressed.
- Mirei maintained that she had done nothing but what Plugg asked.
- Syl threatened that tonight would likely be Mirei's last and that she and Scourge would see Mirei in the morning.
- Syl dug the knife in enough to draw blood.
- Mirei denied playing a game and pushed back, saying that if she were lying, Plugg would not be trusting her with what he had.
- Syl stepped back and recognized that Mirei had "some fire" and "a spark of life behind the farce."
- Syl contrasted Mirei's cabin civility and docility with the way she now tried to bark.
- Syl said she did not bark with words; she barked with knives, and did not fetch bones, she made them.
- Syl spat and left, still calling Mirei Mistress.

## Reconstruction Use

This scene shows that Plugg's use of Mirei as Mistress creates another hostile observer: Syl.

Syl is not merely jealous or dismissive. She has been formally tasked by Plugg to observe how Mirei handles the crew. She watches Mirei's conversation with Sho as suspicious and reports or threatens to route that suspicion through Scourge.

Mirei's refusal to report Tam to Plugg does not protect her from the system, because Syl observes and reports instead.

The scene also shows Mirei push back more forcefully than she had in the cabin. Syl reads that as a spark of fire behind the farce.
