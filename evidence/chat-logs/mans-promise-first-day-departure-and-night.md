## Extracted Facts

- Early departure: Rak attempted to warn Nor'gal and Merchiel that Plugg and the officers were not kind men.
- Nor'gal was visibly shaking with anger and threatened Rak.
- Merchiel physically held Nor'gal back and judged the confrontation not worth it.
- Nor'gal rejected Rak's warning because Plugg / Harrigan's order had given the survivors a chance at life, while Nor'gal saw Rak as giving only death.
- First evening: Sho was catching rats when she spoke with Sandara.
- Sandara counseled not judging a situation before getting the lay of the land / water.
- Sandara believed they were heading back to Port Peril and might get out fairly unscathed.
- Scourge announced a stricter Man's Promise regime: no rum rations, nobody but nightshift above deck, no entertainment, berths after supper, harsh punishment.
- Scourge ordered Mirei to report to the captain with her instrument as entertainment.
- Harvus tried to engage Crimson in war stories; Crimson refused friendship but became civil for a story.
- Rak had lent Mirei a fiddle during dinner so she could perform for Plugg.
- Crimson reacted to Harvus' flail violence and said they might talk more in the future, but still left for bed.
- Sho heard the conversations and sighed deeply before trying to sleep.
- Mirei performed for Plugg on borrowed fiddle and rolled a 32.
- Plugg offered Mirei a hammock in his cabin area, explicitly separating her from the crew below.
- Plugg's weapon check / pat-down was coercive and physically intimidating; he pushed Mirei against the bulkhead and whispered in her ear.
- Plugg took a pouch but let Mirei keep a potion.
- Plugg promised that if Mirei kept him entertained, her life would improve.
- Scourge interpreted or implied agreement beyond just a hammock; Mirei clarified that the only thing she agreed to was the hammock.
- Plugg set strict rules: Mirei could not touch anything without permission, must play for him each night, and could not speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.
- Owlbear functioned as Plugg's door guard / cabin guard and did not initiate cruelty toward Mirei.
- Syl demanded attention below decks and called for five nightwatch volunteers, but immediately treated people as volunteered.
- Fipps was named boatswain for nightshift.
- Harvus and Ebony were assigned to rigging.
- Sho and Goyle were assigned as swabs to clean the hold from the fire.
- Goyle identified himself directly to Sho as former quartermaster of the Man's Promise and told her to follow his lead.
- Fipps gave nightshift orders slowly and harshly.
