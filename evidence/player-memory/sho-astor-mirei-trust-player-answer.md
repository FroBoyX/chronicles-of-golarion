## Raw Player Answers

### 1. When Mirei noticed Sho's disguise, did Sho feel exposed, threatened, relieved, ashamed, or something else?

> Exposed for sure, she was worried that if Mirei had noticed, other people would have as well, and the potential for treating her differently or lesser would be high. But after speaking with her about it, the feelings changed to being able to relax around someone for the first time in a long while, and be herself.

### 2. Did Sho try to deny it, or did she admit the truth once she realized Mirei knew?

> She did try to deny it at first, but when the jig was up, she didn't see a reason to keep up the charade, plus having someone to be an outlet for being herself to help relieve a lot of the tension she had been feeling up to that point.

### 3. Did Mirei keeping the secret change how Sho experienced life aboard the Wormwood?

> It really did. Like someone knew what she was, and could confide in, rather than needing to be bold, strong, and righteous all the time.

### 4. Before Mirei became closed off, how did Sho see her?

> Kind and maybe even a bit more sheltered than herself. She had no idea what Mirei went through during that time, so her thoughts may change on that, if that were the case.

### 5. When Mirei became more distant or closed off, did Sho feel worried, rejected, suspicious, or confused?

> I think Sho felt confusion, she couldn't quite put together why the sudden change happened, but was hoping that it was just something temporary, and that things would go back.

### 6. Did Sho try to ask what was happening, or was survival pressure too heavy by then?

> I think Sho may have been too isolated and embattled to really try and tackle what was going on with Mirei at the time. Each day was a new challenge to survive and tackle, and sadly the more quieter moments seemed to have less and less chance of happening.
