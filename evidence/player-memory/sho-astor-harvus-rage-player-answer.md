## Raw Player Answers

### 1. Before the Man's Promise assault, did Sho see Harvus as honorable, volatile, protective, dangerous, wounded, or something else?

> Before the assault on the Man’s Promise, Sho saw Harvus as a kindred spirit, someone who could fight back against the bullshit of their situation, and could hopefully be trusted to help should the situation arise.

### 2. Did Sho believe Harvus could control his rage when it mattered?

> She had hoped he could before the Man’s Promise, but afterwards she severely doubted it. She could get angry, but even she could tell what was right from wrong in her anger. Harvus’ blindness to this worried her.

### 3. When Harvus killed people Sho meant to spare, did that feel different from Rak's violence, or part of the same failure of command?

> It did feel different. Rak’s violence felt like malice, whilst Harvus’ felt like anger lashing out at any perceived threat. Regardless, she felt powerless to stop it, and she hated both in that moment.

### 4. Did Sho think Harvus understood why surrendered enemies mattered?

> I think she feels he did AFTER the fact, but was too prideful to admit it.

### 5. When Harvus vowed blood against Scourge after learning what happened to Sho, did that make Sho feel protected, alarmed, guilty, or responsible?

> Alarmed I think. I think Sho wasn't expecting such a reaction from someone who had so willingly killed others, and especially not in relation to being in her honor.

### 6. Does Sho believe Harvus' rage can be directed toward justice, or does she fear it will become another version of the Wormwood's violence?

> Sho hopes she can direct Harvus’ rage into noble goals. He’s mad mostly about the right things, he just needs to direct it at those who deserve it, and avoid the collateral damage along the way.

### 7. What would Harvus have to do for Sho to fully trust him?

> Admit that his anger has hurt/killed people that didn't deserve it, and vow to try to do better.

## Extracted Sho-Character Truth

- Sho was not expecting such a reaction from someone who had willingly killed others, especially not in relation to defending her honor.
- Sho hopes Harvus' rage can be directed toward noble goals.
