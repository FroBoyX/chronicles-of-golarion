## Disguise and Exposure

### Raw Player Answer

```text
Sho disguised herself as a man as a mixture of things. It was a bit for safety, (people tended to mess with her less/try to take advantage of her lack of knowledge when she presented as masculine) monastery habit, (They monastery has very gender neutral clothing, more for function than anything) and partially because she doesn't want to dress in anything fancy for anyone other than Agatha when she sees her again.
It was a bit of tension at first, but it quickly turned to trust when Mirei showed that she could and would keep the secret.
She did ask if Mirei would keep it secret, to which Mirei agreed.
Well, none of the player characters knew, but I have to imagine if the ones who press ganged her specifically went through her belongings, they would have had to have figured it out. So possibly select members of the Wyrmwood.
I think she would still try to maintain it a bit just for some level of control, but if confronted about it, I think she’d admit who and what she is.
```

### Extracted Facts

- Sho's male presentation / disguise is a mixture of safety, habit, and emotional self-control.
- Safety: people tended to mess with her less or exploit her ignorance less when she presented as masculine.
- Habit: Sho's monastery used very gender-neutral clothing focused on function.
- Agatha: Sho does not want to dress in anything fancy for anyone other than Agatha when she sees her again.
- Mirei noticing created tension at first.
- Trust formed quickly once Mirei showed she could and would keep the secret.
- Sho directly asked Mirei to keep the secret, and Mirei agreed.
- None of the player characters knew at first.
- The press-gang members who searched Sho's belongings may have figured it out, so select Wormwood crew may have known.
- Sho would still try to maintain the presentation for some level of control.
- If directly confronted, Sho would admit who and what she is.

## Jakes Magpie and the First Warning

### Raw Player Answer

```text
1.) 100% Righteous anger. Sho was many things, but stubborn is VERY much one of them. She was just pissed, and would continue to lash out. 
2.) Not even slightly. Someone had to say something, and she was definitely gonna be it. 
3.) In her head, she can tell herself she should be more cautious, but the moment the crew acts like their actions are normal and justified, her blood gets boiling and she can’t stop herself. 
4.) Nope, The ones who did the deed were the ones to blame, and them alone.  
5.) Oh absolutely. Plug and Scourge are scum who delight in the malice of these kinds of acts and need to be taken down. Harrigan seemed to be the same at first, but due to his later actions, seems to pose a level of grey that confuses her.
```

### Extracted Facts

- Sho's reaction to Jakes Magpie's keelhauling was righteous anger, not strategy.
- Sho is stubborn and would continue to lash out when morally enraged.
- Sho does not regret speaking up. Someone had to say something, and she was going to be the person who did.
- Sho can intellectually tell herself she should be cautious, but when the crew acts as if cruelty is normal and justified, her blood boils and she cannot stop herself.
- Sho does not blame herself for any consequences of speaking out. She blames the people who actually committed the act.
- Sho sees Plugg and Scourge as scum who delight in malicious acts and need to be taken down.
- Sho initially saw Harrigan in a similar light, but later Harrigan actions created ambiguity or grey that confuses her.

## Reconstruction Use

Use this as character-truth support for Sho's early defiance, her repeated inability to stay quiet around normalized cruelty, and her complicated later view of Harrigan.

This also clarifies the disguise thread: Sho's presentation is not only fear or shame. It is also practicality, monastery habit, a boundary around Agatha, and a means of retaining control.
