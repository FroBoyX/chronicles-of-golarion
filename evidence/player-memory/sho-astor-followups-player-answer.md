## Extracted Sho-Character Truth

### Harrigan

- Harrigan became morally grey to Sho primarily through his reward to her and by protecting her from Plugg.
- Harrigan's battle competence may have mattered, but mostly as proof that he had the power to enact his will.
- Sho noticed or believed that some crew, possibly Peppery and possibly Kroop / the cook, genuinely respected Harrigan rather than only fearing him.
- This complicates Harrigan for Sho but does not make him safe, good, or exempt from judgment.
- If Harrigan continues plundering innocent ships and Sho has the opportunity and ability to stop him, Sho will do so.

### Gender / Privacy

- The information Mirei discovered and Sho eventually admitted was specifically gender-related.
- Other private information remains strictly private and should not be treated as revealed through this scene.

### Mirei Scene Placement and Tone

- Sho remembers Mirei confronting her quietly and directly.
- Mirei took Sho aside during one of the dinners and let Sho know she knew.
- Sho initially denied it.
- Mirei gave Sho a look that effectively communicated disbelief, after which Sho owned up to it.
- Sho demanded that Mirei not tell anyone.
- Mirei seemed confused but agreed.
- The two then had a quiet dinner together.
- The player thinks this may have happened around Day 2, but is not certain.
- The later loud whipping cries / possible exposure scene remains unclear, and the player does not remember significant follow-up interaction from most others after that point.

### Peppery

- Peppery's advice to Sho was to choose her battles.
- The advice was not to pick up every cause, but to pick battles she could win and choose some battles not to fight so she could fight another day.
- Sho's restraint from attacking Scourge on the island was partly influenced by Peppery's advice.
- Sho also read Kahina's look as signaling that striking then would be foolish and that a better opportunity would arise.
- Sho sees a minor connection between Peppery and Agatha because both are confident and capable women.
- Agatha remains much deeper and stronger for Sho than the brief Peppery connection.

### Amulet / Cloak

- Sho most likely keeps Harrigan's amulet stashed away with her reversible cloak.
- This links the amulet to Sho's hidden/selfhood gear rather than public display.

### Warning Harrigan

- Sho believes she warned Harrigan instinctually, without fully accounting for who he was.
- She simply did not want to see another person taken out.
- The raw answer is truncated after "worked out both unfortunately and fortu" and should not be overinterpreted beyond that.
