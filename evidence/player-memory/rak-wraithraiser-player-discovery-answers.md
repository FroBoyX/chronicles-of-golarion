## Core / Early Wormwood

- Rak came to Port Peril chasing wealth and glory. Family legacy mattered, but his personal goal was to make a name for himself as a pirate.
- Rak took his infamous Free Captain ancestor as a mix of destiny and brag.
- Besmara was probably more pirate culture than deep personal faith at this early stage.
- Cook's mate was mostly the job Rak got stuck with, but it was tolerable because alchemy and cooking both involve following recipes.
- Kroop began as Rak's boss and became more of a mentor / drinking buddy over time.
- Early Rak valued usefulness and shared survival first. Loyalty might develop later, but early Wormwood Rak was focused on surviving long enough to make a name for himself.
- The early galley / alcohol / Grok loop began as fun and entertainment.

## Grok / Kroop / Crew Ties

- Grok initially mattered because Rak wanted a connection to someone slightly higher up for gear, protection, and survival.
- Rak and Grok's connection began strategic, then became casual fun. It is not currently player-confirmed as a deep romance.
- The closeness with Grok was mostly quiet. Other crew may have known they were friendly, but not exactly how close.
- Kroop looked sad or pathetic to Rak because of the drinking and avoidance of responsibility, but Kroop was useful in keeping Rak alive.
- Badger, Tilly, and Sandara being marked helpful likely means they personally helped Rak at some point, not merely that they were broadly group-helpful.
- Jack, Patch, and Tate were mostly background crew from Rak's perspective, with too little direct interaction for strong opinions.
- Maheem's kill-list note remains unresolved. The player does not remember the exact cause, but frames it as likely something Maheem said or did that Rak took serious offense to. Since ordinary hostile knife-threatening behavior did not put Syl/Patch on that list, Maheem's offense should remain sharper than background hostility.

## Violence / Mercy / Sho

- Rak saw violence aboard the Wormwood as pirate work. Given his pirate-family background, violence was just part of being on a pirate ship.
- Rak has no general line against killing surrendered, downed, or non-immediate-threat enemies if he considers them actual enemies or likely future threats.
- If Rak is neutral toward someone, mercy is possible. If someone is an enemy / hostile / future threat, Rak is inclined to eliminate them.
- Rak understood why Sho objected to his violence, but considered Sho naive / soft for a pirate-ship context.
- If Sho confronted Rak about going too far, Rak would likely laugh it off rather than seriously reconsider.
- Rak wanted the others to find him useful in the short term. Later, he wanted that usefulness to become respect as they understood his strength and ability.

## Harvus / Mirei / Crew-Bond Direction

- Harvus was useful to Rak because of his fighting prowess, though Rak thought Harvus could be too serious.
- Rak trusted Harvus in a fight. Outside a fight, the player does not clearly remember, but Rak likely found Harvus straightforward and not especially likely to trick him.
- Rak did not have much of an early opinion about Mirei beyond the water-selling business partnership.
- Rak did not notice anything strange about Mirei's position with Plugg.
- Rak decides someone is "his people" through time spent working together and building trust.
- For Rak, loyalty to real crew means honesty, trust, shared profit among those at the top, protection of crewmates, and revenge if someone harms them.
- Relationship edges around who exactly counts as Rak's crew at the current fork should be allowed to evolve through play rather than being fully locked now.

## Bonewrack Split / Current Rak Posture

- Rak is traveling with Syl, Shivikah, and Jape in survive / look-for-an-opening mode.
- Rak sees Syl mostly as an enemy.
- Rak currently treats Shivikah as hostile crew by default, but if she reads as another unfortunate press-ganged survivor rather than truly Plugg-aligned, he could see her somewhat more positively.
- Rak sees Jape as a threat, likely because of his association with Plugg and obedience to Plugg's orders.
- Fipps is not salient to Rak from the player's memory; effectively "who dat" unless play makes him matter.
- Rak's current priority at the fork is to find the others so they can take out the hostile escort and potentially take over the Man's Promise while the heavy hitters are absent.
- If Rak hears something ahead, his instinct is to let Syl walk into it first and hope whatever it is weakens or removes the hostile escort.

## Rak Voice Correction

Player/GM correction:

- Drop the surfer affect. Do not write Rak with "sup," "brah," or California beach-stereotype cadence.
- Rak should still have a mouth that can get him in trouble.
- He should retain a nonchalant lack of caring about danger, authority, threats, and social consequences.
- His humor should come from pirate-alchemist arrogance, bad timing, reptilian oddness, and lack of social caution, not beach-dude slang.
- Preserve table-joke bits: "PARKOUR," "halp!", and occasional prolonged "Ssssss" hissing.
- Use hissing sparingly so it remains texture rather than a gimmick.

Working voice target:

> Rak is mouthy, irreverent, casually provocative, and often relaxed at the worst possible moment. He is not a surfer clown. He is a dangerous, practical, glory-hungry pirate-alchemist whose indifference to social consequences can make situations worse.
