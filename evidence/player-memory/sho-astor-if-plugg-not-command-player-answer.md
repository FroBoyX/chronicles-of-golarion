## Raw Player Answers

### 1. If Plugg is not in command, what does Sho imagine the Man's Promise could become?

> It may be a lofty goal for the future, but perhaps an anti-pirate ship. One that travels the seas taking out pirates, but isn't associated with any kind of navy or something. Basically trying to prevent future instances of what happened to the Man’s Promise from happening again. 
> It would in the sense that she would hope they would port in a town that she and everyone willing could escape this pirate existence thrust upon them.

### 2. Who would Sho trust in command?

> Kahina maybe, Sindara if she makes it back, other than that, it’d be a matter of seeing the candidates of those left. Definitely NOT Harvus or Rak, and I don't think Mirei would be cut out for it.

### 3. Would Sho see herself as worthy of command?

> Sho doesn't think she’d be worthy for such a thing.

### 4. What rules would Sho insist on?

> No killing innocent people. I feel like that’s an easy one, but apparently hard for some people to follow. (Glares at Harvus and Rak) Take only what’s needed, do NOT leave people without enough to get by and back to a port. And that’s only until we get to port ourselves. Sho does not intend to stick around

### 5. Could the Man's Promise become better depending on who is in charge?

> Depends on who’s left in charge. I would hope that if Kahina or Sindara were left in charge, things would make changes for the better.

### 6. Would Sho want a voice in decisions?

> She would definitely want a voice in what goes on. Better to make sure people dont fall out of line and resort to cruelty.

### 7. Would Sho stay aboard long-term?

> She already doesnt plan to stay. She would likely leave once getting to a major port town. If anything, there would need to be a larger reason needed for her to stay aboard. A larger threat that she felt she needed to handle, or perhaps someone she needed to save.

## Extracted Sho-Character Truth / Future Preference

- Sho can imagine a lofty future where the Man's Promise becomes an anti-pirate ship rather than a pirate ship.
- That imagined future would involve preventing others from suffering what happened to the Man's Promise.
- Sho also sees the more immediate goal as reaching a town or major port where she and anyone willing can escape the pirate existence forced on them.
- Sho might trust Kahina in command.
- Sho might trust Sandara in command if Sandara returns.
- Other candidates would depend on who remains.
- Sho would not trust Harvus or Rak in command.
- Sho does not think Mirei is cut out for command.
- Sho does not think she herself is worthy of command.
- Sho would insist on no killing innocent people.
- Sho would insist that any taking be limited to what is needed and that victims not be left without enough to survive and reach port.
- Sho frames those rules as temporary survival conduct until they themselves reach port, not as an ongoing pirate code.
- Sho would want a voice in decisions to prevent cruelty and keep people from falling out of line.
- Sho does not intend to stay aboard long-term.
- Sho would likely leave once the ship reached a major port town.
- Sho might stay only for a larger reason: a larger threat she felt she needed to handle, or someone she needed to save.
