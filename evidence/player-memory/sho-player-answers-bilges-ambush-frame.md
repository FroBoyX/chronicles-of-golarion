## Raw Player Answer

```text
Bilges Ambush and the Attempted Frame
1.) I think Sho sees the ambush as an attempted murder first, and then setup upon the botched murder attempt. 
2.) She was in full survival mode. She was genuinely terrified she would die down there and was not pulling any punches (or kicks) to make it out alive. 
3.) Definitely a bit of all. Guilt that she let herself get so panicked that she went all out, discipline/mercy that she should still do the right thing and make sure they didn't die, and fear that she would be held accountable if they DID die. 
4.) I wanna say it was at first something she felt guilt over, before quickly realizing that she had been set up, and rather than going down to help, they had instead gone down to frame her. 
5.) If she thought she hated them before, she didn't know how much her blood could boil at their actions and knew at some point, they needed to be taken down. SHE would take them down. 
6.) I think she’s a bit too laser focused on clearing her name and taking down the guilty party. Bit of the ole tunnel vision.
```

## Extracted Facts

- Sho sees the bilges ambush first as an attempted murder.
- Sho sees the frame as secondary: once the murder attempt was botched, the situation became a setup / frame.
- During the fight, Sho was in full survival mode.
- Sho was genuinely terrified that she would die in the bilges.
- Sho was not pulling punches or kicks while trying to survive.
- Afterward, Sho's response was mixed: guilt, discipline / mercy, and fear.
- Guilt: she let panic push her into going all-out.
- Discipline / mercy: even after the ambush, she believed she should do the right thing and make sure they did not die.
- Fear: she knew she might be held accountable if they did die.
- At first, Aretta's death was something Sho felt guilt over.
- Sho then quickly realized she had been set up.
- Sho believes the people responsible went down not to help, but to frame her.
- The incident intensified Sho's hatred of Plugg and Scourge beyond what she had previously understood.
- Sho concluded they needed to be taken down and that she herself would take them down.
- Sho became laser-focused on clearing her name and taking down the guilty party.
- This tunnel vision may narrow how much she considers broader consequences or other parties' perspectives in the immediate aftermath.

## Reconstruction Use

Use this to refine the Erastus 23 bilges event from Sho's perspective.

The event should not be framed as Sho simply losing control and killing Aretta. From Sho's character truth, the scene begins as an attempted murder against her. Her violence is survival panic under a real threat. The later frame is not incidental; it becomes part of why Plugg and Scourge move from hated officers to targets Sho believes she must personally bring down.

This also clarifies why the event carries both guilt and rage. Sho feels responsible for going all-out, but she also recognizes the surrounding setup and sees the failure to help as part of the frame.
