## Handling Note

This file preserves Sho's player's answers about the hot-box scene with Kahina. It should be used to define Sho's internal state, not to overdetermine Kahina's future actions.

Important split:

- Sho's interpretation of Kahina as judge and mirror is character truth for Sho.
- Sho's acceptance of Kahina's accusation as fair does not mean Sho accepts all circumstances as fair.
- Sho's repentance is action-focused: trust, cooperation, and doing right moving forward, not forgiveness or undoing the past.
- Sho's willingness to shed blood depends strongly on whose blood is demanded.
- Sho does not feel she deserves forgiveness.

## Raw Player Answers

### 1. When Sho woke in the hot-box and saw Kahina, what was her first emotional state?

> Focused and planning, she was mad she didnt get to hit Scourge, but Kahina’s look definitely gave her the impression that something more was going on, and she needed to be ready. 
> Probably a judge and mirror. Judgement on the actions she was directly responsible for, but also a mirror of what had happened to her to bring her onto the Wyrmwood in the first place.

### 2. Did Kahina's accusation feel fair, unfair, or unbearable to Sho?

> I dont think she was happy about it, but she felt the accusation was a fair one, even if the circumstances were anything but. But it also set her on a path of redemption.

### 3. When Sho agreed to Kahina's life-for-life test, was it justice, survival, rage, repentance, or strategy?

> Justice, rage, and repentance I would say. All in high supply. But also in the back of her mind, survival. She had a promise to keep with Agatha afterall.

### 4. Does Sho believe killing Plugg or Scourge would make anything right, or only stop further harm?

> She doesn't think it will UNDO what has already been done, but it will stop them from doing more, and allow those under their influence to be no longer shackled to them. Additionally, punishment for their deeds must be served.

### 5. What would make Sho hesitate if Kahina demanded blood?

> Depends on who’s blood. Any random crewmember would be a cause for it, Mirei, Sandara, Harvus to a slight degree, but she would understand at the same time. Rak would probably give little to no hesitation though, same with any of Plugg or Scourge’s lackeys.

### 6. Does Sho want forgiveness, trust, cooperation, or only a chance to act?

> I don't think she feels like she deserves forgiveness. She seeks trust and cooperation, and honestly a chance to act and do right moving forward.

## Extracted Sho-Character Truth

- In the hot-box scene, Sho was focused and planning rather than broken or passive.
- Sho was angry that she did not get to hit Scourge.
- Kahina's look made Sho suspect something deeper was happening, so Sho tried to be ready.
- Sho experienced Kahina as both judge and mirror: judge of actions Sho directly participated in or failed to prevent, and mirror of Sho's own press-ganging into the Wormwood.
- Sho was not happy about Kahina's accusation, but considered it fair even though the circumstances were not fair.
- The accusation set Sho on a path of redemption.
- Sho's acceptance of Kahina's test was driven by justice, rage, repentance, and survival.
- Survival remained present partly because Sho still has a promise to keep with Agatha.
- Sho does not believe killing Plugg or Scourge would undo what happened.
- Sho believes killing or punishing Plugg and Scourge can stop future harm and free people under their influence.
- Sho also believes punishment for their deeds must be served.
- Sho's willingness to shed blood depends on whose blood is demanded.
- Random crew would cause hesitation.
- Mirei and Sandara would cause hesitation; Harvus would cause some hesitation, though Sho would understand the moral complication more than with some others.
- Rak would cause little to no hesitation for Sho at this point.
- Plugg, Scourge, and their lackeys would also cause little to no hesitation.
- Sho does not feel she deserves forgiveness.
- Sho seeks trust, cooperation, and a chance to act and do right moving forward.

## Canon / GM Truth Boundaries

- This batch defines Sho's internal state and priorities, not Kahina's hidden motive.
