## Raw Player Answer

```text
Peppery Longfarthing, Attraction, and Authority
1.) I think it was a lot of factors honestly. Confidence based on competence, Peppery commanded respect, and was absolutely capable of acting on anything she set into motion. (Or at least Sho saw it that way) There appeared to be no malice in her actions, even if they were cold. It was all to make sure her captain’s will be done. So the fact that there was a level of trust between Peppery and the Captain made Sho see her as a person as well. Also, girl was mysterious and beautiful on top of all of that, so Sho was definitely a bit smitten. She seems to like the confident and capable type of lady. 
2.) This goes a bit into what I was saying before, but I think she views Peppery as different from Plugg and Scourge. Those two seem to delight in the malice they can cause, where Peppery seems to have more going on. A trust between her and the captain, a cold drive to get things done quickly and efficiently, and possibly more there that Sho couldn't delve out of her. 
3.) Very embarrassed. Girl is a dumb mess about it, and will likely never bring it up to anyone, and will vehemently deny anything about it unless challenged by Peppery herself. 
4.) I think it felt a bit kind, dangerous, and unreadable. Sho didn't get the sense that she was being condescending, but that could be part of her inexperience. 
5.) I think she’d try to take them to heart to a degree, choosing when to act, and to not be as rash with actions. Which would later play out on the island when Scourge accused them of betrayal and she had the opportunity to strike, but it would have been at a great cost. 
6.) I think it did, it kind of made it clear why a mutiny hadn't happened sooner. With people like Peppery and even Harrigan being in control, people fell into line much easier. She didn't like that, but she at least had a better understanding of it.
```

## Extracted Facts

- Sho's attraction to Peppery is grounded in multiple factors:
  - confidence based on competence,
  - Peppery's ability to command respect,
  - Peppery's apparent ability to act on anything she set in motion,
  - Peppery's mystery and beauty,
  - and Sho's broader tendency to be drawn to confident, capable women.
- Sho sees Peppery's actions as cold but not malicious.
- Sho believes Peppery acts to ensure the captain's will is done.
- The trust between Peppery and Harrigan makes Sho see Peppery as more fully a person rather than only a threat or officer.
- Sho views Peppery as different from Plugg and Scourge because Plugg and Scourge seem to delight in malice, while Peppery seems driven by competence, trust, duty, and something Sho cannot fully read.
- Sho is embarrassed by the attraction and would likely never bring it up herself.
- Sho would deny it strongly unless challenged by Peppery directly.
- Peppery's warning felt kind, dangerous, and unreadable to Sho.
- Sho did not read it as condescending, though her inexperience may affect that read.
- Sho would try to take Peppery's warning to heart by choosing when to act and avoiding rash action.
- This later informs Sho's restraint on the island when Scourge accuses the group of betrayal and Sho has a chance to strike, but at high cost.
- Peppery and Harrigan helped Sho understand why mutiny had not happened sooner: people with that level of authority and competence make others fall in line.
- Sho does not like that authority structure, but she understands it better because of Peppery.

## Reconstruction Use

Use this to distinguish Sho's view of authority from a simple "all officers are the same" position.

Peppery matters because Sho reads her as competent, cold, dangerous, attractive, and not obviously malicious. This gives Sho a model of authority she does not like but can respect and learn from.

This also clarifies why Peppery's warning matters: it is not just tactical advice. It is one of the inputs that teaches Sho to choose the moment of action rather than always striking as soon as rage or opportunity appears.
