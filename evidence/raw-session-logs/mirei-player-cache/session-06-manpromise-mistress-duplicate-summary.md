## High-Value Reconstruction Extractions

### Post-Boarding Social Distance / Crimson and Mirei

- Crimson tells Mirei that he does not mind her personally, but she is too close to Sho, who he considers an upstart likely to get herself killed.
- Crimson explicitly says Mirei is not worth the risk.
- Mirei accepts the boundary after identifying Sho's outspokenness as part of the issue.
- This supports the archive's existing social-contamination thread: association with Sho and the PCs makes Mirei dangerous to be near.

### First Plugg Performance / Borrowed Fiddle

- Scourge announces that on the Man's Promise there will be no rum rations, no entertainment, and only night shift above deck after supper.
- Scourge orders Mirei to report to the captain's cabin with her instrument.
- Mirei has not recovered her harp.
- Rak privately offers Mirei the fiddle he received from Grok before boarding.
- Mirei accepts, noting that she can summon an instrument magically but only briefly.
- This confirms the source and reason for Mirei using the borrowed fiddle in the first Plugg performance.

### First Plugg Cabin Control Scene

- Owlbear stops Mirei outside the captain's space and tells her to wait.
- Plugg brings Mirei in and asks what she prepared.
- Mirei explains the missing harp and borrowed fiddle.
- Mirei performs extremely well, with a roll of 32.
- Plugg says someone with her skill should not be down with the crew below and offers a hammock near him.
- Mirei accepts because it would be quieter.
- Plugg immediately shifts cold, says he must check her for weapons, pushes her against the bulkhead, orders her to stand still, searches her slowly, takes her pouch, and allows her to keep the potion.
- Plugg promises that if she keeps him entertained, her life will improve.
- This is the cleanest contiguous record of the first Plugg cabin coercion scene.

### Scourge / Hammock / Rules

- Mirei tells Scourge that Plugg wants a hammock brought to the cabin.
- Scourge responds, "So you agreed."
- Mirei clarifies that the only thing she agreed to was having a hammock up there.
- Plugg orders Mirei not to touch anything without permission.
- Plugg orders her to play for him each night and not speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.
- This establishes the immediate rule structure around Mirei's cabin isolation.

### Night Shift / Sho and Goyle

- Syl calls for night watch and puts Fipps Chumlett in charge as night-shift boatswain.
- Sho volunteers or is placed into night shift.
- Syl assigns Harvus and Ebony to rigging, and Sho and Goyle as swabs.
- Goyle identifies himself to Sho as former quartermaster of the Man's Promise and tells Sho to follow his lead.
- This helps place Goyle in the Man's Promise survivor social field before later Bonewrack capture.

### Course Change Suspicion

- Sandara tells Harvus the ship changed course and is not headed the right way for Port Peril.
- Tilly tells Mirei that some of the crew worry Plugg may be taking the Man's Promise for himself because Sandara noticed the course change.
- Samms tells Harvus that Sandara says the ship is heading east and possibly toward Bloodcove.
- Kroop, Sandara, and Rak discuss the likely destination: Rickety's Squibs, near Bloodcove, where ships can be refitted / disguised.
- Sandara concludes that if Plugg is going there, he has betrayed Harrigan.
- This is a strong course-change / mutiny-intent evidence cluster.

### Tilly / Mirei Conversation About Plugg Trust

- Tilly asks where Mirei was the previous night.
- Mirei says she had to entertain Plugg, borrowed a fiddle because her harp was missing, and agreed to bunk near him because it seemed quieter and because she did not want to anger him after enough time on the mast.
- Tilly reacts sharply to the agreement but then asks whether Plugg trusts Mirei or whether Mirei can get him to trust her.
- Mirei says she thinks she can, if her playing remains good enough.
- Mirei asks Tilly to watch for her missing harp.
- This shows Tilly shifting from alarm to practical use of Mirei's Plugg access.

### Plugg Food-Tasting / Chart / Wench Strike

- Kroop tells Mirei the captain wants her to bring his food.
- Sho notices Mirei's expression drop and asks if things are all right.
- Mirei waves it off, then privately tells Sho they will talk soon and not to worry right then.
- The cabin door is locked; Scourge opens it.
- Plugg quickly folds a chart as Mirei enters.
- Plugg orders Mirei to taste both dishes and says this is now part of her duties.
- Scourge calls Mirei "wench."
- Mirei objects to the term.
- Plugg stops Scourge, says Mirei deserves respect, then strikes Mirei and reframes the title as "Captain's wench," which she has not earned.
- Plugg orders Mirei to return in twenty minutes to sing and not be late.
- This is a key escalation: Mirei becomes food-taster, chart witness, and controlled symbol.

### Entertainer Agreement / Dead Captain's Things

- Mirei returns with the fiddle.
- Plugg says the performance is good but not as good as the day before.
- Plugg says that if Mirei agrees to be his entertainer, she will stay with him, stop working as a swab, and be treated with the respect of an officer on the Man's Promise.
- Mirei says she would not mind still helping around the ship because respect without labor would feel sarcastic and fake, but she does like entertaining.
- Plugg says she will earn respect with voice and presence, not labor, and that he will raise her in station as a lady of a pirate lord if she sings his deeds.
- Mirei agrees.
- Plugg says only he will call her wench; everyone else will need a name for her by morning.
- He says the former captain was a woman and that her clothing and belongings are now Mirei's.
- He threatens that if Mirei disobeys, he will give her to Scourge; until then, she has Plugg's protection.
- This is the core Mistress Mirei coercive bargain scene.

### Oathday Erastus 30 / Mistress Mirei Name

- Plugg asks whether Mirei has made a name.
- Mirei proposes "Mistress Mirei."
- Plugg orders her to live into the persona, interact with the crew, and prove she can be the voice of a pirate lord.
- He tells her to find clothing befitting her new station from the captain's clothing and to wear jewelry and a jeweled sword as a sign of his authority.
- Mirei puts on the new clothing, sword, and items.
- Kroop calls attention to "Mistress" and says Plugg ordered a grand meal for her as well.
- Sho notices the dress and asks where it came from; Mirei says it is a long story and cannot explain right then.

### Mistress Mirei Among Crew / Sandara and Sho

- Plugg orders Mirei to go among the crew, talk to them, gauge where they stand, and report back.
- Sandara pauses at seeing Mirei as "Mistress" and bows with pointed formality.
- Mirei says she is just coming down to be around people.
- Sho asks whether things are all right and is confused by Sandara's use of honorifics.
- Mirei quietly explains that she seems to have become the captain's mistress.
- Sho asks whether Mirei is being forced or whether something else is happening.
- Mirei says not really forced, but something else, and cannot get into it right then.
- Sho tells Mirei to talk to her when needed.
- This confirms Sho's immediate alarm and Mirei's careful, incomplete explanation.

### Sandara / Harvus Information Plan

- Sandara privately asks Harvus to see whether he can sneak into the captain's or officers' quarters on shift and find information about where the ship is going.
- Harvus warns he lacks lock skill, but will try.
- Sandara raises the possibility of using Sho to get information the rough way, but notes that doing so would risk Sho's life.
- This is important for the existing open question about whether Sho was ever involved in Sandara's backup rough-way idea. This session preserves the idea being discussed with Harvus, but not its execution.

### Tam Narwhal Challenges Mistress Mirei

- Mirei approaches Tam Narwhal Tate and Jack Scrimshaw, trying to make conversation in her new station.
- Tam asks permission to be frank.
- He tells Mirei she seems out of place, that this is a pirate ship, and that if she wants to inspire or talk to the crew, she needs more character.
- He tells her to go back to her gilded cage.
- Mirei says she hopes to earn the crew's respect.
- Tam says hope leads to Davy Jones and that if Plugg sees her as his voice, he hopes they all end there sooner rather than later.
- He reveals that he personally put Mirei on the Wormwood, calls her still a green swab or shark bait, and warns her not to make promises she cannot keep.
- Mirei says she is not a snitch and will not run to Plugg about it.
- This is one of the best crew-resistance scenes to the Mistress role.

### Syl Knife Threat

- Syl stops Mirei at the stairs after the Tam conversation.
- Syl presses a knife into Mirei's back and says Mirei does not look or sound like a pirate mistress.
- Syl threatens Mirei's voice, saying she wonders what Mirei would sound like if her vocal folds were cut out.
- Syl says Plugg told her to observe how Mirei handled herself and that Syl was not impressed.
- Syl focuses on Mirei whispering with Sho and says Scourge will have a field day.
- Mirei says she was doing what the captain asked: speaking to crew and checking where people stand.
- Syl draws blood and calls Mirei a liar.
- Mirei pushes back, saying if she were lying, Plugg would not trust her with what he has so far, and tells Syl to back off.
- Syl is surprised by the spark of resistance, contrasts it with Mirei's docility in the cabin, and says she barks with knives rather than words.
- Syl says she and Scourge will see Mirei in the morning.
- This is the cleanest preserved Syl knife-threat sequence and should be treated as a major Plugg-pressure / Mistress-role test.
