# Canon Policy

This document defines how the **Chronicles of Golarion** archive determines, records, and revises canon.

## Final Authority

The GM/product owner is final authority.

When evidence conflicts, the GM may:

- confirm one version,
- merge versions,
- mark a contradiction unresolved,
- or override all surviving evidence.

A GM decision becomes canon even when older evidence disagrees.

## Evidence Hierarchy

1. **GM Truth**
   - Direct GM clarification or ruling.
   - Final arbiter of contradictions.

2. **Obsidian Portal Campaign Material**
   - Character pages, wiki pages, adventure logs, GM notes, and campaign records.
   - Treated as historical in-game evidence unless superseded.

3. **Campaign Chat Logs**
   - Direct play evidence.
   - Especially valuable for dialogue, GM adjudication, choices, timing, relationship shifts, and exact encounter state.

4. **Player Journals**
   - Character-perspective sources.
   - Valuable but subjective.
   - Used to establish how a character perceived events, not necessarily objective truth.

5. **GM Memory**
   - Strong evidence when offered as clarification.
   - Can be challenged by records but becomes truth when confirmed as a decision.

6. **Published Adventure Path Material**
   - Structural reference only.
   - Never overrides table events or campaign-specific continuity.

## Evidence Labels

Use these labels when needed:

- **Canon** — Confirmed as objective campaign truth.
- **Reported** — Stated by a source but not independently verified.
- **Perspective** — True to a character's point of view, not necessarily objective.
- **Unresolved** — Known contradiction or gap requiring later decision.
- **Superseded** — Older record replaced by later GM truth or stronger evidence.
- **Unknown** — Information not yet recovered.

## Contradictions

Do not silently erase contradictions.

Record them in `chronology/unresolved.md` or in the relevant entity file until the GM resolves them.

When resolved, leave a short note explaining the decision rather than deleting the trace entirely.

## Character Perspective vs Objective History

Player journals and in-character chat are preserved as perspective.

Example:

- Rak saying he saved everyone is perspective.
- Multiple sources confirming Rak carried Harvus out of danger supports objective canon.

## Append, Do Not Rewrite History

The archive may revise summaries as understanding improves, but campaign history should not be casually overwritten.

When a character status changes, record the change in history rather than pretending the prior status never existed.

## Published Material

Published Pathfinder material may be used for:

- default ship descriptions,
- location scaffolding,
- NPC baseline intent,
- encounter structure,
- mechanics conversion reference.

It must not be used to contradict actual table play.
