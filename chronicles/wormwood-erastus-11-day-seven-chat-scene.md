## Narrative Chat Scene

Rain reached the Wormwood before dawn and stayed.

It soaked the deck until every board shone black beneath the gray sky. Lines swelled in wet hands. Canvas snapped above the masts. Water ran from the rails in steady sheets and gathered wherever the ship's roll gave it time.

The work did not stop.

Riggers climbed into cold rain and came down with their clothes clinging to them. Swabs pushed water from one part of the deck only to watch more fall behind them. Orders carried badly through the weather, forcing every shout to be repeated until the ship seemed full of anger even when no one had strength left for it.

Harvus spent much of the day among the ropes.

The rain darkened his feathers and dragged at his injured wing. Every climb asked more of it than the last. He worked through the ache while the Wormwood rose and dipped beneath him, the deck far below made slick by water and labor.

By late afternoon, he had recovered three things that had been taken from the press-ganged crew.

His ring came back first. Then his necklace. Last came Rosie's fiddle, still recognizable beneath the handling of the quartermaster's stores.

Harvus kept the ring and necklace close. The fiddle was never his to keep.

As the light began to fail, Mirei climbed toward the officers' quarters with rain running from her hair and clothing. Her performances had become familiar enough that she wanted to invite Captain Harrigan to hear one rather than wait for the music to reach him through the deck.

She climbed the quarterdeck steps and knocked at the captain’s cabin door.

Plugg answered from the other side.

> "This had BETTER be good."

Mirei chuckled and called through the door.

> "Just wanted to invite everyone to my performance tonight."

Plugg did not pass the invitation to Harrigan.

> "Get below before the storm hits, and give a performance that will pacify this land-lubber scum... get to it, girl!"

The invitation returned to her as duty.

Mirei went below.

By evening, wet sailors crowded into the middle hold wherever they could find space among the hammocks, crates, animals, and support pillars. The rain beat against the deck above them. Water found narrow paths through seams and down the timbers. Every opening of a hatch brought a breath of colder air and the sound of the weather gathering strength.

Mirei played.

Plugg wanted the crew pacified. The music moved through them without asking what he wanted from it. Men and women hunched over cups and damp clothing listened while the Wormwood creaked around them. Some spoke more quietly. Some stopped speaking at all.

Rosie Cusswell found Harvus during leisure.

> "Harvus! You want a drink?"

> "Sure."

She offered him a bottle she had won from someone in a game.

> "Some lisping pig-nut tried to beat me in a game... so I beat the knave and took it."

She grinned and handed it over.

> "What is this?"

> "Don't know, hits the spot though. Let's go below deck, huh?"

Rosie moved across the deck toward the hold, already tipsy.

> "...and if any of you sheep-biting gecks try anything, I'll beat your asses."

She moved through several wants without settling on one.

> "...so, you wanna play a drinking game... or maybe we'll go to bed... I'm not sure if I'm horny or drunk. I wish I had a bunch of food for me to eat!"

Beneath the insults and bragging, she admitted what sat under all the noise.

> "It gets a little lonely... being so damn short... and cussing all the time!"

Then she returned to the immediate problem.

> "So you wanna play a game now?"

> "...come on, just play a game now."

Harvus accepted a drink.

> "If it's just a harmless drink, I'll have a round."

Rosie stared at him as though he had missed something obvious.

> "Ha, silly dull-brained bird. I gave you the drink! Take a draught."

Harvus downed it without hesitation.

> "That's the stuff!"

Rosie urged him toward the rest of the flask.

> "Good, isn't it? Don't be a fawning codpiece. Drink the rest of the flask. So why did you come to see good old Cusswell?"

Then he told her he had something from Grok.

> "I have something for you. Got it from Grok."

He brought out the fiddle.

Rosie began to answer before she saw it.

> "Don't tea..."

Rosie stopped.

For one moment, all the restless talk went out of her. Then she threw herself at him.

> "I can't believe this!"

> "Thank you, thank you, thank you!"

Her weight struck his injured wing.

> "Sweet Calistria... THAT'S MY WING!"

She pulled back just long enough to take the instrument, ran her hands over it, and drew out a few notes as if she needed proof that it was real.

Then she pounced on him again.

> "Thank you so mu... erm... sorry about that."

Harvus protested about the wing. Rosie reconsidered the apology.

> "Eh, get over it!"

She punched him in the shoulder with the same rough affection she used for nearly everything else.

The fiddle returned to her hands.

Rosie played while Harvus excused himself for the night.

> "Milady, you are of an entertaining sort. I will see you around. But for now, I must retire."

> "Night, you loggerhead, and thanks!"

She kept playing after he had moved away.

For a while, two instruments sounded through the hold.

Mirei's harp carried above the restless noise of the crew. Rosie's fiddle answered from somewhere lower and rougher, uneven at first, then surer as her fingers remembered it.

Later, Harvus found Sandara.

He still had the necklace he had recovered that day. The peridot caught what little light reached them, green beneath the lantern glow.

Harvus knelt and offered it to her.

> "Milady, I have something for you. A token of gratitude for the aid you have rendered unto me."

His gratitude quickly became something more direct.

> "Being in your mere presence intoxicates me with bliss. If you will vouchsafe my request, I would drink of your lips so deeply."

Sandara laughed softly, but she did not mock him.

> "Sure, I'll hold onto it, Harvus."

She accepted the necklace into her keeping. Then she paused and smiled softly to herself.

> "...and... I'll make you a deal."

Harvus waited.

> "Myessss?"

Sandara gave him an answer.

> "If we ever get off this wreck, I'll personally thank you for your gallantry. For now though, let's get some rest."

Harvus accepted the boundary without argument.

> "The torch, my princess, shall be held."

The rain continued above them.

A ring sat once more on Harvus' hand. Rosie had her fiddle. Sandara held the peridot necklace. Mirei's harp still moved through the hold while Plugg remained above, satisfied that he had sent music below to quiet the crew.

He could order the performance.

He could not decide what the crew heard in it.
