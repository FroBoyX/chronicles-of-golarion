## Narrative Log

By Erastus 29, the course change had stopped being only something Sandara noticed in passing. It had become a whisper moving through the crew.

Samms Toppin woke Harvus and looked nervously at the others still sleeping.

> "Harvus, wake up."

Harvus stirred.

> "Sup?"

Samms did not want to talk where others might hear. She urged him to come along and asked whether Sandara had spoken to him that morning.

Harvus answered that Sandara had mentioned the course had altered.

> "Very briefly, she mentioned our course hath alter'd."

Samms confirmed it.

> "Yes, and have been subtly all night."

Then she corrected herself.

> "All day I mean."

Sandara had been tracking it. The Man's Promise was going east.

Harvus understood the implication: not Port Peril. Back out to sea.

Samms looked around before saying where she thought they might be going.

> "Probably toward Bloodcove. What do you think it means?"

Harvus lowered his voice.

> "Probably that Plugg and Scourge are up to something."

Samms did not like the answer, but it fit the fear already forming around the ship. Plugg and Scourge had claimed they were following the captain's orders and sailing for Port Peril. One day in, that no longer looked true.

Samms did not like their prospects.

She also understood the crew was divided.

> "I know there are a fair bit of people who hate that monk. I trust you and Sandara though... keep your eyes open and lets see if we can get a few more solid allies."

Then she left before she was seen lingering below too long.

Later, after dinner, Kroop called Rak into the galley.

> "Rak, I've got to talk to you."

Kroop started to tell him to close the door, then saw Sandara. His alarm broke into relief.

> "Oh... come in, close the door."

Sandara had come to talk to Kroop too.

Kroop explained what he had been thinking. He had served on the Wormwood a long time. Harrigan was dangerous, but Harrigan was Harrigan. Plugg was different.

> "Plugg.... that pup isn't worth his salt, more of a lap-dog with insecure delusions of grandure."

Kroop did not expect Plugg to keep following orders once Harrigan could no longer see or hear him.

Sandara nodded. She had been calculating the course change all day.

> "We are certainly going east... there is a lot of whispers in the crew."

Then she named the danger in those whispers.

> "Dangerous talk... but we need more information first."

Kroop paused when she said east.

> "Damn."

Rak asked what was to the east.

Kroop asked whether he wanted the good version or the more likely version.

Then he gave the more likely answer.

> "We are most likely headed to Rickety Squibs..."

Rak had never heard of it. Neither had Sandara.

Kroop explained that Rickety's Squibs was west of Bloodcove and even more notorious. Rak had not heard of Bloodcove either, drawing the obvious question from Kroop.

> "What, are you new to the sea?"

Sandara explained Bloodcove more patiently: a notorious port on the edge of the Mwangi Expanse. Not a place to go unless someone was looking for a bloodthirsty crew, or running from something.

Kroop added the missing piece. Rickety's Squibs was an isolated dry dock west of Bloodcove. Old Rickety Hake was known for refitting ships into squibs: changing rigging, shape, and features until the vessel was unrecognizable from when it came in.

That turned suspicion into something close to proof.

Sandara understood it at once.

> "If he is going there... then he really has betrayed Barnabus."

She gave the only safe order available.

> "Keep this between us, don't trust anyone."

She would keep an ear out. She had hoped they were going back to port.

Then she ran out.
