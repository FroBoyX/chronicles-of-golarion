# The Rescue of Sandara

## Document Status

- Type: Event chronicle
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Narrative and thematic account of Sandara Quinn's rescue from Riptide Cove

## Summary

The Rescue of Sandara is the first event in Chronicles of Golarion that can fairly be treated as a legend rather than only a session event.

Harvus G'ron returned to Riptide Cove after a failed first assault, accompanied by Mirei, to rescue Sandara Quinn from the grindylows. In the process, Harvus killed the Whale of Bonewrack with Rak Wraithraiser's spear and forced the Brinebrood Queen to flee.

The practical result was the survival of Sandara, Tilly Brackett, and Goyle Onórach.

The narrative result was larger: Harvus stopped being only a wounded, rage-driven victim of Scourge and became someone capable of saving others through the same fire that could have consumed him.

## Distinction From The Fall of Riptide Cove

This document records the rescue as a character and campaign legend.

`chronicles/the-fall-of-riptide-cove.md` records the collapse of the grindylow lair and the Brinebrood Queen thread.

Both events happened together, but they matter in different ways.

## Participants

### Rescuers

- Harvus G'ron
- Mirei

### Rescued

- Sandara Quinn
- Tilly Brackett
- Goyle Onórach

### Indirectly Present

- Rak Wraithraiser, whose spear Harvus used against the Whale
- Sho Astor, absent and later imprisoned aboard the Man's Promise
- Mr. Plugg, whose actions contributed to Goyle's capture and to the broader crisis

## Preceding Failure

The first entry into Riptide Cove failed.

The party encountered hostile grindylows, dangerous water, darkness, and confusion.

Sho was badly hurt. Harvus pushed ahead and nearly died. Rak helped save the group and carried or helped carry Harvus out after collapse.

That failure is essential to the legend.

Harvus did not go back because he thought the caves were easy. He went back because Sandara was still inside.

## The Choice to Return

Harvus' decision was not strategic optimization.

He saw Sandara as the objective.

The others were not meaningless, but in the moment they were secondary to his focus: Sandara was drowning, captured, or dying in the dark.

Mirei chose to accompany him.

That choice matters because Mirei was already under pressure from Plugg and had reasons to stay safe or detached. Instead, she went into the caves with Harvus.

## The Descent

Harvus and Mirei returned by the sea entrance.

They killed or bypassed outer threats and moved through the lair with more care than the earlier failed assault.

Mirei supported from above and behind while Harvus entered the flooded space.

The cave was not understood in-character as a boss arena.

It was understood as a rescue site.

## The Whale

The Whale charged with its mouth open, ready to devour Harvus.

Harvus braced with Rak's spear.

The critical strike killed the Whale in the opening exchange.

Mechanically, the moment was a lucky critical hit with multiplied damage and high rolls.

Narratively, Harvus met death head-on because Sandara was behind the threat.

The table memory is that Harvus should have died.

## Sandara

Sandara's survival is the emotional center of the scene.

Harvus did not emerge as a conqueror.

He emerged carrying or protecting Sandara.

That distinction matters.

The legend is not only that Harvus slew the Whale. It is that he went back for someone.

## Tilly and Goyle

Tilly and Goyle also survived because of the rescue.

Their survival matters politically.

Goyle in particular carries information that Plugg and Scourge intended the PCs to be eliminated.

Tilly is a living witness to Harvus' choice and to the horror of Riptide Cove.

## Mirei

Mirei's role should not be erased by the scale of Harvus' Whale moment.

She accompanied Harvus into the return mission, supported him, and helped make the rescue possible.

Because she was under Plugg's influence and later confessed the betrayal order, her presence in the rescue is an important counterweight to suspicion.

## Rak's Spear

The spear that killed the Whale came from Rak.

If the spear was lost in the Cauldron pool, then Rak's weapon became part of Harvus' legend without Rak being physically present for the killing blow.

This creates a useful relationship detail: Harvus' legend is not entirely solitary, even when the final exchange was.

## Besmara's Tricorne

Sandara's tricorne led the party toward Riptide Cove when it was seen on a grindylow through the stockade spyglass.

At current reconstruction, Sandara likely still has the hat after the rescue, though this needs player confirmation.

The hat was not the priority during the scene. Survival was.

## Campaign Significance

### For Harvus

This is Harvus' defining Book One transformation.

He remains wounded, angry, and tied to revenge, but this event proves that his fire can preserve life rather than only seek vengeance.

### For Sandara

Sandara is no longer only the person who helped the PCs survive the Wormwood.

She is someone the PCs, or at least Harvus and Mirei, risked death to save.

### For Mirei

Mirei's participation becomes evidence that she had not surrendered herself to Plugg's purpose.

### For Rak

Rak's earlier rescue of Harvus and the use of Rak's spear tie Rak to the event even though he was absent from the final battle.

### For Sho

Sho's absence matters.

She is not part of the rescue legend because she is simultaneously trapped in her own crisis aboard the Man's Promise.

This creates parallel climaxes rather than one unified party scene.

### For the Campaign

Before this event, the PCs are mostly survivors of the Wormwood.

After this event, one of them has a story that can spread.

## Possible Tavern Version

This is not canon narration, but it captures how the story might later be told:

> A wing-broken hawk dove alone into the Queen's black pool. The Whale came at him with its mouth wide enough to swallow a man whole. He set his spear, killed the beast in one blow, and came back carrying Besmara's own priestess from the dark.
