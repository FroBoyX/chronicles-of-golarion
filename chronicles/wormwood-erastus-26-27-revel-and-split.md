## Source Note

The direct chat supports the revel, Sho's reward, the confrontation around Mirei, Harvus watching over Sandara, and the party winding down. The Erastus 27 chat clarifies that the Man's Promise captain died during the battle and that Harrigan's post-battle example victim was a weakened captured sailor, not the captain or a confirmed officer.

## Erastus 26 — The Revel Begins

After the Wormwood captured the Man's Promise, the pirates celebrated with food and drink from the captured ship's hold.

Harrigan met with his top officers and divided plunder in the early evening. As the crew became drunk on gold and drink, officers joined the revel.

Riaris Krine found Sho and delivered a blunt assessment of the strike team.

> "Your team can't take [anything] for [anything]... but you got the job done."

She handed Sho a sack to distribute among the team, but said the reward had been reduced because they had lost a member. She told Sho to be more careful next time, then added that Harrigan wanted to see her.

Sho first distributed Harvus' share. She found Harvus sitting with Sandara, and described the contrast she had seen in him during and after the battle: brutal and out of control in the fight, sweet and caring afterward.

Sho gave Harvus his share of gold and one potion of cure light wounds, keeping the other potion available for group need.

## Sho Before Harrigan

Sho then went to Harrigan's quarters.

Harrigan was with Caulky, and Plugg stood at his right. Harrigan ordered Plugg to leave. Plugg scowled at Sho as he passed, warned her to be careful, and slammed the door behind him.

Harrigan reminded Sho how recently she had been condemned.

> "18 hours ago you were going under my boat.... now.... now, I give you your reward."

He tossed a sack at her feet and told her she had done well. He said he had ordered Plugg to lay off her and that she was under Harrigan's protection now.

He also gave her an amulet as thanks for warning him during the battle.

> "We take care of our own. Keep that in mind."

Harrigan said the sack contained an extra 200 gold pieces and that the amulet should help keep her safe in future encounters. He also said the captured officer had given useful information after she stopped screaming, and for that Harrigan had instructed Grok to give Sho another 200 gold.

Then the reward turned into warning.

Harrigan told Sho not to mistake support for friendship. He expected total obedience from all crew members, and Plugg represented him. If Sho stepped out of line again, Harrigan said he would deal with her personally and show her true suffering.

Sho left with reward, protection, and threat all bound together.

## Scourge Blocks the Captives

Sho tried to approach the locked hatch to observe the captives.

Scourge stopped her with the crack of a whip.

He said the hold was off limits to her kind, captain's favor or not, by Plugg's orders. He admitted he was impressed by her team's work, especially the bodies they left behind.

Sho rejected the logic of slaughter.

> "I had no intentions of killing as many as we did. I wont in the future either. Rest assured though Ill always get the job done."

Scourge gave his sea-rule answer: kill or be killed.

Sho stopped and answered before walking away.

> "It doesnt have to be."

## Mirei in the Revel

Sho then found a dangerous scene around Mirei.

Mirei was down or collapsed, and several drunken pirates were crowding her. Crimson claimed he was checking her pulse, but Sho objected that he had a hand in her robes. Maheem, Jape, Patch, and others were present or nearby. Jack Scrimshaw stumbled away to avoid involvement. Conchobhar said Mirei needed air more than anything.

Sho ordered them away and said she would take Mirei below to rest.

Patch asserted she had it handled and that they did not take orders from Sho. Crimson quietly walked away after rummaging through much of Mirei's clothing.

The confrontation escalated. Rak tried to redirect the moment into party energy, calling out to Patch and warning Jape not to draw steel because it was a party. Patch answered Rak's flirtation with dagger talk. Maheem drew on Sho and missed.

Tam "Narwhal" Tate intervened.

He told Sho to end it and walk away. Sho asked whether Mirei would be safe. Tam did not answer directly; he urgently told her to walk.

Rak then seized the practical moment.

> "MIREI WHAT ARE YOU DOING OUT HERE, THIS IS NO PLACE TO SLEEP"

He grabbed Mirei and started taking her down to the hammocks.

Tam threw Sho to the ground hard enough to hurt her, then stood between her and the others.

> "This is a party, not a fight. At sea, we don't hold grudges... save that for LAND! Especially when we have 11 freash bodies below..."

Patch warned Sho that Scourge would hear about this. Maheem promised that if they were ever on land, he would gut Sho in her sleep.

Sho thanked Tam and left for the hold.

## Sandara Wakes

Below, Sandara woke after roughly thirty hours.

Harvus had stayed with her the whole time because her wound had been grievous.

Sandara asked whether they had lost anyone. Harvus said he had seen nothing of Giffer since the fight and feared she had been lost in the fray.

Sandara told Harvus to get some rest. As the party began to wind down, she told him not to let the lifestyle take away his sweetness.

Harvus went to his hammock and collapsed into sleep.

## The Party Winds Down

The revel lasted thirty-six hours, with no work during the celebration.

A few hours after it finally wound down, the ship's bell sounded and called all hands to deck.

## Erastus 27 — Prisoners and the Split

The captured sailors from the Man's Promise were led out.

Fifteen captured sailors had survived the encounter.

The captain of the Man's Promise had already died during the battle.

Harrigan made an example of a weakened captured sailor by throwing that sailor overboard to the sharks.

A Rahadoumi officer was still present in the scene afterward and spat in disgust. That officer's identity and later fate remain unresolved.

Harrigan allowed the other fourteen captured survivors to live if they swore allegiance.

Then he assigned a skeleton crew under Mr. Plugg to take the Man's Promise, and the two ships separated.

This is the formal start of the Man's Promise prize-crew crisis.
