## Narrative Log

By Erastus 29, the Man's Promise no longer felt like a prize crew returning to Port Peril. The first clear warning came quietly, in passing.

Sandara stopped Harvus as he went by. She looked alarmed.

> "Hey... what happened last night?"

Harvus thought she meant the night crew.

> "Night crew milady."

Sandara corrected him.

> "No... the change of course."

The ship was no longer sailing the direction it should have been sailing.

> "We aren't headed in the right direction for Port Peril... it might be nothing."

But she did not look like she believed that.

> "Keep your eye out."

Harvus had no answer for her, but promised to keep on his toes and gave her a courtly farewell.

Morning work assignments began under Syl's voice. She ordered Rak to bring in turtles and warned him not to fail. She sent Mirei below, mocking her as a wench and telling her not to hurt her pretty little hands.

Mirei rolled her eyes and went on.

Tilly Brackett found her soon after. She was cautious, but direct enough.

> "Some of us are worried that Mr. Plugg might be taking the Man's Promise for himself... Sandara said we changed course last night. Not sure about what that means..."

Then Tilly stopped herself mid-thought.

> "Some of u-"

She paused again and changed the question.

> "Where were you last night?"

Mirei told her the truth, or as much of it as she could say in that moment. Plugg had made her entertain him. Her harp had gone missing, so she had borrowed a fiddle. Plugg had asked if she wanted to bunk up near him.

Mirei said she only agreed because it seemed like it might be a good idea. It would be quieter.

Tilly spat.

> "...and you agreed!"

Mirei pushed back in the only way she safely could.

> "I didn't want to piss him off... I have had enough time on the mast to last a good while."

Tilly thought for a moment, and the anger turned practical.

> "So... does he trust you?"

Mirei did not think he trusted her much yet.

Tilly asked the more dangerous question.

> "...can you get him to trust you?"

Mirei thought she could, if she kept her playing good enough to win him over.

Tilly told her to keep her posted.

Mirei asked for one favor in return: keep an eye out for her harp. Someone had taken it before she left the other ship, and her last hope was that the harp was still somewhere aboard the Man's Promise.

In the galley, Kroop greeted Rak with approval as Rak brought in turtles.

> "There you are! You done good."

Kroop then made Rak an offer. There was a second hammock in the cook's cabin. It had more privacy, and these walls did not listen. He tossed Rak the bottle and told him he wanted to talk that night. Rak could put his things there now; Kroop would finish cooking.

Then Kroop handed Mirei a plate.

> "Captain said for you to bring him his food..."

Mirei's face dropped.

Sho saw it.

> "Is everything alright?"

Mirei waved it away.

> "It's fine."

Kroop was less comforting.

> "Yes. I assume it was worth whatever he offered you. Go."

Sho did not let it pass cleanly.

> "Be sure to tell me when it's not."

As Mirei passed her, she whispered:

> "We will talk soon. Don't worry about me right now."

Sho nodded in understanding.

The cabin door was locked. Mirei knocked. Scourge opened it and told her to come in.

Inside, Plugg quickly folded a chart and gestured toward the table.

> "Good... bring it over!"

Mirei set the food down. Plugg ordered her to taste both dishes. She squinted, unsure of the situation, but took a bite from each.

Plugg approved.

> "Good, this is now apart of your duties... am I clear?"

Scourge smiled.

> "Good food, isn't it wench?"

Mirei asked what else her duties now included and told Scourge she did not like being called wench.

Plugg stopped Scourge with a wave.

> "She is right Scourge.... she deserves respect."

Then he stood and struck Mirei.

> "That would be Captains wench..."

He grabbed her chin.

> "If you earn that right.... which you havent."

Scourge sat back, smiling or scowling. Plugg dismissed her.

> "Leave us, and return to sing in 20 minutes."
>
> "Do not be late."

Mirei nodded and rushed out.

The course change was no longer only a worry. Sandara had noticed it. Tilly was asking about it. Kroop was creating a private space. Plugg was hiding charts and adding duties. Mirei was becoming useful to him, but useful did not mean safe.
