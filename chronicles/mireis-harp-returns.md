## Narrative Log

The Wormwood's first lesson had been simple enough: the ship took what it wanted.

Names, sleep, freedom, gear, dignity. All of it could be stripped away and inventoried before the new hands understood what had happened.

For Mirei, one loss mattered more than the rest.

Her harp was gone.

Then, on the first night aboard, she saw it again.

Sandara Quinn had it.

Mirei did not make a speech of it. She simply asked for it back.

> "Can i has that back?"

Sandara looked at her, produced the harp, and asked whether it was hers.

Mirei confirmed it.

> "Yes it is"

Sandara handed it over without making a game of it.

> "You'll probably want that."

It was an act of kindness, but Sandara did not dress it up as charity. She knew what ship they were on and how fast gratitude could become a liability.

> "Anyway, you should keep your friends close aboard ship. I just thought I could help out."

The warning came with the gift.

Keep your head down. Avoid the attention of Scourge and Plugg. Ask if you need something. Do not start anything you cannot finish.

It was not a speech meant to make the Wormwood less dangerous.

It was a speech meant to keep them alive inside it.

## Harvus and Sandara

Harvus G'ron responded to Sandara in his own fashion.

> "Mi lady, your beauty is boundles. It's as if Calistria herself hath transformed corporeal!"

Sandara chuckled at the bird and took it in stride.

> "You know, that's not the first time I've been told that... happens here in the shakles."

It was not rejection.

It was not invitation.

It was Sandara Quinn demonstrating that she had heard better, worse, and stranger aboard pirate ships and survived all of them.

Later, when she met Harvus properly, she asked for his name.

> "Harvus G'ron milady."

She offered her hand.

He kissed her knuckles.

Sandara noticed the damaged wing.

> "That wing of yours okay?"

Harvus answered plainly enough. Some knave had broken it. Was there a doctor aboard?

Sandara named the closest thing the Wormwood had.

> "The Stitchman."

The carpenter, she explained, and someone Harvus might want to avoid.

There were healing potions aboard, but not the kind that would simply undo what had been done to him.

> "Well, I have a few, nothing that would cure that though... sorry to say, you may be living with that for awhile."

Still, Sandara did not leave the matter there. She asked about the pain. She said she would see what she could do to help the wing set properly. Maybe a sling.

On the Wormwood, that counted for more than sympathy.

It was practical kindness.

## How the Harp Was Won

Harvus wanted his own things back as well, and asked how best to approach the quartermaster.

Sandara gave him what she had: information.

Cut-Throat Grok was superstitious.

That could be used.

> "She is just a bit superstisius... if you can convince her that something you want is cursed she might just give it to you. That's how I got that harp back."

Sandara had told Grok that the harp was cursed by the sea. Only Sandara could cleanse it, and even then the owner would have to play it regularly so the ship would not be sunk.

It was ridiculous.

It had worked.

There were other ways to approach Grok. Alcohol, if it was better than the ship's rum. Music, because Grok was fond of it. Superstition, because aboard a pirate ship superstition could move things that reason could not.

Harvus listened.

The options, he observed, appeared boundless.

## The Ship Listens

The exchange did not happen in private.

Nothing aboard the Wormwood truly did.

After Harvus' courtly manners and compliments had played out, **Giffer Tibbs** gave the sort of comment sailors give when they are listening more than they admit.

> "Well he's cute."

Sandara chuckled.

> "Oh. I suppose."

That was the shape of the first night: kindness under watch, flirtation under pressure, warnings disguised as conversation, and the first fragile sense that not every person aboard the Wormwood belonged to Plugg and Scourge.

Mirei had her harp back.

Harvus had a name, advice, and the possibility of care.

The new hands had learned something important.

On the Wormwood, survival began with knowing who might help.

## What This Establishes

- Sandara returned Mirei's harp on the first night or very early aboard the Wormwood.
- Sandara used Grok's superstition to get the harp back, claiming it was cursed by the sea and had to be played regularly to protect the ship.
- Sandara immediately warned the new hands to keep friends close and avoid Plugg and Scourge.
- Sandara introduced the principle that help aboard ship comes through information, favors, and careful timing.
- Harvus' courtly attraction to Sandara began immediately.
- Sandara recognized Harvus' injured wing and offered practical care, even though she could not simply heal it.
- The Stitchman was identified as the carpenter and someone to avoid.
- Grok's vulnerabilities were established: superstition, alcohol, and music.
- Giffer was close enough to react to Harvus' behavior.
