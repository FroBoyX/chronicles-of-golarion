## Narrative Log

By the second day aboard the Wormwood, Sho Astor had already learned that force could change the shape of a conversation.

That morning, Aretta Bansion, Fipps Chumlett, Jaundiced Jape, and Slippery Syl Lonegan had tried to block the new hands from reaching duty. They had been sent to send a message. Instead, they had ended up unconscious.

The ship noticed.

So did **Cut-Throat Grok**.

When Sho found a chance to speak with the quartermaster, she had little of obvious value to offer. Her belongings were still gone. Coin and trade goods were scarce. What she had was information, proof of capability, and a willingness to make herself useful.

Sho offered that instead.

> "Perhaps, but the information acts as a guarantee that Ill be muscle for you in the future"

She pointed to the morning's violence as proof.

> "I was able to knock out three of the four people who stood in our way this morning"

Grok was not unimpressed, but she was not moved to charity.

> "Muscle huh? Why would I need that from a monk who doesn't know how to mind his toung and seems to want to get punished for fighting... I heard about that fight... four bodies out for a full day... you could be useful if you don't get yourself killed... have anything of VALUE to offer?"

Sho had no object to place on the table. Only herself, her information, and the promise of future service.

> "Just my information and my services. Though you do have my apologies if I have made rude comments towards you, and you can be assured I wont be killed."

Grok's answer drew the line cleanly.

> "You should mind your mouth next time.... sorry, can't trade for something that may or may not have value monk. You'll have to come back when I need that, or you have something else."

The bargain failed.

Sho accepted the lesson.

> "Very well. I suppose it will take time to get back what is mine."

The Wormwood had stolen her things quickly. Getting them back would be slower.

## What This Establishes

- Sho sought out Grok early, likely on Day 2 after the harassment fight.
- Sho tried to use information, combat capability, and future service as trade value.
- Grok had already heard about the morning fight.
- Grok knew four bodies were down for the full day.
- Grok recognized Sho's potential usefulness but did not accept uncertain future value as payment.
- Grok warned Sho to mind her mouth.
- Sho's attempt to recover her belongings became a longer-term process rather than a single successful bargain.

## Character Beats

### Sho Astor

Sho frames herself as useful muscle, but also apologizes for prior rudeness.

This shows early practical resistance: she is not only reacting to punishment but actively looking for leverage and routes back to what was taken from her.

### Cut-Throat Grok

Grok is transactional rather than sentimental.

She is willing to acknowledge usefulness, but she wants concrete value. Rumor, future promises, and bravado are not enough.
