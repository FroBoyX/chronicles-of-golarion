## Narrative Log

The Man's Promise did not become a fresh start simply because it separated from the Wormwood.

Early in the departure, Rak tried to speak with two of the surviving Garundi sailors from the captured ship: Nor'gal Daraa and Merchiel V'giatore. Nor'gal was shaking with anger and warned Rak to leave before he took him out.

> "Get out... before I take you out."

Merchiel held him back and told him it was not worth it.

Rak warned them that they would need friends to survive and that the captain and officers were not kind men. Nor'gal rejected the warning as Rak left.

> "You say that, but he gave us a chance at life... all I saw you give is death."

This set the survivor politics clearly. The Man's Promise survivors might hate Plugg and Scourge later, but they did not automatically trust the people who had just boarded their ship.

That evening, Sho was catching rats when she found Sandara. Sho called the circumstances strange. Sandara answered with Besmara's patience: never judge a situation before getting the lay of the land, or the water.

Sandara still believed the stated course. They were heading back to Port Peril and might get out of this fairly unscathed. Sho could only hope.

Bloody Hour was called, and Scourge made clear that the Man's Promise would be stricter than the Wormwood in some ways and poorer in others.

There would be no rum rations. Nobody except nightshift would be allowed above deck. There would be no entertainment. Everyone was to go to berths after supper, and punishment would be harsh.

Then Scourge singled out the exception.

> "Mirei, you're expected to entertain Captain, report to his cabin with your instrument."

Rak had already found Mirei and let her borrow a fiddle during dinner, expecting she would use it for the captain's performance.

Below decks, Harvus tried to keep a social thread with Crimson Cogward. Crimson first refused him: he did not want to be Harvus' friend. But when Harvus offered war stories, Crimson became civil, listened, and gave one of his own boarding stories in return. He was especially interested in Harvus killing with the flail.

Sho heard the conversations and tried to sleep under the weight of what the battle had already done to the crew around her.

Mirei ate quickly and went above. Owlbear stopped her at the cabin door and asked what she was doing on deck. When she said Scourge had sent her to the captain, Owlbear told her to wait. After a few minutes, Plugg called for her to be brought in.

Mirei explained that her usual instrument had gone missing before they left the other ship, but she had found a fiddle. Plugg accepted and ordered her to play. She performed well enough to impress him.

> "Good, tell me Mirei. With skills like that, I don't think you should be down with the skum below, would you like a hammock here?"

Mirei said it would be quieter and accepted the offer.

Then the offer changed shape.

Plugg's face went cold. He said he had to check her for weapons and asked whether she trusted her captain. Mirei said she was not carrying weapons and submitted if he had to check. Plugg pushed her against the bulkhead, turned her around, and told her to stand still.

The search was slow and coercive. Plugg took a pouch from her, allowed her to keep a potion, and whispered that if she kept him entertained, her life would improve.

> "I promise."

Mirei left to find Scourge. When she told him Plugg wanted a hammock brought to the cabin, Scourge said, "So you agreed," and walked away muttering. Mirei clarified that the only thing she had agreed to was having a hammock up there.

When she returned, Plugg set the actual rules. She could set up where he indicated. She was not to touch anything without permission. She would play for him each night. Beyond that, she was not to talk to him or the bear unless spoken to.

Below decks, Slippery Syl demanded attention and called for five volunteers for night watch. She treated reluctance as consent. Fipps Chumlett was named boatswain for nightshift, and Syl made clear his orders would be obeyed.

Harvus was sent into the rigging with Ebony. Sho and Goyle were sent as swabs.

A scarred Rahadoumi sailor asked if Sho was Sho. She answered and guessed he was Goyle.

Goyle attempted something between a snarl and a smile through the fresh scars of battle.

> "I was the quartermaster on this ship..... follow my lead."

Fipps ordered Sho and Goyle below to clean the hold from the fire. Sho trusted Goyle's direction.

The first night of Plugg's command divided the ship into its new order: survivors angry below, chosen entertainers above, nightshift under hostile supervision, and the first signs that the Man's Promise was not going to be a simple passage back to Port Peril.

## What This Establishes

- Date is first evening / first night aboard the Man's Promise after Plugg's command speech, likely Erastus 27.
- Rak tried to warn Nor'gal and Merchiel that Plugg and officers were not kind men.
- Nor'gal rejected Rak because from his view the new order gave survivors a chance at life while Rak had brought death.
- Merchiel restrained Nor'gal and avoided immediate violence.
- Sho spoke with Sandara while catching rats.
- Sandara still believed the stated Port Peril course might let them get out fairly unscathed.
- Scourge announced no rum rations, no deck access except nightshift, no entertainment, and harsh punishment.
- Mirei was ordered to entertain Plugg with an instrument.
- Rak lent Mirei a fiddle during dinner for the performance.
- Harvus and Crimson established a civil but non-friendly war-story exchange.
- Mirei performed for Plugg with a 32.
- Plugg offered Mirei a hammock in his cabin area, then used a coercive weapons check and seized a pouch.
- Plugg allowed Mirei to keep a potion.
- Plugg ordered Mirei to play for him each night and not speak to him or Owlbear unless spoken to.
- Owlbear acted as cabin guard and gatekeeper.
- Scourge interpreted Mirei's cabin placement as agreement to something more than she believed she had agreed to.
- Syl called for nightwatch and made Fipps nightshift boatswain.
- Harvus and Ebony were assigned to rigging.
- Sho and Goyle were assigned to swab work cleaning the burned hold.
- Goyle directly identified himself to Sho as former quartermaster of the Man's Promise.
