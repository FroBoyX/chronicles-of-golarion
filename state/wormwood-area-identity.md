## Purpose

Use this file to keep the Wormwood consistent as a place over the next month of play.

The ship should feel like a recurring cast member. Each area should have a stable identity, social function, and pressure.

This file may guide narrative embellishment, but it must not override direct table evidence.

## Social Geography Principles

- **Vertical hierarchy matters.** Officers and command occupy raised decks, trapped cabins, and protected doors. New hands move below, then are summoned upward.
- **The main deck is public discipline.** It is where the ship turns punishment into theater.
- **The lower hold is crowded but not safe.** It is where the crew sleeps, watches, steals, bargains, threatens, and learns territory.
- **The middle hold is a pressure valve.** It connects deck, berths, galley, officers, and bilges. Use it for transitions, overheard talk, pigs, Owlbear, and traffic.
- **The galley and quartermaster store are practical power centers.** Rak, Kroop, and Grok scenes should make food, gear, rum, and favors feel like survival infrastructure.
- **Forbidden spaces should be felt before entered.** The captain's cabin, officers' quarters, armory, and Peppery's lab can be seen, guarded, avoided, or whispered about long before they matter directly.

## Recurring Transit Motifs

Use these to make repeated movement through the Wormwood feel familiar without repeating AP room descriptions.

| Motif | Use |

| Night hammocks | The lower hold changes after work. Rum, music, whispers, grudges, and small crafts replace the public discipline of the deck. |

## Day Progression Guidance

### Day 2

Day 2 is the first real geography pass before shipboard monotony sets in.

Recommended use:

- Begin in **A10 Lower Hold**, with cramped hammocks, pillars, lockers, Kipper/Patch territory, and the harassment group blocking the way.
- Move through **A6 Middle Hold**, showing pigs, Owlbear chained forward, and traffic between stairs.
- Report late on **A3 Main Deck**, with Scourge waiting near the mainmast, clock, whipping post, and visible officer height.
- Send **Harvus** into rigging / line work connected to the mainmast and upper spaces.
- Send **Mirei** into deck swabbing, hauling rope, or repairs near Tilly.
- Let **Sho** work enough to justify movement and then reach Grok's store or hatch.
- Keep **Rak** in A8 Galley with Kroop, and later connect him to A9 Grok.
- Keep **Conchobhar** and **Crimson** present as background bodies in their assigned roles.
- End with **A3 Bloody Hour**, then night in **A10 Lower Hold** with Mirei's performance and crew reaction.

### Day 3 and later Wormwood days

After Day 2, do not reintroduce the ship from scratch.

Use work as background pressure unless the day's central event is the work itself. Let the characters move through increasingly familiar routes: lower hold to middle hold, middle hold to deck, deck to stores or galley, and night back below.

The Wormwood should become legible in fragments:

- the same stairs out of the lower hold;
- the same stink and animal noise through the middle hold;
- the same mainmast where time, work, and punishment gather;
- the same locked doors that crew pass but do not touch;
- the same hatch where Grok decides whether need has value;
- the same night-space where music, grudges, and small alliances compete for room.

Do not over-describe the AP layout every day. Use short recurring callbacks and put new detail only where the scene needs it.

## Tone For The Ship

The Wormwood is not only a backdrop. It is a hostile system with routines.

It should feel:

- cramped before it feels large;
- vertical before it feels free;
- practical before it feels adventurous;
- watched even when no named officer is present;
- familiar by repetition, not by comfort.

The ship may later return as an antagonist or memory. Build it as a place the characters learn to survive, not a map they simply traverse.
