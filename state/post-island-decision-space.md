# Post-Island Decision Space

## Document Status

- Type: GM operating state
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Possible outcomes after Bonewrack Isle and the Man's Promise confrontation

## Purpose

This document preserves agency.

It does not decide what happens after Bonewrack Isle. It defines the viable states the campaign can enter after the island and what pressures should react to each outcome.

## Core Principle

The PCs do not automatically become a party, and they do not automatically take the ship.

They were press-ganged together. Shared captivity creates opportunity, not obligation.

The surviving NPCs are not followers by default. They are sailors, pirates, prisoners, opportunists, enemies, and traumatized survivors with their own thresholds.

## The Real Climax

The climax of Book One is not simply the defeat of Mr. Plugg or Master Scourge.

The climax is the answer to three questions:

1. Who has the ship?
2. Who chooses to stay?
3. What do they choose to do next?

## Decision Axes

### 1. Control of the Man's Promise

Possible outcomes:

- PCs control the ship.
- An NPC controls the ship.
- Plugg retains control.
- Control is contested.
- No one effectively controls the ship.
- The ship is abandoned.
- The ship is rendered unusable.

### 2. Leadership Model

Possible models:

- Appointment by force.
- Election or acclamation.
- Pirate custom.
- Temporary emergency command.
- Shared council.
- NPC captain with PC backing.
- PC captain with NPC resistance.
- No captain.

### 3. Crew Cohesion

Possible states:

- Crew broadly unifies.
- Crew accepts command reluctantly.
- Crew divides into factions.
- Some NPCs leave at first opportunity.
- Some NPCs refuse to serve under specific people.
- Former enemies remain aboard under watch.
- Man's Promise survivors demand justice or separation.

### 4. Immediate Destination

Possible destinations or objectives:

- Rickety's Squibs, following Plugg's intended route.
- Port Peril.
- Bloodcove.
- Quent.
- Another Shackles port.
- Remain near Bonewrack temporarily.
- Salvage Bonewrack.
- Abandon piracy entirely.
- Become merchants, smugglers, or water sellers.

### 5. Moral Identity

Possible ship identities:

- Pirate crew.
- Privateer-like crew.
- Merchant venture.
- Revenge crew.
- Survival crew.
- Free company.
- Mutineers in hiding.
- Outlaws with no common cause.

## Major Factions After the Island

### PCs

The PCs may or may not remain unified.

Current pressures:

- Harvus is tied emotionally to Sandara and revenge against Scourge.
- Rak has practical shipboard ties through Kroop and Grok and a reputation for useful competence and violence.
- Sho has moral conflict and a live connection to Kahina and Man's Promise survivor anger.
- Mirei is compromised by Plugg's attempted manipulation but has chosen not to betray the PCs.

### Plugg Loyalists

Likely include hostile or unfriendly Wormwood pirates, depending on current survival and location.

Their loyalty may be fear, opportunism, cruelty, or belief that Plugg is their best chance.

They may surrender if Plugg falls, but that does not mean they become trustworthy.

### Scourge's Circle

Scourge's influence is personal, poisonous, and intimidation-based.

His supporters may be more attached to cruelty than to actual command legitimacy.

### Practical Survivors

Kroop, some neutral sailors, and possibly some Man's Promise survivors may care most about survival, food, water, and avoiding Harrigan's vengeance.

### Besmaran / Pirate-Legitimacy Axis

Sandara can shape how pirate custom and Besmara's code are interpreted.

She should not be treated as automatic moral authority, but her voice matters.

### Man's Promise Survivors

Kahina, Goyle, and others have reasons to resent the Wormwood crew and the PCs.

They may ally against Plugg without forgiving the PCs.

## If the PCs Take the Ship

Do not assume this creates unity.

Immediate questions:

- Who is captain?
- Who is quartermaster?
- Who navigates?
- Who commands discipline?
- What happens to Plugg and Scourge if alive?
- What happens to Plugg's loyalists?
- What happens to former Man's Promise sailors?
- Is the ship renamed?
- Does the crew go to Rickety's, Port Peril, or elsewhere?

Likely pressures:

- Harrigan will eventually learn.
- The ship may need refit, resupply, and identity masking.
- The crew must be paid or promised something.
- Plunder and reputation become active concerns.

## If Plugg Retains the Ship

Plugg may still be weakened.

Possible consequences:

- He lacks enough sailors to operate effectively if too many are killed, flee, or refuse.
- He may abandon people on Bonewrack.
- He may execute or confine key threats.
- He may try to reach Rickety's Squibs with a damaged crew.
- Survivors may plot again later.

This is not campaign failure. It is a darker state.

## If No One Holds the Ship

The crew may fragment.

Possible consequences:

- The Man's Promise becomes uncrewable.
- Factions attempt to take boats.
- Bonewrack becomes a temporary survival site.
- The ship becomes salvage for a future faction.
- Some NPCs vanish into the Shackles later.

This is not campaign failure. It is a radically open state.

## If the Ship Is Abandoned

The Man's Promise becomes a future artifact.

Possible later uses:

- recovered by another Free Captain,
- returned as the Motley or another identity,
- used by Plugg or Scourge if they survive,
- becomes a ghost story tied to Bonewrack,
- salvaged by the PCs later.

## If the PCs Leave the Ship

Possible directions:

- return to port by boat,
- seek another crew,
- become merchants,
- flee the Shackles,
- split into different paths.

This outcome requires careful player buy-in but is valid.

## If the Players Choose Commerce

The Bonewrack water joke is a viable campaign seed.

If pursued, the world should respond:

- Who owns or claims the spring?
- Can the water be transported safely?
- Is it actually valuable?
- Does disease or island reputation taint it?
- Does Besmara's faithful treat this as cleverness, blasphemy, or nonsense?
- Does a rival pirate or merchant try to copy or seize the venture?

## GM Guidance

Do not force the AP route.

Use pressure, not rails.

The world should push back through:

- repairs,
- crew numbers,
- food and water,
- legitimacy,
- Harrigan's future vengeance,
- port politics,
- religious custom,
- Man's Promise survivor anger,
- and the practical need to choose a destination.
