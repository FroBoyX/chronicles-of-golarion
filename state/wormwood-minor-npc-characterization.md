# Wormwood Minor NPC Characterization

## Document Status

- Type: Non-narrative canonical tracking / narrative support
- Maturity: Working canon-candidate
- Scope: Wormwood lower-crew NPCs who need recurring narrative weight before later payoffs
- Source basis: AP role/attitude summaries, `state/wormwood-original-crew-ledger.md`, `state/wormwood-crew-roles-and-watch-patterns.md`, GM guidance
- Spoiler scope: GM-facing narrative support, player-safe when excerpted carefully
- Canon status: Working support file. Use to guide description and background seeding, not to override recovered chat or later table evidence.

## Purpose

These NPCs should be seeded as named, recurring presences before their major scenes. They should not read as generic hostile sailors.

Use short visual callbacks, habits, work roles, and social placement to make them recognizable across the Wormwood month.

## Slippery Syl Lonegan

- AP basis: CE female human, rigger, hostile, unhinged murderer who fled to sea to escape the gallows.
- Current campaign weight: primary cold-open antagonist and recurring violent threat.
- Physical direction: tall, slender, cold, sinisterly beautiful, precise rather than ragged. Her stillness should feel predatory.
- Movement: quiet, controlled, and tactical until violence begins. She places herself where exits and angles matter.
- Habit: near-compulsive need to touch one of her two knives. Fingers brush hilts when thinking, waiting, or restraining herself.
- Social reading: reads fear well, but little else. Poor at warmth, uncertainty, guilt, humor, or appeals to loyalty.
- Blind spot: believes violence, threat, or killing solves nearly every problem.
- Narrative use: make her feel like someone who is not simply angry, but already choosing where blood would go if rules stopped mattering.
- Seeding: watching from rigging, standing too close in lower-hold passages, touching knife hilt during conversations, smiling only when someone flinches.

## Aretta Bansion

- AP basis: NE female human, swab, hostile, bad-tempered ex-harlot with big ears.
- Current campaign weight: background antagonist who will be beaten and later killed by Sho.
- Physical direction: sharp-faced, worn down, ears prominent enough to be cruelly memorable, beauty present only as something life has scraped at.
- Demeanor: bitter, reactive, quick to insult, quicker to feel insulted.
- Tragic angle: she knows what it is to be used, traded, mocked, and discarded. The Wormwood has made her meaner instead of safer.
- Social need: wants status among the hostile lower crew because status is the only armor she trusts.
- Blind spot: mistakes cruelty for control and humiliation for justice.
- Narrative use: let players feel she is dangerous and ugly in behavior, but also shaped by a life where softness was punished.
- Seeding: laughing too loudly at others' pain, watching Sho with personal resentment, reacting badly to embarrassment, covering fear with venom.

## Jaundiced Jape

- AP basis: CE male half-orc, swab, hostile, humorless, greedy, mute.
- Current campaign weight: frequent member of the antagonistic pack.
- Physical direction: broad, yellowed eyes or sickly stare, heavy jaw, scarred hands, expression fixed in a scowl.
- Voice use: do not give him ordinary dialogue. Use silence, grunts, hand signs, stares, spat curses from others speaking for him, or physical responses.
- Demeanor: watches longer than is comfortable. Often seems to be counting what someone owns or what it would take to hurt them.
- Social role: pack muscle and silent pressure. Lets others talk, then makes their threat feel real.
- Greed expression: eyes linger on gear, food, coin, instruments, weapons, and lockers.
- Narrative use: a mute presence who makes scenes heavier without needing lines.
- Seeding: blocking paths, cracking knuckles, staring at lockers, grunting at Syl or Fipps, showing contempt through refusal to move.

## Maheem

- AP basis: LN male human, rigger, hostile, big Rahadoumi with a permanent scowl.
- Current campaign weight: Harvus co-worker and Day 2 rigging reveal source.
- Physical direction: large, weathered, Rahadoumi, heavy brow, hard jaw, permanent scowl that may hide fatigue as much as contempt.
- Demeanor: disciplined, blunt, not playful. Hostile, but not chaotic.
- Moral shape: does not need to enjoy cruelty to participate in it. He obeys ship order and protects himself by staying aligned with power.
- Relationship to Harvus: involved in bringing Harvus aboard, but distances himself from Scourge's more personal cruelty.
- Narrative use: make him visually distinct in rigging scenes and useful as a working rigger who can reveal hard facts without becoming an ally.
- Seeding: correcting line work, hauling without complaint, scowling at waste, speaking only when he thinks the words serve a purpose.

## Tam "Narwhal" Tate

- AP basis: NE male dwarf, rigger, hostile, old friend of Master Scourge, large ugly nose.
- Current campaign weight: present for the abduction, softer than most stooges but still part of the pack.
- Physical direction: squat, older, thick-armed, nose dominant enough to explain the name, face ugly but not empty.
- Demeanor: rough, familiar with cruelty, but less eager than Syl or Aretta.
- Social role: old Scourge friend, pack elder, rigger presence, background witness to bad orders.
- Softness limit: can seem less vicious, but still chooses the pack and helps the machine work.
- Narrative use: makes Scourge's network feel older and more ship-rooted than a handful of random bullies.
- Seeding: near Scourge during leisure, sharing old jokes, working rigging with practiced economy, looking away from cruelty he will not stop.

## Shivikah

- AP basis: N female human, swab, unfriendly, very tall Mwangi ex-slaver.
- Current campaign weight: capable fighter and cold-open character with potential allegiance shift.
- Physical direction: very tall, imposing, controlled, with the bearing of someone used to watching people as assets and threats.
- Demeanor: guarded, practical, slow to trust, not sentimental.
- Past shadow: ex-slaver history should make her uncomfortable and morally complicated, not cartoonish.
- Motive for shifting allegiance: not surrender. She should change because she judges Plugg's side as unstable, wasteful, or doomed, and because the PCs demonstrate competence, restraint, or a better survival bargain.
- Social reading: better than Syl. She understands power, fear, value, and the cost of bad leadership.
- Narrative use: a neutral, dangerous observer who can plausibly move when the math changes.
- Seeding: watching fights without cheering, noting who keeps promises, measuring Rak and Sho as threats, respecting competence more than speeches.

## Jack Scrimshaw

- AP basis: N male human, swab, indifferent, young lad talented at scrimshaw.
- Current campaign weight: likely mutiny-side candidate, but motive needs background support.
- Physical direction: young, narrow, hands always busy, knife used for carving more than threat.
- Signature object: bone, shell, tooth, or scrap being carved into small figures, charms, ships, birds, or faces.
- Demeanor: wistful, observant, a little naive but not stupid.
- Motive for siding with PCs: he is drawn to people who still seem to imagine a future beyond the Wormwood's cruelty.
- Social role: leisure-space background, quiet witness, someone who notices performances and stories.
- Narrative use: seed as a humanizing lower-crew presence who can later choose hope or survival over fear.
- Seeding: carving during performances, trading small carved tokens, listening more than talking, looking embarrassed when caught watching kindness.

## Giffer Tibbs

- AP basis: NG female gnome, swab, indifferent, bedraggled, one-eyed.
- Current campaign weight: tragic later casualty, killed by Rak after a perceived slight and failed persuasion context.
- Physical direction: small, bedraggled, one-eyed, bright in flashes despite hard living.
- Demeanor: curious, socially responsive, vulnerable to misreading by harsher people.
- Tragic angle: she is one of the people aboard who still has a recognizably decent center, but the ship gives decency little protection.
- Social role: notices performances, comments on people, makes small humanizing observations.
- Narrative use: build her enough that her death feels like a cost, not a counter.
- Seeding: reacting to music, making small compliments, watching Harvus or Mirei with interest, shrinking from pack cruelty without openly challenging it.

## Tilly Brackett

- AP basis: N female human, swab, indifferent, tough joker who likes her rum.
- Current campaign weight: rescued later, needs background connection and survivability.
- Physical direction: hard-used sailor, practical hands, eyes that laugh before her mouth does.
- Demeanor: tough, dry, skeptical, amused by danger because the alternative is fear.
- Relationship to sea: chose the sea, not necessarily the Wormwood. Wanted salt on her boots.
- Social role: work companion, rum friend, blunt advisor, someone who can make survival sound like a joke.
- Motive for later trust: respects people who endure work, do not whine, and show up when it matters.
- Narrative use: make her a grounded counterpoint to Mirei. Both wanted the sea, but Tilly chose it with rougher expectations.
- Seeding: joking while exhausted, warning people to keep their heads down, drinking without apology, making the next cruel thing sound ordinary because that is how she gets through it.

## Use In Day 2 And Later Wormwood Scenes

- Day 2 harassment should foreground Syl, Aretta, Jape, and Fipps as a hostile unit, with Syl as the cold tactical edge, Aretta as the reactive spite, Jape as silent pressure, and Fipps as physical bully.
- Day 2 rigging should use Maheem as a distinct working presence, not a generic rigger.
- Tam should appear near Scourge or rigging work as part of the older hostile network, especially in leisure or background work scenes.
- Shivikah should be seeded as a capable watcher, not a loud antagonist.
- Jack, Giffer, Badger, and Tilly should make the lower crew feel morally varied.
- Tilly, Giffer, and Jack can all react to music or leisure in ways that make the night cycle feel socially alive.
- Giffer should receive enough warmth before her death that the loss has narrative weight.
- Aretta should receive enough pain and wounded pride before her death that the players are not handed a simple answer about how to feel.
