# Current Ship State

## Document Status

- Type: Current-state operating document
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Wormwood and Man's Promise at the relaunch point

## Purpose

This document tracks the physical and political state of the two ships most relevant to the relaunch.

It is not a plot outline. It is the state from which play resumes.

## Date

Arodus 5, 4691 AR.

## Man's Promise

### Status

The Man's Promise is grounded or recently grounded on the reef surrounding Bonewrack Isle.

The ship is damaged but not necessarily lost.

Repairs are underway or recently underway.

Fresh water was compromised when the ship struck the reef, forcing the island supply mission.

### Command Claim

Mr. Plugg claims command.

Master Scourge enforces that claim.

Their authority is coercive but not fully legitimate.

### Physical Priorities

- Repair hull damage.
- Operate bilge pumps or prevent further flooding.
- Refill fresh water.
- Gather food.
- Maintain enough sailors to move the ship.
- Prevent open mutiny before Plugg is ready to eliminate enemies.

### Known Key Locations

#### Main Deck

Public command and punishment space.

Likely site of the hot-box and immediate crew visibility.

#### Hot-Box

Sho Astor is still believed locked in the hot-box.

This is both a punishment and an active social front because Kahina Bukekayo has spoken with her there.

#### Captain's Cabin

Claimed by Plugg.

Likely contains Plugg's seized authority symbols, including the hospitality's hammock.

Owlbear's exact physical placement needs confirmation.

#### Galley / Cook's Cabin

Kroop's domain.

Important for food, rum, private talk, and practical morale.

#### Main Hold

Damaged area near water storage.

Important for repair status and ship viability.

### Political Factions

#### Plugg / Scourge Axis

Controls punishment, immediate force, and official assignments.

May include hostile Wormwood pirates.

#### Practical Survival Axis

Includes Kroop and any sailors more concerned with water, food, and repair than ideology.

#### PC / Ally Axis

Not unified yet.

May include PCs, Sandara, Tilly, Goyle, Kroop, and helpful crew depending on return events.

#### Man's Promise Survivor Axis

Includes Kahina and possibly other survivors.

This group may oppose Plugg without accepting the PCs.

### Immediate Vulnerabilities

- Insufficient reliable crew if factions fracture too far.
- Unknown outcome of island supply party.
- Unknown survival of Sandara, Tilly, and Goyle from Plugg's perspective.
- Sho is a prisoner but also a possible bridge to Man's Promise survivors.
- Plugg's plan may collapse if Goyle speaks publicly.

## Wormwood

### Status

The Wormwood is no longer physically present at Bonewrack Isle.

It remains the strategic threat in the background.

### Command

Captain Barnabas Harrigan remains in command.

The Wormwood sailed on after assigning Plugg's prize crew to the Man's Promise.

### Relevant NPCs Remaining Aboard

Likely or confirmed Wormwood-side figures include:

- Barnabas Harrigan
- Cut-Throat Grok
- Peppery Longfarthing
- Riaris Krine
- Habbly Quarne
- Caulky Tarroon

Some placements remain unresolved and should be confirmed against logs.

### Strategic Relevance

The Wormwood matters because Harrigan may eventually learn:

- Plugg betrayed or deviated from orders,
- the Man's Promise did not reach the expected destination,
- the PCs mutinied,
- or the ship was abandoned, lost, or renamed.

Any of these outcomes can create future pursuit, negotiation, punishment, or entanglement.

### Grok

Cut-Throat Grok remained aboard the Wormwood.

She knew Rak was leaving and shared a sentimental goodbye with him.

This makes any future Wormwood return or Harrigan plot more personal for Rak.

## Control of the Man's Promise Is Not Settled

The Man's Promise should not be treated as already won.

Possible outcomes:

- PCs control it.
- Plugg controls it.
- An NPC controls it.
- Factions split it.
- No one can crew it.
- It is abandoned.
- It is destroyed or salvaged later.

## Crew Number Constraint

The Man's Promise requires enough sailors to operate.

If too many people die, desert, refuse service, or split into factions, no side may have enough effective crew to simply sail away.

This is a pressure tool, not a railroad.
