## Purpose

This document tracks who moved from the Wormwood to the Man's Promise, who remained behind, who is dead, whose placement remains unresolved, and whether the Man's Promise can still be crewed after a mutiny or faction split.

Use `state/mans-promise-crew-viability-reference.md` as the compact reference for the corrected split model and crew-viability frame.

## Core Problem

The published adventure assumes that enough hostile or unfriendly crew transfer with Plugg to create a final mutiny battle.

The campaign has added deaths, relationships, rescues, split scenes, player-specific influence notes, and uncertain placements.

Therefore the current crew state must be audited rather than assumed.

A mutiny outcome should not be decided only by combat math. The Man's Promise must remain large enough, willing enough, and coherent enough to operate afterward.

## Ship Crew Requirement Reference

The Man's Promise is treated as a Pathfinder First Edition sailing ship for crew-viability purposes.

Working rules reference:

- PF1 sailing ship vehicle statistics use **Crew 20**.
- In this archive, Crew 20 means the effective operating crew needed to sail the ship properly and sustainably.
- Fewer than 20 may allow emergency movement, drifting, short repositioning, or desperate coastal handling, but the ship should be treated as under-crewed and unsafe.

Campaign use:

- Below 20 effective crew, apply pressure through fatigue, missed watches, failed repairs, slow handling, poor storm response, and morale fracture.
- Count only people alive, aboard, physically capable, and willing or controllable.
- Do not count the other eight captured Man's Promise survivors, because they went with the Wormwood after the Erastus 27 ship split.

## Corrected Erastus 27 Ship-Split Model

The Wormwood and the Man's Promise separated on Erastus 27, 4712 AR.

Correct sequence:

1. The Man's Promise was captured.
2. The post-battle captured survivor lineup contained 15 Man's Promise survivors.
3. Harrigan threw one weakened captured sailor overboard as an example.
4. Fourteen captured Man's Promise survivors remained alive.
5. Harrigan promoted Mr. Plugg to captain of the Man's Promise and assigned him a skeleton crew.
6. The Wormwood and the Man's Promise separated.
7. Six named Man's Promise survivors were assigned to Plugg's skeleton crew.
8. The other eight surviving captured Man's Promise crew went with the Wormwood.
