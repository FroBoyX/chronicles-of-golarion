## Purpose

Use this file to make the Wormwood feel like a living named ship rather than a generic crowd.

The ship has a closed, named roster. When narrative needs a background sailor, watcher, bully, working hand, or overheard voice, prefer a named crew member whose role and location make sense.

This file supports narrative reconstruction. It does not override direct evidence, recovered chat, or later GM corrections.

## Core Principles

- The Wormwood runs on watches, work roles, officer control, punishment, and fatigue.
- Daytime PC activity usually happens during assigned work, after finishing work quickly, or while sneaking away.
- Night activity usually happens after rum ration, during lower-hold leisure, or during watch overlaps.
- Some crew are rarely seen in PC-active scenes because their duties put them asleep, working, or isolated when the PCs are moving socially.
- Officers and specialists have territory. Common crew have habits, sleeping areas, and repeated routes.
- The main deck is public theater. The lower hold is common life. The galley and quartermaster store are practical power centers. The poop deck and officer spaces are command territory.

## Watch and Availability Model

### Hard handling

- Patch Patchsalt is a night-watch character.
- Patch should be available for after-dark deck or movement scenes more than daytime social scenes.
- Kipper and Patch have claimed the far forward section of the lower hold near the foremast and stairs.
- Peppery Longfarthing is an officer and ship specialist, not a normal lower-hold social presence.

### Working handling

Use this until contradicted:

- Patch sleeps or keeps low during much of the PC-active daytime.
- Patch becomes narratively important when the PCs move at night, avoid rum, sneak, explore, or return late.
- Peppery is often unavailable during casual daytime PC scenes because she is sleeping, calculating, working odd hours, or isolated in officer or laboratory spaces.
- Peppery can be seeded as a presence through locked doors, strange smells, officer movement, or brief sightings rather than full conversations.
- Kipper may be less visible during ordinary day scenes if tied to night or specialist duties, but this is less certain than Patch.
- Officers rotate visibility. Do not put every officer on deck in every scene.

## Narrative Seeding Rules

- Seed people before their major scenes.
- Let names appear as overheard fragments or repeated presences.
- Do not introduce every named crew member with a full paragraph.
- Use role and area to justify sightings.
- Let areas recur with stable identity.
- Preserve the sense that work comes first, and exploration requires finishing quickly, sneaking away, or exploiting loosened night structure.
- Rum ration marks a social shift. Night scenes should feel different from work scenes.
- The lower hold should feel crowded, with no true privacy.
- The main deck should feel public and exposed.
- The galley should feel chaotic but increasingly legible to Rak.
- The quartermaster store should feel transactional and guarded.
- The poop deck should feel above the crew, visually and socially.
