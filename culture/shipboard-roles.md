# Shipboard Roles

## Document Status

- Type: Culture / operating guide
- Maturity: Draft
- Scope: Pirate ship roles as campaign functions, not strict mechanics

## Purpose

This document explains shipboard roles as social and operational positions in Chronicles of Golarion.

Roles matter because the end of Book One is not only about who wins a fight. It is about whether the surviving crew can actually operate a ship and agree who has authority.

## Core Principle

A ship role is not only a job title.

It is:

- a responsibility,
- a source of authority,
- a political claim,
- and a practical necessity.

If the Man's Promise is taken, abandoned, or contested, these roles become immediate problems.

## Major Roles

### Captain

The captain chooses where the ship sails, what it hunts, what risks it takes, and how command is enforced.

A pirate captain's authority may come from force, crew consent, reputation, success, emergency necessity, or custom.

At the end of Book One, captaincy should not be assumed. It must be claimed, granted, imposed, or refused.

### First Mate

The first mate translates the captain's will into daily authority.

Plugg held this role under Harrigan and then claimed captaincy over the Man's Promise.

Scourge acts as Plugg's enforcing first mate aboard the Man's Promise.

A future crew must decide whether the first mate is a trusted second, a disciplinarian, a political counterweight, or a future threat.

### Boatswain

The boatswain oversees deck work, rigging, sails, lines, anchors, and routine ship labor.

The role can become coercive when used to punish and control sailors.

Scourge corrupts this role by turning necessary discipline into cruelty.

### Master-at-Arms

The master-at-arms maintains weapons, security, discipline, and sometimes shipboard justice.

On a tyrannical ship, this role becomes a terror office.

On a healthier crew, it must still answer how discipline is enforced without recreating Scourge.

### Quartermaster

The quartermaster controls stores, gear, supplies, rations, and sometimes distribution of plunder.

Cut-Throat Grok shows how powerful this role can be.

A quartermaster can be a gatekeeper, merchant, smuggler, fixer, or crew advocate depending on culture.

### Cook

The cook feeds the crew.

This sounds humble until food, morale, rum, and survival are scarce.

Ambrose Kroop's importance comes from this practical authority and from the privacy of the galley.

### Cook's Mate

The cook's mate assists the cook and may handle fishing, slaughtering, preparation, and errands.

Rak's position as cook's mate put him near Kroop, food, rumor, and the practical heart of the ship.

### Rigger

Riggers work the sails, rigging, upper lines, and dangerous heights.

They are essential to ship movement and often exposed to danger in storms and battle.

Harvus, Rak's companions, Samms, Ratline, Tam, and others interact with this role in different ways.

### Swab

Swabs perform lower-status labor: scrubbing, bilges, rats, hauling, messages, and repairs.

This role is often used to humiliate new or low-ranking sailors.

The Wormwood turns swab labor into a system of exhaustion and control.

### Carpenter / Surgeon

The carpenter keeps the ship alive.

The surgeon keeps the crew alive.

On many ships the same person may fill both practical functions.

This role becomes critical after reef damage, battle, disease, and torture.

### Master Gunner

The master gunner controls shipboard artillery, ammunition, and weapons readiness.

This role is less immediately central after Bonewrack but matters for any future naval campaign.

### Cabin Boy / Cabin Girl

A cabin servant attends the captain or officers.

The role can be a position of proximity, vulnerability, intelligence access, and exploitation.

Mirei's forced proximity to Plugg aboard the Man's Promise should be understood through this lens, but not conflated with Scourge's agency.

## Role Vacancies After Bonewrack

If the Man's Promise changes hands, the crew must answer:

- Who is captain?
- Who is first mate?
- Who handles discipline?
- Who controls stores?
- Who navigates?
- Who cooks?
- Who repairs the hull?
- Who can work rigging?
- Who speaks for Man's Promise survivors?
- Who speaks for former Wormwood crew?

## Design Note

A ship can be won in combat and still lost operationally.

If too many trained sailors die or leave, the Man's Promise may not be crewable.

Therefore role assignment should follow the fiction, not only party preference.
